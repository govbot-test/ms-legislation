SR 93 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Harkins, Kirby, Williams, England, Michel Senate Resolution 93 A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING THE OUTSTANDING CAREER OF MISSISSIPPI STATE UNIVERSITY ALL-SEC BASKETBALL GUARD JOSH HUBBARD FROM MADISON, MISSISSIPPI. WHEREAS, on March 17, 2026, Mississippi State's Josh Hubbard picked up NABC All-Southeast District First-Team honors by the national coaching organizations.  For MSU's outstanding guard, he is the sixth MSU player to secure two All-District First-Team nods from the National Association of Basketball Coaches (NABC).  The Madison native is joined by Erick Dampier, Bailey Howell, Jeff Malone, Lawrence Roberts and Tolu Smith III; and WHEREAS, Hubbard is one of five major conference players (ACC, BIG EAST, Big Ten, Big 12, SEC) to amass at least 700 points and 100 assists this season.  Hubbard has dialed up a SEC-leading seven games of 30+ points, while his 19 outings of 20+ points are tied for 2nd on the SEC leaderboard.  He is ranked among the SEC's top 15 in points (22.09  2nd), three-pointers made (2.97  3rd), three-point percentage (34.7  7th), and assists (3.59  13th); and WHEREAS, Hubbard has already shattered MSU's program mark by knocking down 311 career three-pointers.  The 311 treys are tied for 9th on the SEC's all-time list.  He had a performance for the ages and poured in a career-high 46 points against Auburn (February 18) fueled by a program single-game mark of 10 triples and added 42 points versus Georgia (March 7) during the 2025-2026 regular season finale.  Hubbard and Bailey Howell are the only two MSU players with multiple SEC performances of 40+ points.  Hubbard has paired with Jayden Epps to average 35.8 points per contest which is MSU's highest-scoring duo in over four decades.  The last time MSU had two players combine for over 35-plus points was Jeff Malone and Terry Lewis in 1982-1983; and WHEREAS, Josh Hubbard has had his fair share of recognition for all of his work off the court.  He was named the 2026 Coach Wooden Citizenship Cup winner for his exceptional leadership, his character and his commitment to community service.  The Madison native also was selected to the 2025-2026 All-State NACDA Good Works Winter Team and notched SEC Community Service Team honors for the second-straight season.  Josh Hubbard has amassed 1,947 career points, which ranks 4th on MSU's all-time list, ranks 4th for SEC players after their junior season, and ranks 34th overall in SEC history.  Hubbard is one of two SEC players and one of 12 major conference players with multiple seasons of 640+ points and 100+ assists during the 2000s; and WHEREAS, Hubbard has used his platform and his resources through Name Image Likeness in numerous community efforts.  He founded The Josh Hubbard Showcase, a basketball camp which has hosted around 200 aspiring athletes in his hometown of Madison, Mississippi, and was awarded approximately $50,000.00 in scholarship money over the last two years; and WHEREAS, Hubbard partnered with the Mississippi Make-A-Wish Foundation in 2025-2026, where he launched the "Buckets for Wishes" campaign, where every three-point shot he made resulted in donations from sponsors and donors, directly funding wishes for children in Mississippi.  His campaign has raised over $75,000.00 and has already granted 11 wishes for children, most recently a trip to Disney World for one young boy; and WHEREAS, Hubbard purchased close to 100 gifts and spent an afternoon in December 2025 handing the gifts out at the Children's of Mississippi Hospital in Jackson, the same hospital where he was a patient for 19 days at nine years old in 2014.  Hubbard teamed with his showcase, Aerus and US Foods for helping provide food and donations to the Ballas Family Community Kitchen, which served 200 meals a day to the Greenwood, Mississippi, community after the 2025 ice storm; and WHEREAS, Hubbard has also worked closely with Delbert Hosemann, the Lieutenant Governor of the State of Mississippi, on a public awareness campaign to reduce rates of chronic absenteeism among school children; and WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we commend this incredible milestone by a rising university athlete whose dedication, skill, expertise and commitment to excellence has made a lasting impact on his university and on the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby recognize the outstanding career of Mississippi State University All-SEC basketball guard Josh Hubbard from Madison, Mississippi, and extend to Josh and his family the best wishes of the Mississippi Senate for future success on and off the court. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Josh Hubbard and MSU Basketball Coach Chris Jans, forwarded to MSU Athletic Director Zac Selmon, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
