SR 20 (As Adopted by Senate) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Blackwell, McLendon Senate Resolution 20 (As Adopted by Senate) A RESOLUTION COMMEMORATING THE CENTENNIAL CELEBRATION OF ST. LUKE CHURCH OF OLIVE BRANCH, MISSISSIPPI, AND EXTENDING THE BEST WISHES OF THE MISSISSIPPI SENATE TO THE CONGREGATION AND CLERGY. WHEREAS, we join the leadership and congregation of St. Luke Church of Olive Branch, Mississippi, "The Ark of the Community" on the occasion of their 100-year anniversary, a great day in the life of this Christian fellowship; and WHEREAS, since its founding in 1925, St. Luke Church has been a cornerstone of the Olive Branch community, committed to uplifting lives through faith, outreach, education, and compassion; and WHEREAS, the history of the St. Luke Church dates back to 1925, when it was established by Millissis Shannon and her husband, Charles.  There were approximately 12 members.  Church services were held in homes and in what was known as bush harbors; and WHEREAS, the first pastor of the church was Elder Bonner, who was succeeded by Elder James Lee.  Succeeding Elder Lee was Elder W.E Garner, who served for 33 years; and WHEREAS, in 1964, Elder Walter Shannon became the pastor of the church.  Under his leadership, the church was renamed "St. Luke Church of God in Christ."  Elder Walter Shannon served as pastor until the Lord called him home in 1997.  His son, Elder Earl Allen Shannon, was appointed to the leadership position of the church in 1997.  St. Luke Church has grown from 12 members to a congregation of over 1,000 members.  The late Elder Walter Shannon had a vision; Elder Earl Allen Shannon has brought the vision to pass.  Under Elder Earl Shannon's leadership, the church has provided a wealth of outreach programs and ministries to the beautiful city of Olive Branch and worldwide; and WHEREAS, as they reflect on a century of God's faithfulness and service to the people of this state, the church's leadership has remained steadfast in its mission to inspire hope, foster unity, and advance the common good; and WHEREAS, on behalf of the leadership and congregation of St. Luke Church, the Senate is honored to share the celebration of a historic milestone, a 100-year anniversary of ministry, community service, and spiritual leadership in the great State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commemorate the Centennial Celebration of St. Luke Church of Olive Branch, Mississippi, and extend the best wishes of the Mississippi Senate to the congregation and clergy. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to St. Luke Superintendent Earl Allen Shannon on behalf of the congregation and be made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
