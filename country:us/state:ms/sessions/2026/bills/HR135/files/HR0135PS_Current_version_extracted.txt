HR 135 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Carpenter House Resolution 135 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE TISHOMINGO COUNTY HIGH SCHOOL LADY BRAVES BASKETBALL TEAM AND HEAD COACH JOHN STRICKLAND UPON WINNING THE 2026 MISSISSIPPI HIGH SCHOOL ACTIVITIES ASSOCIATION (MHSAA) CLASS 4A STATE BASKETBALL CHAMPIONSHIP. WHEREAS, the Tishomingo County High School Lady Braves Basketball Team completed an outstanding 2025-2026 season by capturing the Mississippi High School Activities Association (MHSAA) Class 4A State Basketball Championship, finishing the season with an impressive 28-2 overall record; and WHEREAS, the Lady Braves demonstrated dominance throughout the postseason, winning all five playoff contests by an average margin of 26 points, reflecting their discipline, preparation and superior level of play against elite competition; and WHEREAS, the team competed against one of the most challenging schedules in the State of Mississippi, securing victories over three of the top 10 teams and eight of the top 25 teams, further solidifying their status as one of the premier basketball programs in the state; and WHEREAS, the Lady Braves showcased exceptional performance in the championship game, converting 27 of 30 free throws, a display of precision and composure that has been recognized as one of the most outstanding free throw shooting performances in championship history; and WHEREAS, the Lady Braves were led by an exceptional group of student-athletes, including starters Abbi Reed, Chloe Glidewell, Kazli Timbes, Claire Ray and Meg Moore, whose leadership, experience and talent anchored the team's performance throughout the season; and WHEREAS, additional team members Arlei Cummings, Ashlyn Butler, Madi Durham, Ansley Feltman, Addie Barber, Keyorah Clemons, Chloe Crum, Katie Methvin and Madison McCullom contributed to the team's depth and success, while managers Mia McCallus and Bella Houston, along with statistician Matthew Wilson, provided valuable support behind the scenes; and WHEREAS, the success of the Lady Braves was guided by Head Coach John Strickland, who, in his first year leading the program, achieved the remarkable distinction of winning a state championship, and was duly recognized as the Region 1-4A Coach of the Year; and WHEREAS, Coach Strickland was supported by a dedicated coaching staff, including Assistant Coaches Logan Lyles, Cory Glidewell and Tanissa Jeffries, whose leadership and commitment contributed to the team's development and championship success; and WHEREAS, the Lady Braves established a remarkable home-court legacy, with senior leaders Abbi Reed and Chloe Glidewell completing their careers with a perfect 63-0 record at home, while Kazli Timbes, Claire Ray and Meg Moore compiled an equally impressive 46-0 home record during their tenure; and WHEREAS, the team captured its sixth consecutive Region 1-4A Championship, underscoring a sustained period of excellence and dominance within regional competition; and WHEREAS, individual accolades further distinguished this championship team, including Meg Moore being named the 2025 Miss 4A Basketball, Mississippi Gatorade Player of the Year and Most Valuable Player of the championship game, while Kazli Timbes was named the 2026 Miss 4A Basketball and Region 1-4A Defensive Player of the Year; and WHEREAS, Claire Ray was named Region 1-4A Offensive Player of the Year, Chloe Glidewell received the MHSAA 4A Scholar-Athlete Award, and all five starters, Reed, Glidewell, Timbes, Ray and Moore  were selected to the Region 1-4A First Team; and WHEREAS, several players earned additional statewide recognition, including Kazli Timbes' selection to the Mississippi/Alabama All-Star Game and Claire Ray's selection to the Mississippi North/South All-Star Game, further highlighting the exceptional talent within the program; and WHEREAS, the achievements of the Tishomingo County High School Lady Braves Basketball Team reflect not only athletic excellence, but also the dedication, teamwork and perseverance of its players, coaches, families and the broader Tishomingo County community; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend student-athletes whose discipline, commitment and success bring honor and distinction to their schools and communities: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Tishomingo County High School Lady Braves Basketball Team and Head Coach John Strickland upon winning the 2026 MHSAA Class 4A State Basketball Championship, and extend best wishes for continued success in future seasons. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Head Coach John Strickland on behalf of the Lady Braves Basketball Team and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
