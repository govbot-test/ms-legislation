HR 138 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Scott House Resolution 138 A RESOLUTION MOURNING THE LOSS AND COMMEMORATING THE LIFE, LEGACY AND SERVICE OF MR. WILLIE H. JEFFERSON, AND EXPRESSING DEEPEST SYMPATHY TO HIS FAMILY AND FRIENDS UPON HIS PASSING. WHEREAS, "To everything there is a season, and a time to every purpose under the Heaven," and in accordance with that divine order, the Almighty summoned the soul of Mr. Willie H. Jefferson from labor to rest on December 22, 2025, leaving behind a legacy of faith, service, love and quiet strength that now lives on in the hearts of his family, friends, colleagues and community; and WHEREAS, born June 24, 1956, in Jones County, Mississippi, to Mr. Tom Jefferson and the late Mrs. Ella M. Jefferson, a young Willie was shaped by values of integrity, perseverance and compassion that would define a life committed to public service and human dignity; and WHEREAS, Mr. Jefferson devoted more than three decades of distinguished service to Ellisville State School, where he was widely regarded as a principled leader, a tireless advocate and a steadfast steward of care for Mississippi's most vulnerable citizens, earning the respect of peers and supervisors alike for his intelligence, loyalty and unwavering concern for quality of service; and WHEREAS, committed to lifelong learning and professional excellence, Mr. Jefferson completed coursework at Jones County Junior College, earned a bachelor of science in social rehabilitation services from The University of Southern Mississippi, and obtained a master of arts in counseling psychology from William Carey College, later becoming a Certified Mental Health Therapist and Licensed Nursing Home Administrator; and WHEREAS, his professional journey reflected both determination and purpose, beginning in June 1973 as a security officer and progressing through numerous leadership roles, including Facility Maintenance Repair, Residential Program Services Supervisor, Nursing Home Administrator, Coordinator of Health Facilities, Division Director II, and Facility Assistant Director, a position he faithfully held from 1992 until his retirement in 2005; and WHEREAS, his contributions extended well beyond a single institution, as he also provided consultation services and professional expertise to the South Mississippi Regional Center, North Mississippi Regional Center, and Boswell Regional Center, and served with distinction on numerous advisory and advocacy bodies dedicated to the advancement of individuals with developmental disabilities; and WHEREAS, in recognition of his exceptional leadership and service, Mr. Jefferson was the recipient of the MAAMR Paul D. Cotten, Ph.D. Leadership Award, and his voice and counsel strengthened organizations such as the Mississippi Development Disabilities Planning Council, the Jones County Extension Service Advisory Board and the Ellisville State School Human Rights Advocacy Committee; and WHEREAS, a man of deep and abiding faith, Mr. Jefferson was a faithfully devoted member of Mount Olive Missionary Baptist Church, where he served on the Deacon Board, exemplifying Christian humility, servant leadership and steadfast devotion to the work of the church; and WHEREAS, above all titles and accomplishments, Mr. Jefferson was a devoted husband, loving father, proud grandfather and cherished friend, whose life was enriched and sustained by the love of his beloved wife, Barbara Jefferson, their daughters, Cynthia and Monica Jefferson, and grandchildren, as well as a host of relatives and friends who found in him both strength and gentleness; and WHEREAS, even as he has crossed the river of life to eternal reward, the legacy of Mr. Jefferson's compassion, leadership and faith continues to speak, reminding all who knew him that a life lived in service to others leaves an imprint that time cannot erase; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and honor the lives of selfless Mississippians, especially those such as Mr. Willie H. Jefferson, whose life's light illuminated the path of hope for all he encountered along this earthly sojourn: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby mourn the loss and commemorate the life, legacy and service of Mr. Willie H. Jefferson, and extend deepest sympathy and heartfelt condolences to his family and friends who grieve his passing, while honoring a life that strengthened institutions, uplifted communities and reflected the highest ideals of public service and Christian faith. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to the family of Mr. Willie H. Jefferson and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
