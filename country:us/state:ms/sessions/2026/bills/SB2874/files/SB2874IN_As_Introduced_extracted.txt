SB2874 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Finance By: Senator(s) McMahan Senate Bill 2874 AN ACT TO BE KNOWN AS THE "COMMON CENTS AND GOLD AND SILVER COMMEMORATIVE COIN ACT"; TO AUTHORIZE MUNICIPAL GOVERNMENTS, COUNTY GOVERNMENTS, AND STATE AGENCIES TO ROUND THE TOTAL AMOUNT DUE FOR CERTAIN FEES, FINES, CHARGES, AND TAXES TO THE NEAREST 10¢ WHEN PAID IN CASH, IN ORDER TO ADDRESS LIMITED AVAILABILITY OF ONE-CENT COINS; TO PROVIDE UNIFORM ROUNDING RULES; TO REQUIRE THAT ROUNDING BE APPLIED ONLY TO THE FINAL AMOUNT DUE AFTER ALL CALCULATIONS; TO PROVIDE THAT NONCASH PAYMENTS SHALL CONTINUE TO BE SETTLED TO THE CENT; TO REQUIRE RECORDKEEPING AND PUBLIC NOTICE; TO REQUIRE EACH GOVERNMENTAL ENTITY TO ADOPT A POLICY DETERMINING WHETHER IT SHALL ROUND ACCORDING TO THE AUTHORITY GRANTED TO IT UNDER THIS SECTION; TO REQUIRE ANY ENTITY CHOOSING TO ROUND TO APPLY ITS ROUNDING POLICY UNIFORMLY; TO AUTHORIZE RULEMAKING; TO AUTHORIZE THE STATE TREASURER TO ENTER INTO A CONTRACTUAL AGREEMENT WITH A PUBLIC OR PRIVATE MINTING COMPANY FOR THE PURPOSES OF MINTING ONE-OUNCE GOLD STATE COMMEMORATIVE COINS OR ONE-OUNCE SILVER COMMEMORATIVE COINS FOR THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI; TO AMEND SECTIONS 25-3-21, 25-7-65, 27-15-153, 27-17-423, 27-19-81, 27-25-1, 27-31-79, 49-19-115, 65-1-46, 65-33-47, 69-7-263 AND 69-10-5, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO CONFORM; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI: SECTION 1.  This section shall be known as the "Common Cents and Gold and Silver Commemorative Coin Act." SECTION 2. (1)  The Legislature finds that governmental entities and the public may experience reduced availability of one-cent coins in circulation beginning in 2026.  The purpose of this section is to authorize a uniform, transparent method for rounding the final total amount due for cash transactions to the nearest Ten Cents (10¢) while preserving all underlying calculations to the cent. (2)  As used in this section: (a)  "Governmental entity" includes state agencies, counties, municipalities, and other entities such as colleges and universities. (b)  "Cash" means United States currency and coin. (c)  "Final total amount due" means the total amount owed after all calculations. (3)  Noncash payments shall not be rounded.  Cash payments may be rounded to the nearest Ten Cents (10¢) as follows: (a)  An amount ending in an amount between One Cent (1¢) and Four Cents (4¢) shall be rounded down; and (b)  An amount ending in an amount between Five Cents (5¢) and Nine Cents (9¢) shall be rounded up. (4)  A governmental entity implementing rounding shall post a notice stating that cash payments may be rounded.  The entity shall note when a charged amount has been rounded. (5)  Each governmental entity shall adopt a policy determining whether it shall round according to the authority granted to it under this section.  Any governmental entity availing itself of the rounding authority granted in this section shall apply its rounding policy uniformly. (6)  The Department of Revenue, and any governmental entity adopting a rounding policy under this section, is authorized to promulgate rules and regulations to implement the rounding policy. SECTION 3. (1)  The Treasurer of the State of Mississippi is hereby authorized to enter into a contractual agreement with a public or private minting company for the purposes of minting one-ounce gold state commemorative coins or one-ounce silver commemorative coins. (2)  (a)  Each commemorative coin shall be minted in accordance with standards similar to those used for American Gold Eagles and American Silver Eagles. (b)  The design of each coin shall include: (i)  The words "State of Mississippi"; (ii)  The phrase "In God We Trust"; (iii)  The year in which the coin is minted; (iv)  On one side, the Great Seal of the State of Mississippi; and (v)  On the reverse side, a design featuring the magnolia flower. (3)  (a)  No monetary value shall be stated on the coin. (b)  The coin shall not feature the image of any human being or group of human beings. (4)  The State Treasurer is authorized to take pre-orders and accept payment for these commemorative coins.  The coins shall be minted to the finest and highest quality standards. The Treasurer shall not sell the coins at below the cost of the metal plus any associated cost or fees.  Profits from the coins shall be  deposited into the State General Fund.  The Treasurer may keep 20% of the profits for administrative and other costs. (5)  The State Treasurer is authorized to oversee the minting process and to enter into the necessary agreements to produce and distribute the commemorative coins. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI: SECTION 1.  This section shall be known as the "Common Cents and Gold and Silver Commemorative Coin Act." SECTION 2. (1)  The Legislature finds that governmental entities and the public may experience reduced availability of one-cent coins in circulation beginning in 2026.  The purpose of this section is to authorize a uniform, transparent method for rounding the final total amount due for cash transactions to the nearest Ten Cents (10¢) while preserving all underlying calculations to the cent. (2)  As used in this section: (a)  "Governmental entity" includes state agencies, counties, municipalities, and other entities such as colleges and universities. (b)  "Cash" means United States currency and coin. (c)  "Final total amount due" means the total amount owed after all calculations. (3)  Noncash payments shall not be rounded.  Cash payments may be rounded to the nearest Ten Cents (10¢) as follows: (a)  An amount ending in an amount between One Cent (1¢) and Four Cents (4¢) shall be rounded down; and (b)  An amount ending in an amount between Five Cents (5¢) and Nine Cents (9¢) shall be rounded up. (4)  A governmental entity implementing rounding shall post a notice stating that cash payments may be rounded.  The entity shall note when a charged amount has been rounded. (5)  Each governmental entity shall adopt a policy determining whether it shall round according to the authority granted to it under this section.  Any governmental entity availing itself of the rounding authority granted in this section shall apply its rounding policy uniformly. (6)  The Department of Revenue, and any governmental entity adopting a rounding policy under this section, is authorized to promulgate rules and regulations to implement the rounding policy. SECTION 3. (1)  The Treasurer of the State of Mississippi is hereby authorized to enter into a contractual agreement with a public or private minting company for the purposes of minting one-ounce gold state commemorative coins or one-ounce silver commemorative coins. (2)  (a)  Each commemorative coin shall be minted in accordance with standards similar to those used for American Gold Eagles and American Silver Eagles. (b)  The design of each coin shall include: (i)  The words "State of Mississippi"; (ii)  The phrase "In God We Trust"; (iii)  The year in which the coin is minted; (iv)  On one side, the Great Seal of the State of Mississippi; and (v)  On the reverse side, a design featuring the magnolia flower. (3)  (a)  No monetary value shall be stated on the coin. (b)  The coin shall not feature the image of any human being or group of human beings. (4)  The State Treasurer is authorized to take pre-orders and accept payment for these commemorative coins.  The coins shall be minted to the finest and highest quality standards. The Treasurer shall not sell the coins at below the cost of the metal plus any associated cost or fees.  Profits from the coins shall be  deposited into the State General Fund.  The Treasurer may keep 20% of the profits for administrative and other costs. (5)  The State Treasurer is authorized to oversee the minting process and to enter into the necessary agreements to produce and distribute the commemorative coins.
