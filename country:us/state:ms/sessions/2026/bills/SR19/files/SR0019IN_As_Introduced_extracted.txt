SR 19 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Carter, Thompson, DuPree, Seymour, Simmons (12th) Senate Resolution 19 A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE GULFPORT HIGH SCHOOL ADMIRALS FOOTBALL TEAM AND COACH BLAKE PENNOCK FOR WINNING THEIR FIRST-EVER STATE MHSAA 7A TITLE GAME IN A THRILLING WALK-OFF VICTORY AND FOR THE MOST WINS IN A SEASON. WHEREAS, the Gulfport High School Admirals won their first-ever football championship on December 7, 2025, defeating Tupelo 21-20 in the MHSAA 7A title game with a last-second Hail Mary pass and extra point, a historic win for the Admirals; and WHEREAS, the championship was a thrilling walk-off victory, securing the program's first state title and the most wins in a season, 12, for Gulfport.  It was Gulfport's first State Championship appearance in 43 years.  Gulfport also scored 514 points in 2025, breaking another program record; and WHEREAS, with 20 seconds remaining, down to last season's 7A State Champion Tupelo and the score 20-14 in Tupelo's favor, Gulfport recovered the ball and was methodical with how they moved it.  A few quick first-down passes by Parker Nettles and a timeout gave the offense one more play from 33 yards out. Gulfport drew up what was essentially a Hail Mary, or as Coach Blake Pennock calls it, "The Rebound" play.  Somehow, someway Mylan Stubbs was able to come down with it with at least five Tupelo defenders in the area.  After Stubbs' catch, Carter Platt trotted onto the field and easily knocked in the PAT with no time remaining to help make history in Gulfport's road to a championship; and WHEREAS, "When you run a play that these guys frequently go through, they execute it easily.  I have coached a number of good quarterbacks in my career and Parker Nettles is one of the best at finishing up the drive in 20 seconds," Coach Pennock said; and WHEREAS, in what was mostly a tale of two halves, Gulfport (12-2) controlled the ball in the first half with lengthy touchdown runs of 54 and 20 yards by Mississippi State signee Cooper Crosby.  The half-time score was 14-7 with the Admirals having the advantage.  On the opening series of the second half, Tupelo (13-2) drove down the field in 13 plays and ended the drive with an eight-yard touchdown run by Kylan Bobo.  The game was tied 14-14 going into the fourth quarter.  After both teams traded possessions for most of the final period, Tupelo running back and 7A Player of the Year Jaeden Hill was able to break the tie when he squeezed into the end zone from three yards out with 27 seconds remaining.  The Tupelo Golden Wave missed the extra point, which quickly came back to haunt them as Gulfport won it all on the game-winning pass and catch along with the successful field goal; and WHEREAS, Gulfport outpaced Tupelo with 376 total yards of offense compared to 266.  Parker Nettles was 12 of 19 passing for 228 yards with one touchdown.  Cooper Crosby was named the game's MVP after finishing with 167 yards on 26 carries with two touchdowns.  The senior back had a 45-yard reception, as well.  Defensively, linebackers Deandrew Williams and Andre Peters III combined for 21 tackles; and WHEREAS, the 2025-2026 Gulfport High School Varsity Football Team Roster is:  Cannon Parker, Syniker Taylor, Amijai Frederick, Javon Anderson, Javious Hales, Cooper Crosby, Cordarious Payton, Pierre Felder, Jackson Wolverton, Jamarion Davis, Shaw Killebrew, Geziah Davis, Eli Carter, Camden Morgan, Jayonne Pollard, Parker Nettles, Duece McDonald, Jastan Khan, Myles McNair, Deandre Williams, Mylan Stubbs, Andre Peters, Michael Darden, Lawrence Peek, Tyrell Seals, Jaden Jones, Carter Platt, Cortell Crandle, Isaiah Ellison, Tyson Otumfour, Jaylen Lewis, Kaleb McDonald, Lawrence Robertson, Tiajeohn Burks, Baylen Foubare, Tyrese Brown, Noah Fitzgerald, Jayqualan Peters, Amari Davis, Avery Amos, Jerondric Dortch, Dalyn Saucier, Zander Carter, Dailen Perkins, Talvador Harry, Mavin Daniel, Jacob Bealer, Cole Hunter, Tyriq Bush, Micah Bullard, Kayun McDonald, Neyovaughey Tavale, Kydus Reese, Phillip Towner, Grant Hunter, Darius Davis, Dylan Davis, Tavion Ingram, Braxton Boswell, Jacoby Ray, Antoine Ray, Jamarion Moses, Bruce Davis, Jerimiah Lightner, Hunter Smith, Timothy Brown, Christopher Harper, Christian Scruggs, AJ Lovett, Malachi Smith, Jayden Noble, Parker Smith, Truett Freeman, Levell Hall, Khamron Brown, Brantley McKinney, Austin Ray, Trevor Phillips, Cooper Gouin, Jy'Savian Causey, Nick Strickland, Aleaton Ausmer, Q'uron Price, Traylon Rockingham, Kylan Easterling, Jaqurean Smith, Samuel Barnes and Jordyn Fernandez; Head Coach Blake Pennock; and Assistant Coaches James Antonini, Chase Cooley, Richaud Floyd, Zach Green, Justin Jones and Patrick Mooneyham; and WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize these student athletes whose first-ever state championship has brought honor to their school, their community and to the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Gulfport High School Admirals Football Team and Coach Blake Pennock for winning their first-ever state MHSAA 7A title game in a thrilling walk-off victory and the most wins in a season for Gulfport, and extend to the players, coaches, parents and sponsors our best wishes for future championships. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach Blake Pennock and Gulfport Principal Mike Lindsey for the team, forwarded to the Gulfport School Board, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
