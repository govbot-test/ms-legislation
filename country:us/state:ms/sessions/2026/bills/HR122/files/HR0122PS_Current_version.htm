MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative Scott

House Resolution 122

(As Adopted by House)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING DR. KATRINA COX AND THE CAST OF THE LAUREL LITTLE THEATRE'S PRODUCTION OF "THE COLOR PURPLE: THE MUSICAL" FOR THEIR HISTORIC ACHIEVEMENTS, ARTISTIC EXCELLENCE AND CULTURAL IMPACT UPON THE CITY OF LAUREL AND THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI.

     WHEREAS, the Mississippi House of Representatives takes great pride in recognizing artistic achievements that enrich communities, inspire citizens and preserve the cultural fabric of the State of Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, the Laurel Little Theatre, a longstanding cultural institution located in historic Downtown Laurel, Mississippi, continues its proud tradition of delivering high-quality theatrical productions that celebrate the arts and reflect the diverse stories that shape our shared heritage; and

     WHEREAS, the August 2025 production of "The Color Purple: The Musical," under the direction of Dr. Katrina Cox, brought together an extraordinary ensemble of local talent from across Jones County and southeastern Mississippi, showcasing exceptional artistry, passion and dedication to the performing arts; and

     WHEREAS, Dr. Katrina Cox made history as the first African-American director at the Laurel Little Theatre, and through her visionary leadership, also brought to the stage the theatre's first all African-American cast, marking a transformative and groundbreaking moment in the institution's history; and

     WHEREAS, through her direction, Dr. Cox delivered a powerful and emotionally resonant production of "The Color Purple," a story rooted in resilience, sisterhood and triumph, reminding audiences that authentic storytelling has the power to uplift, unify and inspire; and

     WHEREAS, the production not only achieved critical acclaim, but also drew unprecedented attendance, welcoming new audiences into the theatre and expanding the reach and accessibility of the performing arts within the Laurel community; and

     WHEREAS, the profound impact of this production extended beyond the stage, fostering unity, creating a sanctuary for artistic expression, strengthening community bonds and inspiring a new generation of performers and patrons of the arts; and

     WHEREAS, in recognition of her historic achievements and transformative leadership, Dr. Katrina Cox was presented with the Key to the City of Laurel by Mayor Johnny Magee, honoring her groundbreaking role and the doors she has opened within the arts and the broader community; and

     WHEREAS, the cast and production team demonstrated extraordinary commitment, professionalism and camaraderie, forming a unified ensemble whose shared dedication and passion elevated the production into a cultural milestone for the Laurel Little Theatre; and

     WHEREAS, the Laurel Little Theatre's production of "The Color Purple: The Musical" has been recognized as a finalist for Mississippi's prestigious Pinnacle Awards, further affirming its artistic excellence and statewide significance; and

     WHEREAS, community arts organizations such as the Laurel Little Theatre play an essential role in enhancing quality of life, supporting economic vitality and preserving Mississippi's rich artistic heritage for future generations; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to honor individuals and organizations whose contributions to the arts bring distinction to their communities and inspire progress across the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Dr. Katrina Cox and cast of the Laurel Little Theatre's production of "The Color Purple: The Musical" for their historic achievements, artistic excellence and profound cultural impact, and extend sincere appreciation for their contributions to the arts and the enrichment of the citizens of Mississippi.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Dr. Katrina Cox, on behalf of the cast and crew of "The Color Purple: The Musical," and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
