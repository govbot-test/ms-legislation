HR 45 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative James-Jones House Resolution 45 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE DYNAMIC LADIES OF DELTA SIGMA THETA SORORITY, INCORPORATED, ON THE AUSPICIOUS OCCASION OF HOSTING ITS MISSISSIPPI DELTA DAYS AT THE CAPITOL ON FEBRUARY 15-16, 2026. WHEREAS, the distinguished ladies of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, will host its annual Delta Days at the Capitol (DDAC) in Jackson, Mississippi, on February 15-16, 2026, heralding the theme "Solid in Our Sisterhood, Secure in Our Fight", to highlight issues that are in alignment with their dedication to progress, advocacy and unity while asserting their dynamic organization's presence, voice and influence in its mission for change; and WHEREAS, their annual journey to the State Capitol building is a time for the bold women of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, to engage with elected officials and promote awareness for important legislative issues impacting the community, and paired with the goal of advocating and asserting the collective strength of the members of this sisterhood to work strategically within the legislative process; and WHEREAS, motivated by their need to hold true to the esteemed organization's legacy and commitment to advancing its arm of public service, the collective goal of the audacious ladies of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, for their 2026 Delta Days at the Capitol is to focus on Broadband Access and Public Utilities, Education, Election Integrity, Voting Rights and Voter Protection, Environmental Justice, Health Equity and Housing Affordability; and WHEREAS, the organization's focus is strategic because they understand that families facing eviction also face a lack of access to essential services and often suffer from poor health due to a lack of health coverage or affordability of health coverage; and WHEREAS, Delta Days at the Capitol remain a vitally important component of this organization's local and national agenda to educate and stimulate its members regarding public policy and advocacy, while highlighting the more than 112 years of the organization's commitment to social action as it remains politically engaged with policy implementation in the State of Mississippi; and WHEREAS, with public service always at the forefront, the organization has partnered with the Mississippi Center for Justice to provide free expungement for those citizens who seek to restore his or her vote as a testament during Delta Days at the Capitol in keeping with the mission and motto established by its founders and highlighting its nine cardinal values, as best symbolized by the nine jewels incorporated into the Sigma emblem:  Honesty, Purity, Fidelity, Justice, Compassion, Dedication, Fellowship, Temperance and Courtesy; and WHEREAS, because the national and local organizations remain engaged in causes that impact the communities of its members through political engagement and social reform, the regional and local chapters work hard to execute plans of action with precision and finality; and WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, is a national, nonprofit and nonpartisan sisterhood and public service organization represented by over 1,000 chapters throughout the United States, England, Japan, Germany, the Virgin Islands, the Bahamas and the Republic of Korea, including its numerous chapters within the State of Mississippi; and WHEREAS, public service and social change have been synonymous with this organization since its founding in 1913, and sparked a movement since its growth to more than 300,000 members internationally through policy platforms that have served as the catalysts driving Delta Days for the past three decades to uphold the mantle of answering the call to civic, social and political activism; and WHEREAS, the continuity of Delta Days at the Capitol is a testament to the legacy, mission and motto established by its founders because the national and local organizations remain passionately engaged in causes that impact the communities of its members through political engagement and social reform, the regional and local chapters work hard to execute plans of action with precision and finality; and WHEREAS, the zealous and dedicated steering committee for this year's Delta Days at the Capitol includes: Southern Regional Director, Amaris L. Johnson; Southern Regional Representative, Noel G. Seymour; Regional Social Action Coordinator, Waikinya J.S. Clanton; Mississippi Social Action Coordinator, Timaka James-Jones; Mississippi Social Action Co-Coordinator, Kia Jones; Mississippi State Coordinator, Karla L. Edwards; and the Mississippi State Facilitator, Kaitlin Myricks; and WHEREAS, in their steadfast commitment to remain the voice for the voiceless in the many communities served by the organization, both alumnae and collegiate chapters are expected, during the two-day Delta Days at the Capitol event, to engage in workshops and sessions led by experts in various areas of political, social and civic involvement, geared toward raising member awareness and increasing member involvement in the public policy-making process; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to commend dedicated civic organizations, such as Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, that has given and continue to give countless volunteer hours to educate and improve communities throughout this great state, the nation and the world: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend the dynamic ladies of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, upon the auspicious occasion of hosting its Mississippi Delta Days at the Capitol, under the advocacy theme "Solid in Our Sisterhood, Secure in Our Fight" on February 15-16, 2026. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to the Mississippi Chapters of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
