HR 82 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representatives McMillan, Ford (73rd) House Resolution 82 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE MADISON-RIDGELAND ACADEMY PATRIOTS BOYS BASKETBALL TEAM FOR WINNING THE 2026 MISSISSIPPI ASSOCIATION OF INDEPENDENT SCHOOLS (MAIS) CLASS 4A DIVISION I STATE CHAMPIONSHIP. WHEREAS, the Madison-Ridgeland Academy Patriots Boys Basketball Team of Madison, Mississippi, delivered an extraordinary performance during the 2025-2026 basketball season, culminating in the capture of the Mississippi Association of Independent Schools (MAIS) Class 4A Division I State Championship, bringing pride and distinction to Madison-Ridgeland Academy, the Madison community and the State of Mississippi; and WHEREAS, the Patriots concluded their remarkable season with a decisive 65-53 victory over Jackson Academy in the MAIS Overall State Tournament championship game at Mississippi College's A.E. Wood Coliseum in Clinton, Mississippi, before a crowd of thousands of enthusiastic supporters, demonstrating exceptional skill, teamwork and determination on the court; and WHEREAS, Madison-Ridgeland Academy completed the season with a 37-2 record, the best record in the program's history, while riding a 19-game winning streak into the championship and earning recognition as one of the premier high school basketball teams in the State of Mississippi; and WHEREAS, the Patriots were led in the championship contest by 6-foot-10-inch sophomore center EJ Dampier, who delivered a dominant performance with 25 points, 12 rebounds and eight blocked shots, along with contributions from teammates including Evan Carr, Caeden Harvey, Will Bizot, and Jack Dalton, whose collective efforts, leadership and execution propelled the team to victory; and WHEREAS, the Patriots' championship success was achieved under the legendary leadership of Head Coach Richard Duease, the winningest high school basketball coach in Mississippi history, who concluded his extraordinary 51-year coaching career, including 44 years at Madison-Ridgeland Academy, with 1,888 career victories, 42 state championships, and 16 MAIS Overall Tournament titles, cementing his legacy as one of the most accomplished and respected coaches in the history of Mississippi high school athletics; and WHEREAS, the 2026 championship season was particularly meaningful as the Patriots rallied together with determination and unity to send their beloved coach into retirement with a championship, reflecting the deep respect and appreciation the student-athletes held for Coach Duease's lifelong commitment to mentorship, discipline and the development of young people through the game of basketball; and WHEREAS, the achievements of the Madison-Ridgeland Academy Patriots Boys Basketball Team are a testament not only to the dedication and talent of its student-athletes and coaches, but also to the unwavering support of the school administration, faculty, families and the broader Madison community, whose encouragement and pride helped propel the Patriots to championship glory; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend the outstanding achievements of Mississippi student-athletes, especially those such as the MRA Patriots Boys Basketball Team, whose hard work, determination and championship spirit bring honor to their school, their community and the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Madison-Ridgeland Academy Patriots Boys Basketball Team upon winning the 2026 Mississippi Association of Independent Schools (MAIS) Class 4A Division I State Championship and extend sincere best wishes for continued success both on and off the court. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to the administration of Madison-Ridgeland Academy, Head Coach Richard Duease on behalf of the Patriots Boys Basketball Team, and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
