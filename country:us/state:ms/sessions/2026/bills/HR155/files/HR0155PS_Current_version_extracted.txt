HR 155 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Sanders House Resolution 155 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE NORTHSIDE HIGH SCHOOL GATORS BOYS BASKETBALL TEAM FOR WINNING THE MISSISSIPPI HIGH SCHOOL ACTIVITIES ASSOCIATION CLASS 2A STATE CHAMPIONSHIP. WHEREAS, the Northside High School Gators Boys Basketball Team reigned supreme against the Pelahatchie High School Chiefs at the Mississippi High School Activities Association (MHSAA) Class 2A State Championship, their first ever state championship; and WHEREAS, when Northside High School senior and small forward Dai'Qwaunn Price jumped into the arms of senior center Jaquavius Jackson with his arms flexed and fists raised high in the air, the ultimate goal had been accomplished on March 7, 2026, at the Mississippi Coliseum in Jackson; and WHEREAS, the Gators played a fast paced style of basketball, using stingy defense to win their first state championship as they defeated the Pelahatchie High School Chiefs 51-39 in the championship game of the MHSAA 2A State Boys Basketball Playoffs, and Northside won its last 12 in a row to finish the year at an impressive 28-3; and WHEREAS, Northside is only in its eighth year of existence as it formed from the merger of John F. Kennedy Memorial High School in Mound Bayou, Mississippi, and Broad Street High School in Shelby, Mississippi, in 2018, and fans from Shelby, Mound Bayou and the surrounding towns in the North Bolivar School District cheered the Gators on with every play in Jackson, and the state championship in boys basketball was the first in the North Bolivar area since 1989 when Broad Street captured the 2A State Crown, and  the win for Northside was also sweet revenge as the Gators lost to the Chiefs 59-49 in the 2025 semifinals; and WHEREAS, the Gators' effort against Pelahatchie was spearheaded by senior shooting guard Jamarion Johnson who scored a team-high 21 points with six rebounds and two steals, and he was named the Most Valuable Player of the Game; and WHEREAS, the win in the state championship game was huge for Head Coach Demarcus McKinley, and in the previous five years at Northside, he has guided the Gators to the quarterfinals or further each year, and they had been to the semifinals twice and finished the MHSAA 2A State Runner-Up in 2023, and when the game against the Pelahatchie Chiefs came to a close and he realized his team won the title, Coach McKinley was in awe; and WHEREAS, the key to the Gators' success this season has been defense, and it was huge for them in the playoffs, and to advance to the state championship game, Northside dominated Bay Springs 55-29 in the semifinals on March 4, 2026, at the Mississippi Coliseum in Jackson; and WHEREAS, it the policy of the House of Representatives that we recognize this first-ever championship by a group of outstanding student-athletes who have brought honor to Northside High School, their community and to the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Northside High School Gators Boys Basketball Team and Coach Demarcus McKinley for winning their first Class 2A State Championship, and extend our best wishes for continued success in all their future endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach Demarcus McKinley, the Northside High School Gators Boys Basketball Team, and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
