Senate Resolution 54

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION EXTENDING THE COMMENDATION OF THE MISSISSIPPI SENATE TO COLONEL WILLIAM L. HENRY, COMMANDER OF CAMP SHELBY JOINT FORCES TRAINING CENTER, ON THE OCCASION OF HIS OFFICIAL CHANGE OF COMMAND CEREMONY FOLLOWING 34 YEARS OF DEDICATED MILITARY SERVICE.

     WHEREAS, on February 6, 2026, a Change of Command Ceremony was held at the Mississippi Armed Forces Museum at Camp Shelby, Mississippi, where Colonel William L. Henry, Commander of Camp Shelby Joint Forces Training Center, was honored as Outgoing Commander by the Official Military Party and Incoming Commander Ashley C. Sullivan; and

     WHEREAS, Colonel Henry's dedication, expertise and commitment to excellent military service is a matter of record:

     ●  Appointed as the Commander of Camp Shelby Joint Forces Training Center in December 2022, Colonel Henry assumed responsibility for the management of a 138,000 acre joint training site providing training capabilities for over 100,000 warriors from all services and components annually, as well as providing Mobilization Force Generating Installation capabilities to FORSCOM;

     ●  Enlisting in the Army National Guard in 1991 and then commissioned in 1997 through the ROTC program at The University of Southern Mississippi, Colonel Henry has served for over 34 years.  In 2003, as the battalion's Operations and Intelligence Officer, he deployed to western Iraq with the 298th Combat Sustainment Battalion.  In 2009, Colonel Henry again deployed with the 155th Armored Brigade Combat Team to Iraq where he served as the 106th Brigade Support Battalion's Executive Officer.  He has served during numerous state emergencies including Hurricanes Katrina and Gustav.  As Battalion Commander of the 298th CSB, Colonel Henry led the Mississippi Task Force deploying to the U.S. Virgin Islands providing support in the wake of Hurricanes Irma and Maria.  Colonel Henry has served full-time for the Mississippi National Guard for over 25 years in positions that include Battalion Administrative Officer, State Training Officer, Deputy G3, Deputy G4, Surface Maintenance Manager and the Joint Force Headquarters J3 Domestic Operations Officer (Joint Assignment);

     ●  Colonel Henry holds a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice, as well as a Master of Science degree in the Administration of Justice from The University of Southern Mississippi.  As a 2022 graduate of the United States Army War College, he also holds a second Master of Science degree in Strategic Studies.  Colonel Henry is also a 2017 Defense Strategy Graduate and a 2016 graduate of the Harvard John F. Kennedy School of Government's Leadership in Homeland Security Program.  He is recognized as a qualified National Defense Strategist and is a fully joint-qualified officer with Joint Qualification Level Three certification;

     ●  Colonel Henry has received numerous awards and decorations to include multiple awards of the Bronze Star, Meritorious Service Medal, Army Commendation Medal, Army Achievement Medal, National Defense Service Medal, Army Service Medal, Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary, and Global War on Terrorism Service;

     ●  Colonel Henry lives in Petal, Mississippi, with his wife of 25 years, Shelly.  They have three children, Morgan (Trae) Miller, Emily (Blake) Nicholson and Grant Henry.  Lee and Shelly also have four grandchildren, Blair, Reese, Baylor and Brooks; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we join the Mississippi National Guard in honoring Colonel Henry's dedication, expertise and commitment to military excellence which have made a lasting impact on Camp Shelby, our community and to the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby extend the commendation of the Mississippi Senate to Colonel William L. Henry, Commander of Camp Shelby Joint Forces Training Center, on the occasion of his Official Change of Command Ceremony following 34 years of dedicated military service, thanking him for his tenure as Commander at Camp Shelby and for his service to the Mississippi National Guard.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Colonel William L. Henry, transmitted to the Adjutant General of the Mississippi National Guard and Colonel Ashley Sullivan for appropriate display and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
