MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Senator(s) Simmons (13th), Simmons (12th)

Senate Resolution 98

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE NORTH BOLIVAR SCHOOL DISTRICT NORTHSIDE HIGH SCHOOL GATORS BOYS BASKETBALL TEAM AND COACH DEMARCUS MCKINLEY FOR WINNING THEIR FIRST CLASS 2A STATE CHAMPIONSHIP.

     WHEREAS, when Northside High School senior and small forward Dai'Qwaunn Price jumped into the arms of senior center Jaquavius Jackson with his arms flexed and fists raised high in the air, the ultimate goal had been accomplished on March 7, 2026, at the Mississippi Coliseum in Jackson; and

     WHEREAS, the Gators played a fast paced style of basketball using stingy defense to win their first state championship as they defeated the Pelahatchie High School Chiefs 51-39 in the championship game of the Mississippi High School Activities Association 2A State Boys Basketball Playoffs.  Northside won its last 12 in a row to finish the year at an impressive 28-3; and

     WHEREAS, Northside is only in its eighth year of existence as it formed from the merger of John F. Kennedy Memorial High School in Mound Bayou and Broad Street High School in Shelby, Mississippi, in 2018.  Fans from Shelby, Mound Bayou and the surrounding towns in the North Bolivar School District cheered the Gators on with every play at Jackson.  The state championship in boys basketball was the first in the North Bolivar area since 1989 when Broad Street captured the 2A State Crown.  The win for Northside was also sweet revenge as the Gators lost to the Chiefs 59-49 in the 2025 semifinals; and

     WHEREAS, the Gators' effort against Pelahatchie was spearheaded by senior shooting guard Jamarion Johnson who scored a team-high 21 points with six rebounds and two steals.  Johnson was

named the Most Valuable Player of the Game; and

     WHEREAS, the win in the state championship game was huge for Northside Head Boys Basketball Coach Demarcus McKinley.  In the previous five years at Northside, McKinley had guided the Gators to the quarterfinals or further each year.  They had been to the semifinals twice and finished 2A State Runner-Up in 2023.  "It feels great," said McKinley about bringing home the Gold Ball.  "We've been working hard.  I'm proud of the kids, and it's just been a long time coming.  I just appreciate those guys for believing in me."  When the game against the Chiefs came to a close and he realized his team won the title, McKinley was in awe; and

     WHEREAS, the key to the Gators' success this season has been defense, and it was huge for them in the playoffs.  To advance to the state championship game, Northside dominated Bay Springs 55-29 in the semifinals on March 4, 2026, at the Mississippi Coliseum in Jackson; and

     WHEREAS, the Gators will graduate seven seniors with Johnson, Price, Thomas, Davis, Jackson, James Hogan, and Jacarious Baynes; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize this first-ever championship by a group of student athletes who have brought honor to their school, their community and to the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the North Bolivar School District Northside High School Gators Boys Basketball Team and Coach Demarcus McKinley for winning their first Class 2A State Championship, and extend to the team, coaches, parents and sponsors our best wishes for future championships.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach Demarcus McKinley and Northside High School Principal Johnnie Vick for the team, forwarded to the North Bolivar School District and be made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
