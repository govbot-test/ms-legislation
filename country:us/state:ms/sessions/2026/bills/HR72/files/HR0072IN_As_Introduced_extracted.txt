HR 72 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Thompson House Resolution 72 A RESOLUTION CONGRATULATING THE MISSISSIPPI WORKERS' CENTER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS ON THE OCCASION OF ITS 30TH ANNIVERSARY AND COMMENDING THE ORGANIZATION FOR ITS CONTRIBUTIONS TO IMPROVING THE LIVES OF FELLOW MISSISSIPPIANS. WHEREAS, the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights, a nonprofit entity dedicated to improving the quality of life of the working poor and other residents of the State of Mississippi, reaches an extraordinary milestone in December 2026 when the organization proudly celebrates the 30th anniversary of its founding; and WHEREAS, based in Greenville, Mississippi, the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights was created in 1996 by Civil and Human Rights Attorney Jaribu Hill, who serves as the Executive Director of the Center and has been with the organization for over 30 years; and WHEREAS, through public awareness campaigns, legal advocacy and education, the Workers' Center engages with workers to create better workplace conditions and to ensure that all workers are treated with dignity and respect and have equal access to good jobs and humane living conditions, regardless of race, creed, gender or religion; and WHEREAS, as part of a statewide initiative to educate low-wage workers in the Delta and other parts of Mississippi about their rights, the Workers' Center publishes and distributes a manual entitled "Knowledge is Power!", which informs readers on a variety of important topics, including rights protected under the United States Constitution and federal and state laws, along with civics instruction on exercising one's right to vote and participating in a democratic society; and WHEREAS, over the last three decades, the human rights organization has represented and advocated for hundreds of Mississippians, including low-wage Black workers who are the victims of employment discrimination and hostile work environments, as well as parents of school children in the Mississippi Delta fighting for quality and equal education; and WHEREAS, in partnership with client members and other workers' rights organizations, the Workers' Center also endeavors to educate communities by conducting public hearings, town hall meetings and forums, health summits and other meaningful programs designed to improve the standard of living for fellow Mississippians; and WHEREAS, as a result of the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights' work with partners, many employers in the Magnolia State have adopted a zero tolerance policy against all forms of intolerance; and WHEREAS, the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights, fighting for the dignity and safety of all workers, is often the only voice of those suffering - the working poor - who are often robbed of voice and denied their rightful place in society; and WHEREAS, in celebration of its countless successes on behalf of fellow Mississippians, the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights will join human rights defenders and activists from throughout the Southeast and beyond at the biannual Southern Human Rights Organizers Conference (SHROC) in Jackson, Mississippi, in December 2026 to celebrate the 30-year milestones of both the Workers' Center and the SHROC; and WHEREAS, on the occasion of the 30th year of tireless devotion and service by the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights to fellow Mississippians who might otherwise never been heard, it is most appropriate to give special recognition to this outstanding Mississippi organization which, from its humble beginnings to this day, has been a source of support, strength and hope for the least of our brothers and sisters: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby congratulate the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights on the occasion of the 30th anniversary of its founding and commend the organization for its contributions to improving the lives of fellow Mississippians. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That we do hereby extend sincerest wishes to the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights for continued success and growth for countless years to come. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
