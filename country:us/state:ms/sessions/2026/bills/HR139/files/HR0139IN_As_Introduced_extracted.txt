HR 139 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Bell (65th) House Resolution 139 A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING DR. SARA HARPER FOR WINNING THE PRESTIGIOUS 2024 DR. RUTHIE SMITH STEVENSON DOCTORAL AWARD IN RECOGNITION OF HER OUTSTANDING CONTRIBUTIONS AS PRINCIPAL OF MCWILLIE ELEMENTARY SCHOOL. WHEREAS, on October 25, 2025, Mississippi College awarded Dr. Sara Harper the 2024 Dr. Ruthie Smith Stevenson Doctoral Award in recognition of her exceptional contributions as a principal within the Jackson Public School District; and WHEREAS, Dr. Harper received her bachelor's degree in elementary education from Millsaps College and her master's degree in counseling from Jackson State University, and she diligently continued her education at Mississippi College, where she earned a specialist's degree and doctoral degree in educational leadership and supervision; and WHEREAS, launching her impactful career in public education, Dr. Harper began teaching in the Jackson Public School District in 2000, training the minds and igniting the imaginations of her students, and in 2007, she joined the staff at McWillie Elementary School as a teacher for Montessori Elementary I and II; and WHEREAS, Dr. Harper became the principal of McWillie Elementary in 2013, and in her time at the helm of its administration over the past 13 years, the school has consistently maintained an "A" rating, ranking in the top five percent of schools across the State of Mississippi and as one of the top three schools in the district; and WHEREAS, with over 400 students enrolled and 300 students in an early childhood program, McWillie Elementary is the only public Montessori school in Mississippi, and the school's faculty, staff, students, parents and community members have greatly benefited from Dr. Harper's leadership and guidance, which has empowered them to take ownership of the process of educating the citizens of Mississippi; and WHEREAS, Dr. Harper is guided by her belief that educators must prepare safe, nurturing learning environments for the appropriate stages of child development, fostering each student's natural desire to learn while cultivating his or her unique gifts and abilities, and her faithful pursuit of this mission has paid off in spades, as the school has witnessed the amazing progress of its students who have stepped into the next chapter of their lives as self-assured, successful individuals and problem-solvers; and WHEREAS, distinguishing herself as a mentor to her peers, Dr. Harper currently serves on the Mississippi Department of Education Principal Advisory Council and as a principal coach for the Jackson Public School District, providing support and guidance to other principals and helping them make decisions that positively impact all children; and WHEREAS, Dr. Harper further developed her skills as a natural leader at the Mississippi Leading Innovative Teaching and Learning Environments in Early Childhood Institute, where she graduated as a cohort member in 2024, and she is a graduate and current board member of Leadership Greater Jackson, one of the most successful training programs for young leaders with the desire and potential to work together to better the Jackson community; and WHEREAS, outside of the walls of McWillie Elementary, Dr. Harper is also dedicated to serving children in the community at large as a member of Junior League of Jackson, which provides services and assistance to children in the Metro Jackson area through academic growth projects, essential needs projects, health and mental well-being programs, and signature projects with the Children's Cancer Center at Children's of Mississippi and the Mississippi Children's Museum; and WHEREAS, Dr. Harper also extends her exemplary efforts beyond her work in elementary education by lending her expertise to local universities and colleges, both as a mentor for candidates in the Master's of Arts in Teaching Program and the Education Doctoral Program at Belhaven University and as an active advisory board member and facilitator of the Principals' Institute at Mississippi College; and WHEREAS, a testament to her abundance of hard work and unwavering dedication to providing an excellent educational environment, Dr. Harper was selected as the Mississippi Congressional District 2 Administrator of the Year, an accolade that places her among the top four administrators statewide; and WHEREAS, Dr. Harper has been recognized with many other honors and awards throughout her illustrious career, including Jackson Public School District Administrator of the Year, McWillie Elementary School Teacher of the Year, Mississippi College Top Honor's List (4.0 GPA), Jackson State University Dean's List (4.0 GPA), Lee Elementary School Marva Collins Award and Lee Elementary School Teacher of the Year; and WHEREAS, using her God-given talent as an instructor, Dr. Harper teaches Sunday School to 3 and 4-year-olds at Word of Life Church, where she is an active member, and she resides in Madison with her husband, Ontario, and her daughter, Holland, who is a PreK-4 Montessori student at McWillie Elementary; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend extraordinary Mississippi educators and administrators, especially those such as Dr. Sara Harper, whose passion for education, commitment to her students, and superb leadership brings great pride to her community, the City of Jackson, and the entire State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Dr. Sara Harper for winning the 2024 Dr. Ruthie Smith Stevenson Doctoral Award in recognition of her vast contributions as Principal of McWillie Elementary School and wish her continued success in all her future endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Dr. Sara Harper and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
