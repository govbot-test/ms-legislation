SC 546 (As Adopted by Senate and House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Simmons (12th) Senate Concurrent Resolution 546 (As Adopted by Senate and House) A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION COMMENDING THE CITY OF GREENVILLE, MISSISSIPPI, AND MAYOR ERRICK D. SIMMONS FOR SIGNIFICANT, DATA-DRIVEN REDUCTIONS IN VIOLENT CRIME; RECOGNIZING THE CITY AS A NATIONAL MODEL FOR INNOVATIVE, COORDINATED PUBLIC SAFETY STRATEGIES; AND ACKNOWLEDGING NATIONAL RECOGNITION FOR ITS RESULTS. WHEREAS, the City of Greenville, Mississippi, under the leadership of Mayor Errick D. Simmons, has achieved a measurable and sustained reduction in violent crime, including an approximate 79% reduction in violent crime, a 90% reduction in homicides, and a 76% reduction in aggravated assaults; and WHEREAS, these results were achieved during a period of rising violence through decisive, proactive leadership and the implementation of a temporary, targeted citywide curfew strategy, coupled with long-term prevention and enforcement measures; and WHEREAS, Greenville's efforts and outcomes have received national recognition, including coverage by The New York Times, highlighting the city as a leading example of how small and mid-sized cities can successfully reduce violent crime through bold, coordinated action; and WHEREAS, Mayor Simmons demonstrated leadership by declaring that "enough is enough," prioritizing the safety of residents and restoring public confidence in neighborhoods where violence had disrupted daily life; and WHEREAS, Greenville's success is rooted in a comprehensive, data-driven public safety strategy, including:   Targeted Interventions: Focusing law enforcement and resources on high-risk individuals and geographic "hot spots" responsible for a disproportionate share of violent crime;   Coordinated Law Enforcement Efforts:  Strengthening collaboration among city, county, state, and federal agencies, including multi-agency operations and intelligence-sharing;     Community-Police Partnerships:  Building trust through engagement with residents, faith leaders, and community organizations, resulting in improved cooperation and higher clearance rates;   Youth Engagement & Prevention:  Expanding mentorship, after-school programming, conflict resolution initiatives, and school-based supports to address root causes of violence;   Trauma-Informed & Behavioral Health Support:  Partnering with regional providers to address the emotional and psychological impacts of violence on youth, families, and first responders;   Violence Interruption Strategies:  Utilizing credible messengers, faith-based outreach, and community-led interventions to prevent retaliation and break cycles of violence;   Environmental Improvements (CPTED):  Enhancing lighting, addressing blight, and improving public spaces to deter criminal activity; and WHEREAS, the City of Greenville has embraced cutting-edge public safety technology, including the deployment of a comprehensive Flock Safety systemfeaturing automated license plate recognition, gunshot detection, and Drone as First Responder technologymaking Greenville one of the first cities in Mississippi to implement a fully integrated, technology-driven public safety network; and WHEREAS, Greenville has also taken a leadership role beyond its borders by convening a regional Delta-wide crime prevention summit, bringing together mayors, law enforcement, educators, faith leaders, and behavioral health providers to develop a unified strategy addressing violence across the Mississippi Delta; and WHEREAS, this regional approach emphasizes collaboration, shared accountability, and coordinated action to address violence as a collective challenge impacting multiple communities; and WHEREAS, the leadership of Mayor Errick D. Simmons, in partnership with Chief Marcus Turner, city officials, and community stakeholders, reflects a commitment to innovation, accountability, compassion, and results-driven governance; and WHEREAS, the City of Greenville has demonstrated that meaningful reductions in crime are possible when cities align enforcement, prevention, technology, and community engagement under a unified strategy focused on saving lives: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING THEREIN, THAT: 1.  The City of Greenville, Mississippi, is hereby commended for its measurable and sustained reduction in violent crime and recognized as a National Model for Innovative and Effective Public Safety Strategies; 2.  Mayor Errick D. Simmons is hereby recognized for bold, decisive leadership in implementing comprehensive, data-driven, and community-centered approaches to reducing crime and restoring public safety; 3.  The collaborative efforts of law enforcement, community leaders, educators, faith-based organizations, and regional partners are hereby acknowledged and commended for their role in achieving these outcomes; 4.  The City of Greenville's use of advanced public safety technology, regional coordination, and prevention-focused strategies is hereby recognized as a best-practice model for other municipalities; and 5.  The commitment of Greenville's residents and stakeholders to building a safer, stronger community is hereby honored and applauded. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Mayor Errick D. Simmons and the City Council of the City of Greenville and made available to the Mississippi Department of Public Safety and the Capitol Press Corps.
