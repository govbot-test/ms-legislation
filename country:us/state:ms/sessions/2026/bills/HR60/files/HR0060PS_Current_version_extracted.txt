HR 60 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Thompson House Resolution 60 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING AND HONORING ATTORNEY JARIBU HILL UPON THE OUTSTANDING OCCASION OF HER 30TH YEAR ANNIVERSARY WITH THE MISSISSIPPI WORKERS' CENTER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS. WHEREAS, as a Civil and Human Rights attorney, Jaribu Hill, Founder and Executive Director of the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights, is celebrating her 30th anniversary with this outstanding organization; and WHEREAS, through her organization, Attorney Hill has provided legal representation and advocacy for hundreds of workers in the State of Mississippi, and her efforts have led to the adoption of "Zero Tolerance Against Hate" policies being implemented in workplaces across the state, and she also won an important judgment against the Ku Klux Klan in Pelion, South Carolina, and has assisted Mississippi Delta parents in their fight for school equity; and WHEREAS, as an author and an international spokesperson on Civil and Human Rights topics, and in support of the human rights struggles of workers across the globe, Attorney Hill has traveled to Asia, Africa, Europe and the Caribbean; and WHEREAS, Attorney Hill has been the recipient of numerous honors and awards, including the coveted Gloria Award, named for Gloria Steinem, and she is a Skadden Fellow and the recipient of the R. Jess Brown, the highest award given to a Mississippi lawyer by the Magnolia Bar Association; and WHEREAS, additionally, Attorney Hill was a legal observer and cultural artist during the Women in War Crimes Tribunal, held in Tokyo, Japan, in December 2000, and the author of the poem, "Haunting Mirrors," written to honor Comfort Women and other victims of sexual slavery, and excerpts from this poem were made part of the important judgment rendered during the Tribunal; and WHEREAS, Attorney Hill is admitted to the U.S. Supreme Court, serves as a Special Master in Washington County's Chancery Court and serves on Mississippi's Access to Justice Commission, and she is also a former Municipal Judge for the City of Hollandale, Mississippi; and WHEREAS, Attorney Hill has written numerous articles and is published in law reviews and journals, including Southern University Law Review, Columbia University Black Law Journal, and Columbia University Law Review, and she is the author of Knowledge is PowerA Know Your Rights Manual and co-author of The Black College Guide, and has appeared on various television and radio programs, including TV One's acclaimed series:  Murder in Black and White, and she hosts "Talking Rights With Jaribu", a weekly radio program on WDSV 91.9 FM; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to commend extraordinary Mississippians, especially those such as Attorney Hill, whose dedication to civil and human rights brings honor to the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby recognize and honor Attorney Jaribu Hill upon her 30th year anniversary with the Mississippi Workers' Center for Human Rights, and extend best wishes for continued success in all her future endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Attorney Jaribu Hill and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
