Section 1:
HR 79 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Wallace House Resolution 79 A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE SIMPSON ACADEMY LADY COUGARS BASKETBALL TEAM AND COACH LINDA DEAR ON WINNING THE 2026 MIDSOUTH ASSOCIATION OF INDEPENDENT SCHOOLS (MAIS) CLASS 4A DIVISION I STATE CHAMPIONSHIP. WHEREAS, on February 14, 2026, the Simpson Academy Girls Varsity Basketball Team tasted the ultimate sweetness of victory when the Lady Cougars defeated the East Rankin Academy Lady Patriots in the 2026 Midsouth Association of Independent Schools (MAIS) Class 4A Division I State Championship, incredibly bringing home to Mendenhall the team's first championship trophy since 2019; and WHEREAS, while both teams showcased exemplary skill and determination during the championship battle on the hardwood at Jackson Prep, the Lady Cougars took the lead from the very start of the game and never surrendered it, as the team's mental stamina and unimaginable amount of fortitude never waivered for a moment as they outlasted and overpowered the Lady Patriots until the last buzzer sounded with the 54-45 final score on the board; and WHEREAS, the championship win, the Lady Cougars' fourth state title in Simpson Academy's girls basketball program history, paved the way for the team's trip to the MAIS Girls Basketball Overall Championship held on the campus of Mississippi College, where the 31-6 Lady Cougars played phenomenally well and reached the overall finals only to come up short against Madison-Ridgeland Academy; and WHEREAS, the Simpson Academy Lady Cougars' success is attributable to the remarkable commitment and hard work of its talented student-athletes, including:  Brooke Blackledge, Danni Cooper, Maggie Shivers, Jayda Smith, Elie Stewart, Anniston Bass, Charlye Bowen, Emma Henderson, Ansley Jones, Madi Davidson, Elle McNulty, MC Hunt, Lucy Walker, Gracie Pinkerton and Annmarie Blakeney; and WHEREAS, the Lady Cougars have been superbly coached and inspired by Head Coach Linda Dear, who has been at Simpson Academy for 23 years, and Assistant Coaches Chaucer Funchess and Josh McNulty, all of whom who have broadened the players' knowledge of the game and refined their skills in execution, and they continue to build upon the legacy of superior play and true sportsmanship forged by the tenacious and undaunted spirit of each team member; and WHEREAS, the members of this team will forever carry the unforgettable memories they made along the way to their hard-earned championship status, whether it be the long hours of grueling practices or Coach Dear's famous red rag, a longtime rallying towel that always fires up the Lady Cougars and their fans, as well as the lessons of hard work, dedication and perseverance learned during this amazing season; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend excellence such as that displayed by the Simpson Academy Lady Cougars Basketball Team, and it is with great pride that we congratulate this elite group of student-athletes and Coach Linda Dear, who have brought honor to Simpson Academy, their families, the Mendenhall community, and the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Simpson Academy Lady Cougars Basketball Team and Coach Linda Dear on winning the 2026 Midsouth Association of Independent Schools (MAIS) Class 4A Division I State Championship and extend best wishes to the team for continued success in all their future academic and athletic endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Head Coach Linda Dear on behalf of the Simpson Academy Lady Cougars Basketball Team and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
