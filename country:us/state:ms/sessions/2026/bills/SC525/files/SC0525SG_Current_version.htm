A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION COMMEMORATING THE CENTENNIAL CELEBRATION OF THE FOUNDING OF DELTA STATE UNIVERSITY.

     WHEREAS, we join the students, alumni, faculty and administration of Delta State University on the occasion of its Centennial Celebration, memorializing a century of academic excellence; and

     WHEREAS, on February 19, 1924, Mississippi Senator W. B. Roberts and Mississippi Senator Arthur L. Marshall introduced Mississippi Senate Bill 263 to create Delta State Teachers College, and it was signed by Mississippi Governor Henry L. Whitfield on April 9, 1924; and

     WHEREAS, Delta State Teachers College opened its doors officially on September 15, 1925, to 97 students and 11 faculty.  Delta State's first graduation was held in June 1928 with 13 students receiving Bachelor of Science degrees.  Delta State Teachers College officially became Delta State College on February 16, 1955.  In the summer of 1965, Delta State added a graduate program, which was a great step forward in providing additional training for the excellent teachers it had been educating since its establishment.  On March 15, 1974, Delta State College became Delta State University; and

     WHEREAS, Delta State University if a four-year public institution whose nearly 2,700 students come from most U.S. states and more than 35 countries; and

     WHEREAS, acknowledging its beginning as a teacher's college, the university sustains excellence in teacher education while continuing to expand offerings in traditional, as well as, unique fields of study; and

     WHEREAS, programs such as those available through the Delta Music Institute, the Commercial Aviation Department, and the Center for Interdisciplinary Geospatial Information highlight the university's commitment to meeting the evolving needs of the students it serves with world-leading, industry-acclaimed programs; and

     WHEREAS, Delta State University Statesmen and Lady Statesmen have a strong tradition of excellence in athletics, serving as a charter member of the Gulf South Conference, and boasting 14 national championships, including six in women's basketball, one in baseball, one in football, and six in swimming and diving; and

     WHEREAS, situated in the heart of the Mississippi Delta, Delta State offers top-notch academic programs in business, arts, sciences, nursing, education, and more; and

     WHEREAS, the Delta Council was organized at Delta State University on March 5, 1935, and Delta State President William M. Kethley served as its first president; and

     WHEREAS, the Marshall Plan was first announced at Delta State by Under-Secretary of State Dean Acheson on May 8, 1947; and

     WHEREAS, Delta State worked to create the Mississippi Delta National Heritage Area, which was officially designated by Congress in 2009.  It is one of 55 National Heritage Areas in the United States designated by Congress that tell nationally important stories, celebrating our nation's diverse heritage through community-based partnerships and local collaboration; and

     WHEREAS, Delta State University partnered with the local community to attract the Grammy Museum Mississippi, a museum focused on the musical achievements of Mississippians, to Cleveland, Mississippi, which opened on March 5, 2016; and

     WHEREAS, Delta State University has earned state, regional and national recognition as an exemplary institution dedicated to academic excellence, affordable access and accountability in the pursuit of its mission and the delivery of university, liberal arts, career/technical, workforce and adult education programs, and made possible, in part, by the commitment of the Mississippi Legislature to provide operating and capital funding support; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize the legacy and contributions of a great university which has brought honor to the Mississippi Delta and to the entire State of Mississippi:   NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING THEREIN, That we do hereby commemorate the Centennial Celebration of the founding of Delta State University in Cleveland, Mississippi, and extend to the students, alumni, faculty and administration our best wishes on this auspicious occasion.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Dr. Daniel J. Ennis, President of Delta State University, forwarded to the Board of Trustees of State Institutions of Higher Learning and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
