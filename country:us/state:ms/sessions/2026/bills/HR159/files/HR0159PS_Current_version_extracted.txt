HR 159 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Bell (21st) House Resolution 159 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING STAFF SERGEANT WILLIAM THURMAN EPPS, A WORLD WAR II VETERAN, UPON THE CELEBRATION OF HIS 100TH BIRTHDAY. WHEREAS, on July 12, 1926, the United States of America had just celebrated its Sesquicentennial, the 150th anniversary of its founding, hosted in Philadelphia; President Calvin Coolidge was leading the country through a period of political unrest across the globe; Hollywood was still dominated by silent film stars; electricity was decades away from rural Mississippi; and mechanized farming remained a fledgling vision in a field of dreams; and WHEREAS, however, on the same day in the City of Golden, Mississippi, the attention of the Epps family was singularly focused on welcoming a bundle of joy by the name of William Thurman Epps to the world, celebrating the precious gift of love and light with which they had just been blessed; and WHEREAS, Staff Sergeant William Thurman Epps, the third born of seven children, is celebrating the most auspicious occasion of the 100th anniversary of his birth on July 12, 2026, reflecting on a century's worth of life experiences - from the lessons he learned in his steady work on the family farm to his years of distinguished military service in the United States Army; and WHEREAS, known to all as Thurman, Staff Sergeant Epps spent his early life balancing grammar school with his work on his family's farm, and he was raised on the fundamental values of resilience, patience and responsibility, dedicating his time entirely to the farm after completing the eighth grade; and WHEREAS, in the midst of America's efforts fighting alongside the Allied powers in World War II, Staff Sergeant Epps received his "Greetings" letter from the President of the United States when he turned 18, a young farm boy who had never been outside of Tishomingo County called to serve his country in the United States Army, and he entered into service at Camp Shelby in October of 1944, ready to defend this great nation with exemplary patriotism and courage; and WHEREAS, Staff Sergeant Epps served in Nuremberg and displayed extraordinary leadership throughout his time in the Army, rising quickly through the ranks to his ultimate station as Staff Sergeant, and he was placed in charge of a platoon of men assigned to guard German prisoners, tasked with carrying German soldiers for trial and supervising the platoon while doing work details; and WHEREAS, though he has remained remarkably humble about his service, stating he did not see "anything but enough," Staff Sergeant Epps' brave service was recognized with multiple medals, including the Army of Occupation Medal, World War II Victory Medal and European African Middle Eastern Campaign Medal; and WHEREAS, Staff Sergeant Epps was honorably discharged from the Army in 1947, and after completing his service, he returned to Dennis, Mississippi, to tend to the family farm; and WHEREAS, after selling the farm, Staff Sergeant Epps was employed at Blue Bell Manufacturing until the time of his retirement, and with the company of his beloved wife, Nita, he settled into the routine of his simple way of life at their home in Golden, Mississippi, enjoying his beautiful garden, raising chickens, and keeping both the property and the household in immaculate condition; and WHEREAS, it has been nearly 10 years since Staff Sergeant Epps last had a garden, but even today, he still enjoys being outside in the fresh air and mowing his yard; and WHEREAS, a man of hard work, integrity and independence, Staff Sergeant Epps has forged a legacy of quiet strength and determined responsibility over the last 100 years, and he has generously shared his wisdom after discovering the secret for a long, happy life - a daily helping of veggies, a well-tended schedule, and a body in motion; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to honor our special Mississippi centenarians, especially those such as Staff Sergeant William Thurman Epps, whose selfless military service in World War II has helped to ensure that freedom will continue to ring throughout this country and whose love for his nation and his neighbor exemplify what it means to be an American and a Mississippian: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Staff Sergeant William Thurman Epps on the auspicious and blessed occasion of his 100th birthday, and wish him continued health, happiness and wellness in the years to come. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Staff Sergeant William Thurman Epps and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
