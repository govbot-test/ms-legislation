MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Constitution

By: Representative Ladner

House Concurrent Resolution 29

A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION PROPOSING AMENDMENTS TO SECTION 117 AND SECTION 250, MISSISSIPPI CONSTITUTION OF 1890, TO REQUIRE THE GOVERNOR AND EVERY PERSON SELECTED FOR OFFICE IN THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI AFTER JANUARY 1, 2027, TO BE A NATURAL-BORN CITIZEN OF THE UNITED STATES; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

     BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That the following amendments to the Mississippi Constitution of 1890 are proposed to the qualified electors of the state:

I.

     Amend Section 117, Mississippi Constitution of 1890, to read as follows:

     "Section 117.  The Governor shall be at least thirty (30) years of age, and beginning on January 1, 2027, shall * * *have been a be a natural-born citizen of the United States * * * twenty years, and shall have resided in this state five (5) years next preceding the day of his election."

II.

     Amend Section 250, Mississippi Constitution of 1890, to read as follows:

     "Section 250.  All natural-born citizens of the United States who are qualified electors and no others shall be eligible to office * * *, except as otherwise provided in this Constitution; provided,; however, that as to an office where no other qualification than that of being a natural-born citizen of the United States who is a qualified elector is provided by this Constitution, the Legislature may, by law, fix additional qualifications for such office.  The requirement that a person be a natural-born citizen of the United States as a qualification for holding office applies to all persons selected for office after January 1, 2027."

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That these proposed amendments shall be submitted by the Secretary of State to the qualified electors at an election to be held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday of November 2026, as provided by Section 273 of the Constitution and by general law, with the amendments in this resolution being voted on as one (1) amendment since the proposed amendments pertain to one (1) subject.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the explanation of this proposed amendment for the ballot shall read as follows: "This proposed constitutional amendment provides that the Governor and every person who is selected for office in the State of Mississippi after January 1, 2027, must be a natural-born citizen of the United States."
