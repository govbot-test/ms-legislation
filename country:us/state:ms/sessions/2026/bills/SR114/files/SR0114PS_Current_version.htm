MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Senator(s) Rhodes, Kirby, Harkins, McCaughn

Senate Resolution 114

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE 2025-2026 EAST RANKIN ACADEMY PATRIOTS BOYS SOCCER TEAM AND HEAD COACH REID WESTOVER FOR WINNING BACK-TO-BACK MAIS DIVISION III STATE CHAMPIONSHIPS.

     WHEREAS, East Rankin Academy Patriots Boys Soccer Team won back-to-back MAIS Division III State Championships in February, 2026, defeating Madison-St. Joseph 2-1 in overtime to secure their second consecutive title; and

     WHEREAS, eighth-grader Noah Craig scored the game-winning goal, following up on the school's first-ever title win in 2025; and

     WHEREAS, Reid Westover is the boys head soccer coach at East Rankin Academy.  He led the Patriots to their first-ever MAIS Division I State Championship on February 13, 2025, with a 1-0 victory over Heritage Academy.  Coach Westover has been recognized for building the program into a state-championship-winning team; and

     WHEREAS, the 2025-2026 East Rankin Academy Patriots Boys Soccer Team Roster:  Andy Bowman, Noah Craig, Channing Diveley, Callan Hunt, Cason Patrick, JM Daigle, Austin Craig, Cale Cotnam, Abner Hernandez, Baylor Sartin, Nic Riemann, Toler Logan, Jon Riemann, Camryn Young, Hector Ramirez, Robert Has, Montavious Loper and Landyn Williams; and Head Coach Reid Westover; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize these student-athletes who have brought honor to their school, their community and to the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the 2025-2026 East Rankin Academy Patriots Boys Soccer Team and Head Coach Reid Westover for winning back-to-back MAIS Division III State Championships, and extend to the team, coaches, parents and sponsors our best wishes for future championships.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Head Coach Reid Westover and Head of School Robyn Winstead for the team, forwarded to the East Rankin Academy Board of Trustees, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
