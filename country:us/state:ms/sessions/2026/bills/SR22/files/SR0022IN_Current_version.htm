MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Senator(s) McLendon

Senate Resolution 22

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING CHARLIE KIRK FOR HIS CONTRIBUTIONS TO CONSERVATIVE GRASSROOTS ENGAGEMENT, FREE SPEECH ADVOCACY, AND YOUTH CIVIC PARTICIPATION, AND RECOGNIZING THAT SEPTEMBER 10 OF EACH YEAR IS "CHARLIE KIRK DAY" IN THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI.

     WHEREAS, the State of Mississippi has a long-standing tradition of valuing free speech, civic engagement, and the active participation of its citizens in the democratic process; and

     WHEREAS, Charlie Kirk has emerged as one of the most influential conservative grassroots leaders of his generation.  He dedicated his career to educating, engaging, and empowering young Americans to participate in civic life; and

     WHEREAS, through nationwide outreach, public speaking, and organizational leadership, Charlie Kirk encouraged millions of young people to think critically, engage respectfully in public discourse, and exercise their constitutional rights; and

     WHEREAS, Charlie Kirk consistently advocated for free speech, limited government, personal responsibility, and the foundational principles upon which the United States of America was established; and

     WHEREAS, his efforts have significantly expanded youth civic participation across college campuses and communities nationwide, fostering a renewed interest in grassroots involvement and democratic engagement; and

     WHEREAS, Charlie Kirk's work has had a lasting and immeasurable impact on conservative grassroots engagement and free speech advocacy, inspiring the next generation of civic leaders; and

     WHEREAS, it is fitting and proper for the State of Mississippi to recognize individuals whose work strengthens civic participation, promotes constitutional principles, and encourages young Americans to engage constructively in the public square:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we hereby commend Charlie Kirk for his contributions to conservative grassroots engagement, free speech advocacy, and youth civic participation nationwide.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That we recognize that September 10 of each year is designated as "Charlie Kirk Day" in the State of Mississippi in recognition of his impact on civic engagement and

constitutional advocacy.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this designation is intended to honor the importance of free expression, civic involvement, and the vital role of young Americans in shaping the future of our state and nation.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be transmitted to the family of Charlie Kirk, his wife Erika Kirk, and to members of the Capitol Press Corps.
