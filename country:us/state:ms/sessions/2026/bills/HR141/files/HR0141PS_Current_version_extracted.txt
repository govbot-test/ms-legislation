HR 141 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Sanford House Resolution 141 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING MR. CHRISTOPHER PRICE NEWMAN FOR HIS MANY YEARS OF DISTINGUISHED SERVICE IN LAW ENFORCEMENT UPON THE AUSPICIOUS OCCASION OF HIS RETIREMENT. WHEREAS, after decades of exemplary public service, Mr. Christopher Price Newman is retiring from law enforcement, marking the end of a career but cementing a legacy of honor, courage, professionalism and steadfast commitment to the safety and well-being of his fellow citizens; and WHEREAS, Mr. Newman, a native of Clinton, Mississippi, received his bachelor's degree from The University of Southern Mississippi before graduating from the North Mississippi Law Enforcement Training Center in 1993, where he earned awards for the highest academic average and the highest overall average; and WHEREAS, launching a career that would span multiple agencies and various leadership roles across the State of Mississippi and beyond, Mr. Newman began his service in law enforcement with the City of Starkville in 1992, and during his time as a police officer for the city, he quickly rose to the rank of Sergeant; and WHEREAS, after accepting a position with the United States Border Patrol, Mr. Newman graduated from the Federal Law Enforcement Training Center in Glynco, Georgia, as part of USBP Class 300, and he was assigned to the United States Border Patrol Station in Douglas, Arizona; and WHEREAS, Mr. Newman was selected as a member of the Bike Patrol Unit (BPU) during his time in Douglas, where he worked with his teammates day and night in the most arduous terrain and unpredictable weather conditions imaginable in devoted service to his country, and together, the dedicated members of his unit helped to keep American streets safe by intercepting numerous drug shipments, securing our nation's border by arresting those attempting to cross; and WHEREAS, upon returning to his home state, Mr. Newman was selected by the Sheriff of Covington County to be the Chief Investigator of the Covington County Sheriff's Office in 2004, and in this role, he investigated all felony crimes in the county, diligently working a range of important cases involving aggravated assault, burglary, credit card fraud, identity theft and murder; and WHEREAS, Mr. Newman also obtained his certification as an NRA Certified Law Enforcement Firearms Instructor while working with the sheriff's office, a credential he still holds today; and WHEREAS, only two years later, Mr. Newman was selected for the position of criminal investigator at the Mississippi Department of Agriculture and Commerce, a testament to his incredible work ethic and meticulous approach to his duties, and his impressive work for the agency resulted in the successful prosecution of suspects accused of arson, timber theft, cattle theft, false pretense and burglary; and WHEREAS, as a result of his skillful investigative efforts, Mr. Newman secured an indictment against an infamous timber thief in southern Mississippi, sealing the fate of the defendant with his credible testimony and leading the jury to return a guilty verdict on the charges of timber theft and false pretense, and he was recognized as the "Top Cop" in 2013 for his stellar work on the case; and WHEREAS, Mr. Newman joined the Mississippi Attorney General's Office as a criminal investigator in 2016, and upon being assigned to the Medicaid Fraud Division, he worked cases that led to the conviction of multiple suspects for Medicaid fraud, including one of the most prolific fraudsters in the Jackson metro area; and WHEREAS, further honing his expertise, Mr. Newman also received the Certified Fraud Examiner credential while employed by the Attorney General's Office; and WHEREAS, Mr. Newman continued to achieve excellence in his position as a criminal investigator with the Mississippi Department of Revenue, and cementing his place at the top of his field, he was responsible for securing the conviction of a defendant in a criminal tax evasion case that resulted in one of the largest restitution payments from such a case in the department's history; and WHEREAS, in recognition of his undeniable efficacy and ability to lead by example, Mr. Newman was promoted to the prestigious position of Chief of the Criminal Investigation Division at the Mississippi Department of Revenue in March 2024, and during his time in this role, the Criminal Investigation Division realized some of their largest numbers ever recorded for criminal cases worked, criminal indictments and criminal convictions, seeing growth of nearly 300% for the Fiscal Year 2025; and WHEREAS, as Mr. Newman celebrates his well-earned retirement and reflects upon his accomplishments, both personal and professional, it is most appropriate to honor the impact of his most admirable service and sacrifice, commemorating his unwavering determination to stand up for those less fortunate and show compassion to both victims and suspects involved in his cases; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend outstanding public servants, especially those such as Mr. Christopher Price Newman, whose dedicated service and impeccable leadership have greatly enriched his community, bringing great pride to the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Mr. Christopher Price Newman upon the occasion of his retirement, and extend sincere wishes for continued success and joy in the lighter days ahead. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Mr. Christopher Price Newman and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
