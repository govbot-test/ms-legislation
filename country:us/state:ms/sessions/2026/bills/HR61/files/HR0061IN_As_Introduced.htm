MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative Nelson

House Resolution 61

A RESOLUTION ACKNOWLEDGING THE OBSERVATION OF THE "REST Y'ALL MISSISSIPPI COLLECTIVE DAY OF REST" TO BE RECOGNIZED ANNUALLY ON MARCH 20, THE FIRST DAY OF SPRING, THROUGHOUT THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI.

     WHEREAS, the State of Mississippi continues to confront significant public health challenges associated with chronic stress, burnout, cardiovascular disease, mental health strain and caregiver fatigue affecting individuals, families and communities across the state; and

     WHEREAS, research and public health guidance consistently affirm that intentional rest and recovery are essential to physical health, emotional regulation, mental well-being, workplace productivity and long-term resilience; and

     WHEREAS, rest is not inactivity, but a preventative and life-sustaining wellness practice that strengthens individuals, supports families and enhances the sustainability of communities; and

     WHEREAS, Black women in Mississippi are disproportionately impacted by chronic stress due to intersecting responsibilities as caregivers, professionals, community leaders and family anchors, which may contribute to adverse health and wellness outcomes; and

     WHEREAS, Black Women DO Heal, Inc., a nonprofit organization with strong Mississippi roots, provides community-based wellness leadership and promotes rest, recovery and sustainability as essential components of holistic health and well-being; and

     WHEREAS, the first day of Spring symbolizes renewal, restoration and new beginnings, aligning with the values of healing, balance and sustainability for individuals and communities throughout Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize initiatives that promote awareness of public health, wellness and preventative practices that strengthen the people of this state:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby acknowledge and recognize March 20 of each year, the first day of Spring, as the "REST Y'all Mississippi Collective Day of Rest," encouraging Mississippians to embrace intentional practices of rest, reflection and renewal in support of individual and community well-being.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That public agencies, private businesses, educational institutions, nonprofit organizations, healthcare systems, faith-based organizations and community groups are encouraged to acknowledge this day through voluntary, rest-supportive practices, including flexible scheduling, wellness activities, reduced meetings, quiet spaces or educational conversations addressing stress and burnout.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That participation in the "REST Y'all Mississippi Collective Day of Rest" is voluntary and nonmandatory, intended solely to promote awareness, holistic health and sustainability across the State of Mississippi.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Black Women DO Heal, Inc., and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
