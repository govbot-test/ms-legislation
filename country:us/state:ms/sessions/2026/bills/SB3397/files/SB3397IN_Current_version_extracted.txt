SB3397 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Judiciary, Division B By: Senator(s) Hickman Senate Bill 3397 AN ACT TO RESTORE THE RIGHT OF SUFFRAGE TO FRANKIE O'NEAL WARD, JR., OF JEFFERSON DAVIS COUNTY, MISSISSIPPI; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI: SECTION 1.  The right of suffrage is hereby fully and completely restored to Frankie O'Neal Ward, Jr., of Jefferson Davis County, Mississippi, who was disqualified as an elector on August 16, 2002, as a result of his conviction in Simpson County for two (2) counts of armed robbery and one (1) count of grand larceny.  As a related matter, he was also convicted in Simpson County of kidnapping.  He was sentenced to serve four (4), concurrent terms in the custody of the Mississippi Department of Corrections: (a)  A fifteen-year term for the first charge of armed robbery with five (5) years suspended and a five-year term of post-release supervision; (b)  A fifteen-year term for the second charge of armed robbery with five (5) years suspended and a five-year term of post-release supervision; (c)  A ten-year term for the charge of kidnapping; and (d)  A three-year term for the charge of grand larceny.     He was discharged from the custody of the Mississippi Department of Corrections on June 14, 2012, due to the expiration of his sentence.  Upon representation from the Mississippi Department of Corrections, he was discharged from probation on March 29, 2016.  He completed all terms and conditions of his sentence. The Legislature is informed that he has since conducted himself as a law-abiding and honorable citizen in a good and lawful manner. SECTION 2.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after July 1, 2026.
