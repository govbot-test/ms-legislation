SR 99 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) DuPree, Johnson, Hartness, Fillingane Senate Resolution 99 A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING LEGENDARY BASKETBALL COACH STEVE KNIGHT AS HE RETIRES AFTER A 44-YEAR CAREER AS MEN'S BASKETBALL COACH AT WILLIAM CAREY UNIVERSITY AND HIS 800TH CAREER VICTORY. WHEREAS, we join the basketball program at William Carey University in honoring the career and legacy of legendary Coach Steve Knight; and WHEREAS, after 44 seasons on the Carey bench, Mississippi's all-time winningest college basketball coach, Steve Knight, announced his retirement on March 19, 2026, bringing an end to a remarkable career that ranks amongst the best in college basketball history; and WHEREAS, Knight was named as the Crusader Basketball Head Coach in 1982, kicking off a career that most only dream of. In just his second season (1984), the Crusaders were Gulf Coast Athletic Conference Champions for the first time under his leadership. Knight's Crusaders won five more GCAC Championships in 1985, 1987, 1990, 1992, and 1997.  During that span, Carey went to the NAIA National Tournament seven times; and WHEREAS, in 1987, along with his duties as the head basketball coach, Knight was named as William Carey's Athletic Director.  He served in that role for 30 years until 2016.  During his tenure as Athletic Director, William Carey more than tripled the number of sport offerings from five in 1987 to 17 in 2016.  Knight was selected as a conference Athletic Director of the Year four times and the NAIA Southeast Athletic Director of the Year in 2005-2006.  He also served as President of the Gulf Coast Athletic Conference from 2001-2005 and again from 2006-2010; and WHEREAS, even doing double duty for so many years, Knight's basketball machine kept rolling.  Carey left the GCAC in favor of the Southern States Athletic Conference in 2010, and the wins kept piling up.  Carey made it to the NAIA National Tournament six times between 2010 and 2019, including SSAC Championships in 2014 and 2018.  The 2014 campaign ended with a 28-3 mark, setting a school record for wins in a season and earning Knight the title of NAIA National Men's Basketball Coach of the Year.  In 2019, Knight's team earned a trip to the NAIA Fab Four for the first time in school history.  The latest team achievement came just this season, when the Crusaders tied the school record for wins (28) en route to another SSAC Championship and trip to the NAIA National Tournament; and WHEREAS, Knight finishes his career with 819 wins, leaving him in an impressive eighth place in NAIA history.  Before announcing his retirement, he was first on the list of active coaches.  His teams amassed twenty-two 20 win seasons in his career, including 11 regular season conference championships, eight tournament championships, and 15 trips to the NAIA postseason.  Knight has been selected as a District/Conference Coach of the Year 15 times, including this season.  In 2015, he became the first ever representative from William Carey to be inducted into the Mississippi Sports Hall of Fame.  In addition to the Mississippi Hall of Fame, he is also a member of the University of Southern Mississippi (M Club) Hall of Fame, the William Carey University Athletic Hall of Fame, the Hattiesburg Public School District Hall of Fame, and the Southern Miss Alumni Hall of Fame; and WHEREAS, in his baseball career, Coach Knight became an outstanding pitcher for Hall of Famer Pete Taylor at USM for four seasons.  He once threw a no-hitter with fellow Hall of Famer Corky Palmer behind the plate and played basketball three seasons for Hall of Famer M.K. Turk.  In professional baseball, he spent two seasons in the Seattle Mariners organization with a 3.23 earned run average and 18 saves and retired from baseball to begin a coaching career; and WHEREAS, "The basketball program and the university owe a lot to Coach Knight," said WCU Athletic Director Tracy English.  "For forty-four years he produced championships and sent young men back to their communities to be productive citizens.  He coached hundreds of players that went on to become coaches, bankers, doctors, businessmen, preachers"; and WHEREAS, "I have had the opportunity to work with awesome, supportive people, including Presidents, administrators, staff, and fellow coaches, and could not have envisioned a better job experience than what I have had for so long in my hometown," Coach Knight said: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby extend and recognize Legendary Basketball Coach Steve Knight as he retires after a 44-year career as men's basketball coach at William Carey University and his 800th career victory. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach Knight, forwarded to Athletic Director of William Carey University Tracy English and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
