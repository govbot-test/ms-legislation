MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative Mangold

House Resolution 30

(As Adopted by House)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING THE LIFE, MOTHERHOOD AND WISE COUNSEL OF MRS. JUANITA PAULINE SMITH BURNS AND CONGRATULATING HER UPON THE OCCASION OF THE CENTENNIAL ANNIVERSARY OF HER BIRTH ON FEBRUARY 19, 2026.

     WHEREAS, on February 19, 1926, the United States was under the leadership of President Calvin Coolidge, the State of Mississippi was governed by Governor Henry L. Whitfield, rural electrification had yet to reach much of the South, and the nation stood between the trials of World War I and the economic hardship that would soon follow with the Great Depression; and

     WHEREAS, on that historic date, Mrs. Juanita Pauline Smith Burns was born in her family home in the Pleasant Hill Community of Lincoln County, Mississippi, to the union of her late parents, Mrs. Bertha Norton Smith and Mr. Robert Leon Smith, beginning a life that would span a full century of faith, perseverance, family devotion and service to others; and

     WHEREAS, the eighth of ten children, Mrs. Burns' life was shaped by the values of hard work, humility and community that defined her upbringing, values she would later impart to generations of her family and all who came under her influence, both direct and indirect; and

     WHEREAS, a 1944 graduate of Lincoln County's Enterprise High School, the genteel and demure Southern Belle who was embodied in the elegant poise and grace of Miss Smith would in that same year marry the chivalrous and charming Carl M. Burns, the gentlemen the holy deity designed especially for her, on May 5, 1944, beginning a devoted marital union as bone of another's bone and flesh of one another's flesh, which endured as a timeless treasure for nearly 53 years, until God's chariot angels escorted his soul into the Lord's presence in 1997; and

     WHEREAS, Mrs. Burns' life journey carried her to Brownsville, Texas, and Baton Rouge, Louisiana, before she returned home, residing for more than seventy-four years in the Pleasant Hill Community, where her presence became the hallmark emblem as a steady and trusted pillar of strength, resilience and faith; and

     WHEREAS, through decades of honorable labor, Mrs. Burns worked as a cashier at Jitney Jungle, as a clothing apparel inspector at Kellwood in Summit, Mississippi, for 34 years, and later continued her service as a senior aide at Mamie Martin Elementary School and as a receptionist at the Social Security and Unemployment Office in Brookhaven, Mississippi, exemplifying diligence, integrity and grace in every role she assumed; and

     WHEREAS, the proud mother of one son, Roe Burns, and mother-in-law to Kay Wallace Burns, the branches of Mrs. Burns' familial tree has been further blessed with the fruitful addition of three grandchildren, three grandchildren-in-law, eleven great-grandchildren, and one great-great-grandchild, allowing her to stand as the matriarch in the orchard of her five-generation family, whose foundation is rooted in faith, wisdom and unconditional love; and

     WHEREAS, Mrs. Burns is the oldest living member of Pleasant Hill Baptist Church, where she has faithfully served as a beginner's Sunday School teacher, church hostess, member of the Woman's Missionary Union and participant on a pastor search committee, offering steady guidance and wise counsel within her church family; and

     WHEREAS, her life has been further enriched by her generosity of spirit, expressed through her love of cooking and baking, her gift of sharing chocolate and coconut pies, her skill in quilting, and her enduring commitment to maintaining meaningful relationships throughout her life; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the Mississippi House of Representatives to pause in honor to a woman whose century of life reflects steadfast faith, selfless service, nurturing motherhood and wisdom that has strengthened family, church and community alike:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend the life, motherhood and wise counsel of Mrs. Juanita Pauline Smith Burns, congratulate her upon the occasion of the centennial anniversary of her birth and induction as the newest member of the centenarian club, and extend to her heartfelt wishes for continued peace, joy and the abiding love of family and friends.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Mrs. Juanita Pauline Smith Burns and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
