House Bill 1730

AN ACT TO CREATE SECTIONS 31-11-37 AND 31-11-39, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO DEFINE TERMS USED HEREIN; TO PROHIBIT THE DESIGN, BIDDING, PERMITTING, CONTRACTING FOR OR CONSTRUCTION OF FLAT OR LOW-SLOPE ROOF SYSTEMS IN THE NEW CONSTRUCTION OF COVERED PUBLIC FACILITIES FUNDED IN WHOLE OR IN PART WITH GOVERNMENTAL FUNDS; TO ESTABLISH A MINIMUM ROOF SLOPE STANDARD OF THREE-TO-TWELVE FOR PRIMARY ROOF PLANES; TO AUTHORIZE LIMITED, CLEARLY DEFINED EXCEPTIONS FOR INCIDENTAL ROOF AREAS AND DOCUMENTED ENGINEERING CONSTRAINTS; TO REQUIRE CERTIFICATION OF COMPLIANCE AS A CONDITION PRECEDENT TO PLAN APPROVAL, CONTRACT AWARD, FUNDING, REIMBURSEMENT AND DISBURSEMENT; TO AUTHORIZE SUSPENSION OR RECOVERY OF FUNDS FOR NONCOMPLIANCE; TO AUTHORIZE RULEMAKING BY THE DEPARTMENT OF FINANCE AND ADMINISTRATION FOR IMPLEMENTATION AND COMPLIANCE REVIEW; TO AMEND SECTION 31-11-3, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO CONDITION THE DEPARTMENT OF FINANCE AND ADMINISTRATION'S APPROVAL, SUPERVISION, AND EXPENDITURE OF FUNDS FOR PUBLIC CONSTRUCTION PROJECTS ON COMPLIANCE WITH THE ROOF SLOPE PROHIBITION AND CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS; TO AMEND SECTION 37-101-15, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO CONDITION CAPITAL PROJECT APPROVALS AND CONSTRUCTION AUTHORITY OF THE BOARD OF TRUSTEES OF STATE INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER LEARNING ON COMPLIANCE WITH THE ROOF SLOPE PROHIBITION; TO AMEND SECTION 57-1-55, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO CONDITION GRANTS, LOANS, BOND-FUNDED ASSISTANCE, REIMBURSEMENTS, MATCHES AND OTHER FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE ADMINISTERED BY THE MISSISSIPPI DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY ON COMPLIANCE WITH THE ROOF SLOPE PROHIBITION; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

     BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI:

     SECTION 1.  The following shall be codified as Section 31-11-37, Mississippi Code of 1972:

     31-11-37.  For purposes of this act, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings ascribed in this section, unless the context of use clearly requires otherwise:

          (a)  "Covered public facility" means any building or structure that is:

              (i)  Owned, leased, operated or primarily occupied by the State of Mississippi, a state agency, a state institution of higher learning, a community or junior college district, a county, a municipality or any political subdivision, authority, board or commission of the state; and

              (ii)  Constructed as a new facility, new building, or new building addition that increases the gross roof area of an existing public facility.

          (b)  "Governmental funding assistance" means any state, local or federal funds administered, appropriated, granted, loaned, bonded, reimbursed, matched or otherwise provided by or through a governmental entity for a covered public facility project, including without limitation general obligation bond proceeds, state aid, grants, loans, and other financial assistance administered by the Department of Finance and Administration, the Board of Trustees of State Institutions of Higher Learning, or the Mississippi Development Authority.

          (c)  "Low-slope roof" means a roof having a slope of less than three (3) units vertical in twelve (12) units horizontal (3:12).

          (d)  "Flat roof" means a roof having a slope of less than one (1) unit vertical in twelve (12) units horizontal (1:12).

          (e)  "New construction" means the construction of a new public facility or the replacement of an existing structure, and does not include routine maintenance, repair, or roof replacement that does not materially alter the structure of the building.

     SECTION 2.  The following shall be codified as Section 31-11-39, Mississippi Code of 1972:

     31-11-39.  (1)  A covered public facility that is constructed using governmental funds, whether in whole or in part, shall not be designed, bid, permitted, contracted for or constructed with a flat roof or a low-slope roof.  For purposes of compliance with this subsection, the primary roof planes of a covered public facility shall have a minimum slope of at least three (3) units of  vertical rise for every twelve (12) units of horizontal for a ratio of 3:12.

     (2)  (a)  Subsection (1) of this section does not prohibit:

              (i)  A roof area used solely for mechanical equipment platforms, elevator overruns, stair towers, skylight curbs, clerestories, solar installations, parapets, gutters, scuppers, crickets or other appurtenances that are incidental to and necessary for the operation of the facility;

              (ii)  Canopies, walkways, porte-cocheres, covered patios and similar accessory coverings that are not part of the primary roof plane; or

              (iii)  A roof area that, due to documented engineering constraints, must be designed at a slope less than the 3:12 primary roof plane required under subsection (1), provided that such area does not exceed ten percent (10%) of the total gross roof area of the covered public facility.

          (b)  Any exception claimed under this subsection (2) must be supported by written findings from the architect or engineer of record describing the specific constraint and the minimum practicable slope that can be achieved.

     (3)  (a)  For any covered public facility subject to this section, the entity seeking approval, funding, reimbursement or other governmental funding assistance shall certify compliance with this section in the form and manner required by the approving or funding authority.  No state agency, political subdivision, board, commission or authority shall approve plans, issue a notice to proceed, enter into a construction contract, award a bid, disburse funds, or reimburse costs for a covered public facility subject to this section unless the project complies with this section.

          (b)  If governmental funding assistance has been awarded or disbursed for a covered public facility that is later determined to be noncompliant, the awarding authority may suspend disbursements and may require repayment of all or a portion of the governmental funding assistance, consistent with applicable law and the terms of the award.

     (4)  The Department of Finance and Administration may promulgate rules necessary to implement this section, including standardized certifications and compliance review procedures.
