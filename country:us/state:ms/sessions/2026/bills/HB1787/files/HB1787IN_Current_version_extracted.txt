Section 1:
HB1787 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Local and Private Legislation By: Representative Remak House Bill 1787 AN ACT TO AUTHORIZE THE BOARD OF SUPERVISORS OF DESOTO COUNTY, MISSISSIPPI, TO PURCHASE RADAR SPEED DETECTION EQUIPMENT FOR USE BY THE SHERIFF'S DEPARTMENT OF THE COUNTY IN THE ENFORCEMENT OF SCHOOL ZONE SPEED LIMITS ON THE ROADS AND STATE HIGHWAYS OF THE COUNTY OUTSIDE THE CORPORATE LIMITS OF ANY INCORPORATED MUNICIPALITY; TO PROVIDE THAT ANY FUNDS RECEIVED THROUGH USE OF SUCH EQUIPMENT SHALL BE USED TO FUND RESOURCE OFFICERS IN THE DESOTO COUNTY PUBLIC SCHOOLS; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI: SECTION 1. (1)  The Board of Supervisors of DeSoto County, Mississippi, is authorized, in its power, to expend, out of any available funds of the county, for the purchase of radar speed detection equipment.  Such expenditure may be in an amount that the board determines is sufficient, and such purchase shall be for use by the sheriff's department.  If the board of supervisors authorizes the expenditure of funds for this purpose, then the Sheriff of DeSoto County and any duly sworn deputy sheriff of the county may operate and use the equipment to enforce the motor vehicle speed limits on any public county road or state highway where there is located a school zone, which is located outside the corporate limits of any incorporated municipality. As used in this subsection (1) "school zone" means designated roadway segments approaching, adjacent to, and beyond school buildings or grounds, or along with school related activities occur. (2)  Any funds received in association with the issuance of a citation resulting from a speeding violation detected by a sheriff or deputy sheriff through the use of radar speed detection equipment shall be used to fund school resource officers in the various public schools of the county. SECTION 2.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its passage.
