MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative Calvert

House Resolution 27

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE LAMAR SCHOOL RAIDERS FOOTBALL TEAM AND HEAD COACH JACOB LAND UPON WINNING THE 2025 MID-SOUTH ASSOCIATION OF INDEPENDENT SCHOOLS (MAIS) CLASS 4A DIVISION III STATE CHAMPIONSHIP.

     WHEREAS, the Lamar School Raiders Football Team once again demonstrated exceptional excellence, discipline and determination by winning the Mid-South Association of Independent Schools (MAIS) Class 4A Division III State Championship with a decisive victory over Brookhaven Academy on Saturday, November 22, 2025; and

     WHEREAS, this championship marks the seventh state football title in Lamar School history, a milestone that reflects the sustained tradition of athletic excellence, character development and competitive success cultivated within the Lamar Raiders football program; and

     WHEREAS, the Raiders opened the championship contest with commanding intensity, scoring two early touchdowns to establish a 14-0 lead, while the Lamar defense delivered an electrifying performance highlighted by two decisive interception returns for touchdowns, one by senior Jalin Trotter and another by senior MiKeal Willis, firmly shifting the momentum of the game; and

     WHEREAS, despite significant adversity during the contest, including the loss of key players due to sideline incidents that resulted in multiple ejections, the Raiders remained resilient, composed and unified, ultimately overcoming all obstacles to secure the state championship title; and

     WHEREAS, under the steady leadership of Head Coach Jacob Land and Assistant Coaches Adrian Hopkins, Jonathan Reneau, Matthew Whiting, Charlie Ballou, Caleb Wilcke, McLeod Norwood, Jeff Breland, Todd Vowell and Gavin Mosley, the Raiders have fostered a culture grounded in accountability, perseverance, sportsmanship and purpose, both on and off the field, which proved instrumental in their championship run; and

     WHEREAS, Coach Land credited the team's unwavering resolve, character development and shared sense of purpose as the defining factors in the Raiders' success, noting that the team's commitment to personal growth and unity enabled them to withstand adversity and finish the season as champions; and

     WHEREAS, the 2025 Lamar School Raiders State Championship Football Team roster is comprised of:  Jalin Trotter, Robert Gardner, Jhaqwon Slack, Jackson Whitcomb, Luke Brady, Samuel Roberts, MiKeal Willis, Sullivan Reed, Gavin Aull, Ashton Jones, Walker Mims, Hamp Haarala, Benson Oates, Maddox Mims, Briggs Calvert, Cade Vowell, Logan Cross, Knox Davis, Landon Johnson, Caleb Randall, Joe Morrow, Pace Perrett, Preston Perrett, Khari Towner, Chase Putman, Luke Morrow, Tyler Brown, Bryson Boswell, Cormac Dulaney, Lucas Williams, James Ross, Henry Prewitt, Wendell Bell, Mason Brock, Jackson Spitzkeit, Nate Lee, Hayes Lancaster, Ryan McDaniel, Samuel Hines, Brett Calvert and Jack Voss; and

     WHEREAS, the success of the Raiders was further supported by the dedication of Team Managers Liam Clayton, Clarke Newlin, Sam Komar, Hudson Haarala, Travis Boston, Landrey Wilkerson, EJ Carter and Keegan Aull; Athletic Trainer Alan Tingle; Athletic Director Matt Boone; and Head of School Leigh Ann Ballou, whose leadership and commitment contributed significantly to the program's excellence; and

     WHEREAS, the Raiders' championship achievement has brought great pride to Lamar School, Lauderdale County and the State of Mississippi, while serving as a powerful example to student-athletes across the state of the rewards of hard work, discipline and teamwork; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend outstanding achievements by student-athletes whose accomplishments bring honor to their schools, communities and the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Lamar School Raiders Football Team and Head Coach Jacob Land upon winning the 2025 MAIS Class 4A Division III State Championship, and extend to the players, coaches, staff, parents and supporters best wishes for continued success in all their future endeavors.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Lamar School Head of School Leigh Ann Ballou, Head Coach Jacob Land on behalf of the Raiders Football Team and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
