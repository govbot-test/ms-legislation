HR 157 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Holloway (76th) House Resolution 157 A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING AND COMMENDING DR. MARIE HAMILTON-ABSTON AND ESSIE DAVIS-FLORENCE, OWNERS OF HAMILTON-DAVIS MENTAL HEALTH, INC., AND HAMILTON-DAVIS CARE AT HOME, INC., FOR THEIR OUTSTANDING CONTRIBUTIONS IN PROVIDING MENTAL HEALTH AND HOME CARE SERVICES TO THE CITIZENS OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI. WHEREAS, Hamilton-Davis Mental Health, Inc., is a provider of non-medical home care services to the elderly or disabled individuals, who may require assistance in their own homes and communities to assist in the maintenance and retention of their independence and well-being without the stress and hardships of interrupted routines and changes in daily habits; and WHEREAS, Hamilton-Davis Mental Health, Inc., is a proud recipient of the 2015 Black Enterprise Community Pillar of the Year Award for services that enrich lives and is committed to building both its brand and community; and WHEREAS, Hamilton-Davis Mental Health, Inc., is a provider of comprehensive mental health services, including Mississippi Department of Mental Health (DMH) Private Provider services for individuals with substance use disorders, as well as DMH Home and Community Based Services (HCBS) ID/DD Waiver services for individuals with intellectual and/or developmental disabilities; and WHEREAS, Dr. Marie Hamilton-Abston, a Madison native, who holds a cosmetologist license, bachelor's degree in accounting, a master's degree in health administration, and doctorate in health administration, is a trailblazer in Mississippi history as the state's first African-American owner of an opioid treatment clinic, demonstrating an unwavering commitment to providing withdrawal management services to people suffering from chronic addiction to opioids and opioid derivatives; and WHEREAS, Dr. Marie Hamilton-Abston is the co-owner of multiple thriving business establishments, consisting of: Hamilton-Davis Mental Health, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Home Care, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Medical Supply, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Adult Day Care, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Transport, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Assisted Living Agency, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Community Outreach, Inc., Hamilton-Davis K-12 Private Academy, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Auto Sales, Inc., Zuri Marie Cosmetics, Zuri Marie Virgin Hair and Zuri Marie Fashion Designer, exemplifying entrepreneurial excellence while also fulfilling her roles as a devoted mother, grandmother, daughter, sister, aunt, community leader and multi-business owner and the rewards of fulfillment that accompany each role; and WHEREAS, Dr. Marie Hamilton-Abston is also a reality TV star of "Belle Collective," an American reality television series that airs on the Oprah Winfrey Network (OWN), which premiered on January 15, 2021, and has continued through multiple seasons, with Season 6 debuting in October 2025; and WHEREAS, Essie Davis-Florence, a native of Pelahatchie, Mississippi, is a graduate of Pelahatchie High School and earned a bachelor of business degree in accounting from Jackson State University; and WHEREAS, Essie Davis-Florence is a partner and co-owner of Hamilton-Davis Mental Health, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Home Care, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Medical Supply, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Adult Day Care, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Transport, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Assisted Living Agency, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Community Outreach, Inc., Hamilton-Davis K-12 Private Academy, Inc., Hamilton-Davis Auto Sales, Inc., Zuri Marie Cosmetics, Zuri Marie Virgin Hair and Zuri Marie Fashion Designer; and WHEREAS, Essie Davis-Florence is the co-founder of the first opioid treatment clinic established by an African-American woman, an achievement that underscores her commitment to expanding access to critical healthcare services and addressing substance use disorders within underserved communities across Mississippi; and WHEREAS, her work in healthcare and service sectors demonstrates both innovation and resilience, particularly in navigating complex regulatory environments to deliver impactful lifechanging services; and WHEREAS, beyond her professional accomplishments, Essie Davis-Florence is deeply committed to family, community, and legacy, embodying the values of leadership, service and excellence that strengthen the fabric of the State of Mississippi while also maintaining a balanced family life as the wife of entrepreneur, Leslie Florence, and the mother of three children, Taj Florence, Dwaun Patterson and Jaedyn Florence; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and honor strong, accomplished, and visionary African-American women who are the symbols of Mississippi's remarkable talents in education, leadership, entrepreneurship, service and reflect the exceptional talents and contributions of the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby recognize and commend the owners of Hamilton-Davis Mental Health, Inc., and Hamilton-Davis Care at Home, Inc., for their dedication to providing high-quality mental health, home care, substance abuse and intellectual and developmental disability services to the citizens of Mississippi, and extend best wishes for continued success in all their future endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Dr. Marie Hamilton-Abston and Essie Davis-Florence, and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
