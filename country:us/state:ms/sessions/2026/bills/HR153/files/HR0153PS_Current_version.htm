MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative Anthony

House Resolution 153

(As Adopted by House)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING YASMINE WARE UPON BEING NAMED A 2025 HARRY S. TRUMAN SCHOLAR AND BECOMING THE UNIVERSITY OF MISSISSIPPI'S 20TH TRUMAN SCHOLAR.

     WHEREAS, Yasmine Ware, a University of Mississippi (Ole Miss) student, has been named a 2025 Harry S. Truman Scholar, becoming the institution's 20th recipient of this prestigious national honor, and one of only 54 students selected nationwide from a competitive pool of more than 700 applicants; and

     WHEREAS, the Harry S. Truman Scholarship, established by Congress in 1975 as a living memorial to the 33rd President of the United States, is the premier graduate scholarship for aspiring public service leaders, recognizing students who demonstrate exceptional academic achievement, leadership potential and a steadfast commitment to careers in government and public service; and

     WHEREAS, Miss Ware, a native of Madison, Mississippi, is an interdisciplinary studies major with concentrations in international studies, Chinese and global security, whose academic excellence and intellectual curiosity have distinguished her as a scholar of the highest caliber; and

     WHEREAS, throughout her collegiate career, Yasmine has demonstrated exemplary leadership and service, serving as President of the Undergraduate Black Law Student Association, a member of the Columns Society, a participant in the Associated Student Body President's Cabinet and Senate, and a senator within the Sally McDonnell Barksdale Honors College; and

     WHEREAS, her commitment to public service extends beyond the classroom, as evidenced by her internships in the United States Congress, her international studies with the United Nations, and her research endeavors abroad, including the study of cultural heritage in Kyoto, Japan; and

     WHEREAS, Miss Ware has distinguished herself as an award-winning communicator and storyteller, producing the nationally recognized podcast "Yasmine's Warehouse," which earned top honors in The New York Times' Student Podcast Contest and led to her invitation to the White House as press, reflecting her unique ability to elevate voices and narratives that might otherwise go unheard; and

     WHEREAS, her dedication to preserving history and advancing community awareness is further exemplified by her receipt of the inaugural James H. Meredith Community Transformation Award, through which she developed the documentary "The Mound Bayou Memoirs," chronicling one of the nation's first all African-American municipalities and emphasizing the importance of cultural preservation; and

     WHEREAS, Miss Ware's vision for the future is rooted in service to others, as she aspires to pursue a Juris Doctor and advanced graduate studies in public administration and international development, with the goal of working within international institutions to promote peace, security, and cultural heritage preservation; and

     WHEREAS, Yasmine's selection as a Truman Scholar is a testament not only to her academic excellence and leadership, but also to the unwavering support of her family, including her parents, Dr. Terrance Ware and Zandrea Ware, and her brother, Jacob, whose encouragement has helped shape her strong sense of purpose and commitment to making a meaningful impact in Mississippi and beyond; and

     WHEREAS, Miss Ware's achievements reflect great credit upon herself, her family, the University of Mississippi, and the State of Mississippi, and serve as a powerful inspiration to future generations of students who aspire to lead lives of service and distinction; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend outstanding Mississippians whose accomplishments bring honor to this state and exemplify the highest ideals of leadership, scholarship and service, as demonstrated by Yasmine Ware:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Yasmine Ware upon being named a 2025 Harry S. Truman Scholar, becoming the University of Mississippi's 20th recipient of this distinguished honor, and extend sincerest wishes for continued success in all her future endeavors.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Yasmine Ware, the Chancellor of the University of Mississippi and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
