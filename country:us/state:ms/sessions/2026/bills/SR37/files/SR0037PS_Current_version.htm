MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Senator(s) Whaley, England

Senate Resolution 37

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING ABBEY HARTY FROM EDAM, SASKATCHEWAN, AS MISS RODEO CANADA 2026 AND WELCOMING HER TO MISSISSIPPI ON THE OCCASION OF THE DIXIE NATIONAL LIVESTOCK AND RODEO SHOW.

     WHEREAS, Abbey Harty has been crowned Miss Rodeo Canada 2026; and

     WHEREAS, Abbey is a 22-year-old first-generation rodeo athlete from Edam, Saskatchewan, raised in a rural ranching community.  She has been involved in rodeo and horsemanship for over a decade, competing in multiple rodeo associations across Western Canada.  Through her involvement in the sport, Abbey has developed strong values of perseverance, respect, and community, principles that guide both her personal and professional life; and

     WHEREAS, Abbey currently serves as an executive member and Social Media Manager for the University of Saskatchewan College Rodeo Team.  She also proudly holds a position as a board member of the Canadian College Rodeo Association, where she contributes to the promotion and advancement of collegiate rodeo across Canada.  Beyond the rodeo arena, Abbey has worked in the healthcare field for over five years and is currently employed at a long-term care facility.  She is pursuing a career in Nursing at Saskatchewan Polytechnic, with aspirations of obtaining a Master's degree in Dementia Studies.  In 2018, Abbey's rodeo career was abruptly interrupted following a serious ATV accident that resulted in a partial arm amputation.  After undergoing multiple surgeries and years of rehabilitation, she rediscovered her resilience and determination, ultimately returning to the rodeo arena with a renewed perspective and deep appreciation for the sport; and

     WHEREAS, Abbey's journey in rodeo later led her to discover a passion for rodeo queening.  Her path began as the North Battleford Rodeo Princess and continued through her role as Miss Rodeo Agribition; and

     WHEREAS, now representing Canadian Professional Rodeo as Miss Rodeo Canada, Abbey is honored to share the traditions, values, and heritage of Canada's western way of life  an honor she carries with pride and purpose; and

     WHEREAS, as Miss Rodeo Canada 2026, Abbey will serve as the official representative of Canada.  During her reign, she will travel over 40,000 miles and appear at nearly 100 rodeo performances, as well as attend various schools, civic groups, and special events to educate the public about rodeo, its sponsors, and its opportunities.  Olivia will also serve as a spokesperson at promotional events and will model in commercial advertisements for western clothiers, publications, sponsors, and other service providers in the western industry; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we welcome Miss Rodeo Canada 2026 to the Hospitality State:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby recognize Abbey Harty from Edam, Saskatchewan, as Miss Rodeo Canada 2026 and welcome her to Mississippi on the occasion of the Dixie National Livestock and Rodeo Show.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Abbey Harty, forwarded to the Commissioner of Agriculture and Commerce, and be made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
