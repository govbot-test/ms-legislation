2026 Regular Session

Senate Resolution 52

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING ALPHA PHI ALPHA FRATERNITY, INC., AND MISSISSIPPI ALPHA NETWORK ON THE OCCASION OF ITS 55TH ANNIVERSARY AS THE FIRST INTERCOLLEGIATE FRATERNITY FOR AFRICAN AMERICAN MEN.

     WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., founded on December 4, 1906, at Cornell University, stands as the first intercollegiate Greek-letter fraternity established for African American men and has long been committed to the principles of scholarship, fellowship, good character, and the uplifting of humanity; and

     WHEREAS, the presence of Alpha Phi Alpha in the State of Mississippi dates back to 1927, when Alpha Epsilon Lambda was chartered in Jackson, Mississippi, marking the beginning of the fraternity's organized alumni movement within the state; and

     WHEREAS, in 1949, the movement expanded into the collegiate community with the chartering of Gamma Upsilon Chapter on the campus of Tougaloo College, establishing a lasting legacy of leadership development and academic excellence among Mississippi's college students; and

     WHEREAS, from those early beginnings, Alpha Phi Alpha's footprint in Mississippi has grown to include 13 additional alumni chapters and nine additional college chapters, serving communities across the state through mentorship, civic engagement, voter education, scholarships, and advocacy; and

     WHEREAS, in 1971, Jackson, Mississippi, hosted the first district conference at the historic King Edward Hotel, reflecting the strength, organization, and statewide impact of Alpha men committed to advancing their communities; and

     WHEREAS, for fifty-five years, Mississippi Alpha Network has served as a unifying body for chapters throughout the state, fostering collaboration, leadership training, youth development initiatives, and programs addressing education, economic empowerment, health awareness, and social justice; and

     WHEREAS, Mississippi Alpha Network continues to build upon the fraternity's historic legacy of leadership exemplified by distinguished Alpha men who have shaped the civic, educational, legal, and political landscape of Mississippi and the nation:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby recognize Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., and Mississippi Alpha Network on the occasion of its 55th Anniversary as the first intercollegiate fraternity for African American men and extend our best wishes on this auspicious occasion.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be transmitted to the leadership of Mississippi Alpha Network for distribution to the chapters of Alpha Phi Alpha and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
