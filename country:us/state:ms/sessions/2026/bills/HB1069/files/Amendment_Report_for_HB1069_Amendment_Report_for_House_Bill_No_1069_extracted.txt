Amendment_Report_for_HB1069 Senate Amendments to House Bill No. 1069 TO THE CLERK OF THE HOUSE: THIS IS TO INFORM YOU THAT THE SENATE HAS ADOPTED THE AMENDMENTS SET OUT BELOW: AMENDMENT NO. 1 Amend by striking all after the enacting clause and inserting in lieu thereof the following: SECTION 1. (1)  There is hereby established the Wind-Tower Agricultural and Environmental Impact Study Committee.  The committee shall be composed of: (a)  The Commissioner of the Mississippi Department of Agriculture and Commerce, as the chair of the committee; (b)  The Executive Director of the Mississippi Department of Wildlife, Fisheries and Parks; (c)  The Executive Director of the Public Service Commission (PSC); (d)  One (1) representative from the Mississippi Agricultural Aviation Association (MAAA); (e)  One (1) member appointed by the Mississippi Farm Bureau Federation; (f)  One (1) member from the Mississippi Delta Council; (g)  One (1) senator from the Mississippi Senate, appointed by the Lieutenant Governor; and (h)  One (1) representative from the Mississippi House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the House. (2)  The committee shall: (a)  Study aviation incidents, wildlife-mortality data and farmland loss associated with wind-tower development; and (b)  Develop statewide placement and permitting standards, including a wildlife-buffer map modeled after regulatory framework enacted in Arkansas. (3)  Appointments to the study committee shall be made within thirty (30) days of the effective date of this act.  At the first meeting, the committee shall elect from among its membership a vice chairman and any other officers determined to be necessary, and shall adopt rules for transacting business and keeping records. (4)  A majority of the members of the study committee shall constitute a quorum.  The study committee shall develop and report to the Legislature its findings and recommendations on or before January 1, 2027, and repeat the reporting process annually. (5)  In the adoption of rules, resolutions and reports, and in the election of a vice chairman and any other officers determined to be necessary, an affirmative vote of a majority of the members present shall be required.  All members shall be notified in writing of all meetings, such notice shall be mailed at least five (5) days before the date on which a meeting is to be held.  Members of the study committee shall be reimbursed in the same manner as for attending out-of-session committee meetings. (6)  The PEER Committee shall provide the staff and other support necessary for the study committee to perform its duties. (7)  To effectuate the purposes of this act, any department, division, board, bureau, committee, institution or agency of the state, or any political subdivision thereof, shall, at the request of the chairman of the study committee, provide the facilities, assistance, information and data needed to enable the study committee to carry out its duties. (8)  (a)  For a period of one (1) year following the effective date of this act, any person or entity proposing to construct a new wind-tower facility in this state shall submit a Waterfowl Impact Assessment to the Mississippi Department of Wildlife, Fisheries and Parks for review and approval prior to commencing construction of such facility. (b)  The Waterfowl Impact Assessment shall evaluate potential impacts of the proposed wind-tower facility on migratory waterfowl, wildlife habitat, and related agricultural resources within the proposed project area and any recognized migratory corridors. (c)  The Mississippi Department of Wildlife, Fisheries and Parks may request additional information from the project proponent as necessary to complete its review of the Waterfowl Impact Assessment. (d)  The Mississippi Department of Wildlife, Fisheries and Parks shall establish reasonable procedures for the submission, review, and approval of Waterfowl Impact Assessments required under this subsection (8). (e)  No construction of a new wind-tower facility subject to this subsection (8) shall commence until the Mississippi Department of Wildlife, Fisheries and Parks has approved the Waterfowl Impact Assessment. (f)  The provisions of this subsection (8) shall apply only to proposals for the construction of new wind-tower facilities initiated after the effective date of this act. SECTION 2.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after its passage. Further, amend by striking the title in its entirety and inserting in lieu thereof the following: AN ACT TO CREATE THE WIND-TOWER AGRICULTURAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT STUDY COMMITTEE; TO PROVIDE THE COMPOSITION AND DUTIES OF THE STUDY COMMITTEE; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES. SS26\HB1069PS.J Amanda White Secretary of the Senate
