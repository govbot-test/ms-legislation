Senate Resolution 57

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING THE LAW ENFORCEMENT SERVICE AND COMMUNITY LEADERSHIP OF MBI SPECIAL AGENT MILTON WILLIAMS, JR., UPON HIS RECEIPT OF THE PRESTIGIOUS NORTHERN REGION SPECIAL AGENT CITATION.

     WHEREAS, born and raised in Lambert, Mississippi, Milton Williams, Jr., has dedicated his life to law enforcement service, leadership, and community impact; and

     WHEREAS, Milton is part of a historic law enforcement legacy.  He currently serves as the third Black Polygraph Examiner in the history of the Mississippi Department of Public Safety, an agency established in 1938, following in the footsteps of his father, Judge Milton R. Williams, Sr., who was the first Black Polygraph Examiner in the department's history; and

     WHEREAS, driven by a commitment to uplifting others, Milton founded Men United, Inc., a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization serving Quitman, Coahoma, and Tallahatchie counties in Mississippi.  Through mentoring, leadership development, character education, and S.T.E.A.M. programming, Men United has positively impacted thousands of young people and families.  The organization also hosts large-scale community initiatives, including annual food giveaways serving more than 3,000 families and toy giveaways reaching over 12,000 children, earning broad recognition and support; and

     WHEREAS, Milton also founded Women United, an organization focused on empowering young women through mentorship, education, and leadership development.  Together, these initiatives work to strengthen communities throughout the Mississippi Delta and beyond; and

     WHEREAS, his work has garnered support from nationally recognized figures, including Floyd Mayweather and Steve Harvey, and he has served as a mentor at the Steve & Marjorie Harvey Foundation Camp in Atlanta, Georgia; and

     WHEREAS, before dedicating himself fully to nonprofit leadership, Milton served in law enforcement with the Mississippi Highway Patrol, trained and interned with the United States Marshals Service, and graduated at the top of his class from the Mississippi Department of Corrections Training Academy in 2006.  He currently serves as a Special Agent with the Mississippi Bureau of Investigation, continuing a legacy of service within Mississippi's criminal justice system; and

     WHEREAS, Milton Williams, Jr., is a committed public servant, nonprofit leader, and advocate for community advancement, focused on creating lasting, meaningful change through leadership, service, and opportunity; and

     WHEREAS, we join the Department of Public Safety in expressing our appreciation for all MBI members' commitment, professionalism and service to our state, a debt which we can never repay:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend the law enforcement service and community leadership of MBI Special Agent Milton Williams, Jr., upon his receipt of the prestigious Northern Region Special Agent Citation and extend our best wishes to Milton and his family for future success.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Milton Williams, Jr., forwarded to the Commissioner of Public Safety and be made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
