MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative Bell (65th)

House Resolution 41

(As Adopted by House)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING MR. JOHN T. GRANT, JR., AS THE EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR OF THE CRICKET CELEBRATION BOWL, AS A HIGHLY RESPECTED BUSINESS LEADER IN ATLANTA AND AS ONE OF THE MOST INFLUENTIAL FIGURES IN BLACK COLLEGE ATHLETICS.

     WHEREAS, Mr. John T. Grant, Jr., the Executive Director of the Cricket Celebration Bowl and the MEAC/SWAC Challenge Kickoff, is an exemplary leader whose incredible work ethic and vision has catapulted him into being one of the most influential figures in Black college athletics, since joining ESPN Events in August 2015; and

     WHEREAS, since earning the title of executive director, Mr. Grant has been steadfast in his commitment to excellence and has grown the MEAC/SWAC Challenge Kickoff into a marquee annual event between college football programs from the Mid-Eastern Athletic and Southwestern Athletic conferences while also building the Cricket Celebration Bowl into the crown jewel of Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCU) football with champions of the MEAC and SWAC playing at Mercedes Benz Stadium each December in a nationally-televised ABC broadcast for the unofficial Black college football national championship; and

     WHEREAS, his unmatched execution of both events has helped to grow the national exposure of HBCU football and earn him the distinction of being named as one of the 20 Most Influential Black Figures in College Football by Sports Illustrated in 2023; and

     WHEREAS, he served as an elected member of the board until his selection as Chief Executive Officer in 2001, and also served as Chairman of the Atlanta Football Classic, the organization's largest fundraiser from 1999 through 2001, and in true Grant leadership fashion, the Atlanta Football Classic flourished setting record attendance and revenue levels each year and growing into one of the top three recurring sporting events in the state; and

     WHEREAS, with blood, sweat and tears, Mr. Grant has toiled in the Atlanta market for more than three decades in various prestigious positions including Chief Executive Officer of 100 Black Men of Atlanta, an organization that provides support and improves the quality of life for African Americans, particularly youth, through educational and economic opportunities in the Atlanta community; and

     WHEREAS, as evidence of his tenacity, just four years after moving to Atlanta in 1988, Mr. Grant became the youngest member to join the 100 Black Men of Atlanta at the age of 30, and was voted the organization's Man of the Year in 1989, after leading the organization's flagship program, Project Success; and

     WHEREAS, Mr. Grant's achievements have been recognized with numerous honors and awards, including the Atlanta Business Chronicle as one of the 100 Most Influential Atlantans, 100 Most Influential in Hospitality, 50 Most Influential Nonprofit Leaders, 50 Most Influential Leaders in Sports, and Who's Who in Sports (2016), the 2015 Atlanta Hospitality Hall of Fame, Atlanta's 2016 Men of the Year by Atlanta Tribune Magazine, the Atlanta Friendship Initiative, a nonprofit aimed at building relationships among Atlanta leaders to eliminate racism that he co-founded in 2016, and a recipient of Atlanta Business Chronicle's 2022 Visionary Award; and

     WHEREAS, he currently serves on several boards including the Atlanta Convention and Visitors Bureau, Atlanta Sports Council, and the Executive Committee of the National Wildlife Federation; and 

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to commend business leaders who raise the bar of economic development in a manner that benefits their community as well as the nation as a whole:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend Mr. John T. Grant, Jr., for his many years of exemplary leadership as Executive Director of the Cricket Celebration Bowl and the MEAC/SWAC Challenge Kickoff, and wish him many more years of continued success.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Mr. John T. Grant, Jr., and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
