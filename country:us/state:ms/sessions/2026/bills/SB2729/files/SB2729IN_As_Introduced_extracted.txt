Section 1:
SB2729 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Municipalities; Judiciary, Division B By: Senator(s) Blount Senate Bill 2729 AN ACT TO PROVIDE THAT IT SHALL BE UNLAWFUL WITHIN A MUNICIPALITY FOR AN OWNER OF A RETAIL ESTABLISHMENT PERMITTED UNDER SECTION 67-1-51(1)(D) TO ALLOW SUSTAINED, PROHIBITED NOISES BETWEEN THE HOURS OF 12:00 A.M. AND 12:00 P.M. ON THE PREMISES OF SUCH AN ESTABLISHMENT; TO PROVIDE CIVIL PENALTIES FOR THE FIRST THREE VIOLATIONS OF THIS SECTION; TO PROVIDE CRIMINAL FINES AND PENALTIES FOR THE FOURTH AND SUBSEQUENT VIOLATIONS OF THIS SECTION; TO PROVIDE THAT THIS SECTION SHALL BE LEGAL AND BINDING WITHIN THE JURISDICTION OF EACH MUNICIPALITY IN THIS STATE; TO AUTHORIZE THE GOVERNING AUTHORITY OF A MUNICIPALITY TO OPT OUT OF THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION THROUGH A VOTE OF THE GOVERNING AUTHORITY; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI: SECTION 1. (1)  As used in this section, (a)  The term "owner" means a person who owns a retail establishment permitted under Section 67-1-51(1)(d). (b)  The term "prohibited noises" means specific noises on the premises of an establishment permitted under Section 67-1-51(1)(d) interfering with enjoyment of property or public peace and comfort including, but not limited to, the following acts: (i)  The playing or operation of any sound equipment in such a manner, or with such volume as to disturb the peace, quiet, comfort, or repose of persons in any dwelling, apartment, hotel, hospital or other types of residence; (ii)  The use of any motor vehicle, motorcycle, or motorboat so out of repair which emits or creates loud, raucous, or rattling noises; (iii)  The discharge into the open air of the exhaust of any stationary steam engine, stationary internal combustion engine, or motorboat engine, except through a muffler, or other device which will effectively and efficiently prevent loud and raucous noises; (iv)  The discharge into the open air of the exhaust from any motor vehicle, motorcycle, or motorboat except through a muffler, or other device, which will effectively and efficiently prevent loud and raucous noises; (v)  The creation of loud and raucous noise on any street or property adjacent to any school, church, public library, or court which is in session or adjacent to any hospital; provided, that a conspicuous sign is located in or near such street or property indicating or designating that whatever the establishment or building at issue, be it a school, church, public library, court, or hospital, is adjacent thereto; or (vi)  The use of mechanical loudspeakers or sound equipment on or in motor vehicles or motorcycles for the purpose of advertising any show sale, or display of merchandise. (c)  The term "sustained" means continuing for a period of fifteen (15) minutes. (2)  It shall be unlawful for an owner to allow sustained, prohibited noises between the hours of 12:00 a.m. and 12:00 p.m. on the premises of an establishment permitted under Section 67-1-51(1)(d). (3)  (a)  A first violation of this section shall be punished by a civil penalty of Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00). (b)  A second violation of this section shall be punished by a civil penalty of One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00). (c)  A third violation of this section shall be punished by a civil penalty of Two Thousand Dollars ($2,000.00). (d)  A fourth violation of this section shall be punished by a criminal fine of Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) and by imprisonment in the county jail for thirty (30) days. (e)  A fifth or subsequent violation of this section shall be punished by a criminal fine not to exceed Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00), and by imprisonment in the county jail for not less than one hundred and eighty (180) days but not to exceed twelve (12) months. (f)  (i)  The civil penalties assessed under this section shall be deposited in the general operating fund of the county or municipality assessing the penalty. (ii)  The criminal fines assessed under this section shall be deposited in the State General Fund. (4)  (a)  This section shall be legal and binding within the jurisdiction of each municipality in this state. (b)  This section shall be legal and binding within the jurisdiction of each municipality in this state unless a municipality opts out of the provisions of this section through a vote by the governing authority of the municipality. (5) The fines and penalties provided in this section shall be in addition to any other fine or penalty provided by law. SECTION 2.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after July 1, 2026.
