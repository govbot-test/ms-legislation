SR 110 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Whaley, Chism Senate Resolution 110 A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE 2025 WEST UNION EAGLES BASEBALL TEAM AND COACH ASHLEY RUSSELL FOR WINNING THEIR FIRST-EVER MHSAA CLASS 1A STATE CHAMPIONSHIP. WHEREAS, the West Union High School Eagles won their first-ever MHSAA Class 1A Baseball State Championship in May 2025, sweeping Taylorsville in the championship series; and WHEREAS, West Union won the decisive Game 2 with a 4-1 score on May 23, 2025, following a 4-2 victory in Game 1.  Senior pitcher Grant Martin was named the series MVP; and WHEREAS, the Eagles, who finished the 2025 season with a 21-31 record, clinched the title with stellar defense, including a diving catch by center fielder Mack Wallis to end the game.  This marked the team's first baseball state title in program history; and WHEREAS, the Eagles are coached by Ashley Russell, who is in his 19th season; and WHEREAS, the 2025 West Union High School Eagles Championship Baseball Team include:  Jaxon Herod, Braxton Burks, Mack Wallis, Coleman Wallis, John Grey Morrison, Luke Conlee, Noah Johnson, David Roberts, Ganon Manning, Cohen Russell, Brody Brock, Jackson McGaha, Gray Lowe, Cooper Newby, Nate Lowe, Tripp Johnson, Tyson Henry, Jackson Dix, Caleb Mills, Reed Cooper, Scout Reaves and Jake Foreman, and Coach Ashley Russell; and WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize these student-athletes who have brought honor to their school, their community and to the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the 2025 West Union Eagles Baseball Team and Coach Ashley Russell for winning their first-ever MHSAA Class 1A State Championship and extend to the players, coaches, parents and sponsors our best wishes for future championships. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach Ashley Russell and West Union High School Principal Troy Trout for the team, forwarded to the Union County Board of Education and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
