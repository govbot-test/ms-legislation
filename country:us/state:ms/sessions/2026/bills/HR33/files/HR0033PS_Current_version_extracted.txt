HR 33 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Mangold House Resolution 33 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING MISS ABBEY HARTY UPON BEING CROWNED MISS RODEO CANADA 2026 AND RECOGNIZING HER DEDICATION TO THE SPORT OF RODEO AND WESTERN HERITAGE. WHEREAS, in December 2025, Miss Abbey Harty, a 22-year-old first-generation rodeo athlete from Edam, Saskatchewan, was crowned Miss Rodeo Canada 2026, a prestigious title that recognizes her dedication to the sport of rodeo and the promotion of western culture; and WHEREAS, raised in a rural ranching community, Abbey has been involved in rodeo and horsemanship for over a decade, competing in multiple rodeo associations across Western Canada; and WHEREAS, through her involvement in the sport, Abbey has developed strong values of perseverance, respect, and     community  principles that guide both her personal and professional life; and WHEREAS, Abbey currently serves as an executive member and Social Media Manager for the University of Saskatchewan College Rodeo Team, and she proudly holds a position as a board member of the Canadian College Rodeo Association, where she contributes to the promotion and advancement of collegiate rodeo across Canada; and WHEREAS, beyond the rodeo arena, Abbey has worked in the healthcare field for over five years and is currently employed at a long-term care facility, and she is pursuing a career in nursing at Saskatchewan Polytechnic, with aspirations of obtaining a master's degree in dementia studies; and WHEREAS, in 2018, Abbey's rodeo career was abruptly interrupted following a serious ATV accident that resulted in a partial arm amputation, and after undergoing multiple surgeries and years of rehabilitation, she rediscovered her resilience and determination, ultimately returning to the rodeo arena with a renewed perspective and deep appreciation for the sport; and WHEREAS, Abbey's journey in rodeo later led her to discover a passion for rodeo queening as well as her path began as the North Battleford Rodeo Princess and continued through her role as Miss Rodeo Agribition; and WHEREAS, now representing Canadian Professional Rodeo as Miss Rodeo Canada, Abbey is honored to share the traditions, values, and heritage of Canada's western way of life  an honor she carries with pride and purpose; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and honor outstanding individuals such as Miss Abbey Harty, who exemplifies dedication, passion, and leadership within the rodeo community and beyond: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Miss Abbey Harty upon being crowned Miss Rodeo Canada 2026 and extend best wishes for continued success in all her future endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Miss Abbey Harty, the Canadian Professional Rodeo Association and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
