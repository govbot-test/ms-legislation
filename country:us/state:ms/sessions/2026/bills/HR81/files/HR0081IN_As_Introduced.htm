MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representatives Taylor, Karriem, Osborne, Roberson, Sanders

House Resolution 81

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING JOHELEN "JOEY" WALKER ON HER HISTORIC ELECTION AS THE FIRST WOMAN AND AFRICAN AMERICAN TO SERVE AS TAX ASSESSOR AND COLLECTOR OF OKTIBBEHA COUNTY, MISSISSIPPI.

     WHEREAS, JoHelen "Joey" Walker, a lifelong resident of northeast Mississippi, made history in the Magnolia State in November of 2023 when she broke both gender and racial barriers by being the first woman and first African American elected to the Office of Tax Assessor and Collector of Oktibbeha County, Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, born and raised in the Starkville community, Ms. Walker, fondly called "Joey" by her family and friends, graduated from Starkville High School before furthering her education at East Mississippi Community College and Mississippi University for Women; and

     WHEREAS, although she grew up at a time when the majority of leadership roles in Oktibbeha County were overwhelmingly held by white men and opportunities for women, particularly African-American women, were severely limited, Ms. Walker developed an early understanding of the importance of economic empowerment and civic participation; and

     WHEREAS, Ms. Walker, with 10 years experience in Lowndes County and 14 years in Starkville as deputy clerk, followed her own sage advice that she frequently shares with young leaders to "be the change that you want to see" when she decided to enter the race for Tax Assessor and Collector; and

     WHEREAS, known for her deep commitment to public service, fairness and accountability, Ms. Walker considers taxation to be more about people than merely collecting payments for car tags and property taxes, and as tax assessor and collector, she is entrusted with overseeing property assessments, ensuring fair and equitable tax valuations, managing tax collection operations, and safeguarding public revenues that fund schools, infrastructure and essential services; and

     WHEREAS, in her first term at the helm of the tax assessor and collector's office, Ms. Walker, often described by her coworkers as meticulous, principled and deeply community oriented, has helped modernize office operations, increase public confidence, and ensure that all citizens of Oktibbeha County are treated with dignity and transparency; and

     WHEREAS, beyond the tax assessor and collector's office, Ms. Walker, a mother of two and grandmother of four, embodies the belief that public service is a calling grounded in trust, and she is an inspiration to young women and African Americans in Starkville and throughout Oktibbeha County as the epitome of perseverance and progress through hard work; and

     WHEREAS, Ms. Walker's tenure as the Oktibbeha County Tax Assessor and Collector has clearly demonstrated that inclusive leadership strengthens institutions, and by maintaining the highest professional standards, she has reshaped perceptions of who can lead in county government while successfully fulfilling her duties; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize outstanding Mississippians such as JoHelen "Joey" Walker, a trailblazing public servant whose legacy of principled leadership and encouragement for governmental participation among underrepresented communities is a shining example of barriers being challenged, leading to doors being opened for those willing to follow, a powerful symbol of progress in the Magnolia State's ever evolving civic landscape: 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate JoHelen "Joey" Walker on her historic election as the first woman and African American to serve as the Tax Assessor and Collector of Oktibbeha County, Mississippi, and extend sincerest wishes for continued success in all her future endeavors.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Oktibbeha County Tax Assessor and Collector JoHelen "Joey" Walker and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
