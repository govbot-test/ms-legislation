SR 17 (As Adopted by Senate) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) McCaughn, Chassaniol, DuPree, England, McLendon, Simmons (12th), Simmons (13th) Senate Resolution 17 (As Adopted by Senate) A RESOLUTION EXTENDING THE RECOGNITION OF THE MISSISSIPPI SENATE TO ART EDUCATION MENTOR AND ADVOCATE DOROTHY "DOTTIE" ARMSTRONG FROM NEWTON, MISSISSIPPI, AS THE RECIPIENT OF THE 2026 GOVERNOR'S ARTS AWARD FOR EXCELLENCE IN ARTS EDUCATION. WHEREAS, the Governor's Arts Awards program has announced its 2026 recipients, which include Art Education mentor and advocate Dorothy "Dottie" Armstrong from Newton, Mississippi, as the 2026 Governor's Arts Award recipient for Excellence in Arts Education; and WHEREAS, the Governor's Arts Awards program signifies the important relationship between government and the arts.  Established in 1988, the Governor's Arts Awards program recognizes individuals and organizations who have made noteworthy contributions to or achieved artistic excellence in Mississippi and is the most prestigious award for living artists in the state.  The Mississippi Arts Commission presents the awards each year in partnership with the Governor's Office.  The 38th Governor's Arts Awards ceremony is February 12, 2026, at The Westin Jackson.  The Mississippi Arts Commission (MAC) and the Governor's Office are pleased to announce four outstanding artists and one legacy arts organization as recipients of the 2026 Governor's Arts Awards; and WHEREAS, Dorothy Armstrong, affectionately known as "Miss Dottie," was an artist as a child, but she didn't know it.  Living on a dairy farm in Jacksonville, Florida, she entertained her six younger brothers and sisters with sock puppet shows complete with costumes and scenery.  But even though she loved to create, she didn't think she could do what she wanted the most: draw and paint.  When she went to high school, she would walk down the halls of Paxon High and peek into the doors of those wonderful art classes, wishing she could be there if only she knew how to draw and paint.  Naturally, once she started her teaching career as a high school English teacher, she couldn't stop drawing and painting.  She made posters and pictures to illustrate the lessons in her class.  Over the next several years, she enrolled in every art class available in her area.  Dr. Ted Alexander, Superintendent of Newton schools, suggested that she pursue a master's in art education at Mississippi College and start an art program at Newton High.  He had already written a grant for the elementary school, which brought in two full-time art teachers and substantial supplies. There was so much excitement as the spirit of art took over the town; and WHEREAS, Mrs. Armstrong kept the interest alive by maintaining art in the news through public art projects at schools, buildings, the hospital, businesses, and the local library.  Many pieces are gone now due to Hurricane Katrina's destruction and years of enduring the weather.  At one time, there were over a dozen murals outside, with others inside at the theatre and the depot; and WHEREAS, students had the opportunity to share and exhibit their artwork in art shows that served as cultural events for the community.  These events included live music, catered refreshments, and often television reporters; and WHEREAS, Dottie gave back to the community, but she kept her perspective, putting God and her family first in her life.  She was recognized as a Star Teacher several times and a Woman of the Year more than once by the Chamber of Commerce; and WHEREAS, after retiring in 1999, she expanded her private adult and children's classes.  She continues to keep her studio active and available.  The Secret Garden Art Studio is a happy place full of light, color, and creative energy; everyone is welcome and comfortable being there; and WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we pay tribute and express appreciation for the talent of a Mississippi art educator who has made Mississippi a better place and exemplifies the charitable and artistic traditions of our great state: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby extend the recognition of the Mississippi Senate to Art Education mentor and advocate Dorothy "Dottie" Armstrong from Newton, Mississippi, as the recipient of the 2026 Governor's Arts Award for Excellence in Arts Education and extend our congratulations to this outstanding art instructor on this auspicious occasion. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Mrs. Armstrong and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
