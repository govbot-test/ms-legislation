MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Senator(s) Wiggins, England, Seymour

Senate Resolution 77

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE PASCAGOULA HIGH SCHOOL PANTHERS BOYS BASKETBALL TEAM AND COACH LORENZO WRIGHT FOR THEIR FIRST STATE TITLE IN SCHOOL HISTORY.

     WHEREAS, Blake Nettles hit a go-ahead layup with 1:04 remaining as the Pascagoula High School Panthers claimed a 56-54 victory over Terry and won the MHSAA Boys Class 6A State Championship on March 5, 2026, at the Mississippi Coliseum; and

     WHEREAS, it marked the first state title in Pascagoula High School history for the Panthers (28-6) and featured Blake Nettles, 2026 Mississippi 6A Mr. Basketball; and

     WHEREAS, the game remained close throughout.  The teams were tied 10-10 after the first quarter and 28-28 at halftime.  Terry opened the second half with an 8-2 run to lead 36-30, but Pascagoula responded with six-consecutive points to tie it at 36-36.  The Bulldogs then went on a 9-1 spurt for a 45-37 advantage and took a 45-42 lead into the fourth quarter.  Pascagoula opened the fourth with a 9-4 run to go ahead 51-49, highlighted by Ireyion Harvey's three-pointer with 3:48 left.  Kamel Wiggins' layup gave Terry a 52-51 lead, but Nettles drove for a layup with 1:04 remaining to put Pascagoula back in front 53-52.  Tamir Boykin's layup with 22 seconds left extended the Panthers' lead to 55-52.  Wiggins made two free throws with 17 seconds remaining to cut the deficit to 55-54.  Evan Beck then made 1 of 2 free throws with 5 seconds left for a 56-54 advantage.  Terry's final shot bounced off the rim at the buzzer; and

     WHEREAS, "We felt like we had a state championship team the last two years, but this team did what it took and came to work every single day and got better.  This is awesome and something you can't take away from us.  First state championship in Pascagoula history.  Three years now, we finally did it," said Pascagoula High School Head Coach Lorenzo Wright, who spent 10 years at the helm of the Panthers; and

     WHEREAS, Adam Griffin scored a game-high 15 points to lead Pascagoula and was named the game's Most Valuable Player.  "We never lost faith in the guys and we made plays and played hard all game and played with heart.  Once one of my shots went in, it felt like they all were going in.  So it was a confidence boost.  We made history, so we're excited and going to celebrate this for a while," Griffin said; and

     WHEREAS, it took the Panther program almost 50 years since its last trip to the state finals to get back there, and about eight decades after first fielding a team, Pascagoula High School finally brought home that elusive state title.  A Panther team making its third-straight trip to the final four and its eighth appearance at the Big House in the past 15 seasons was finally able to head back home with a smile and a Gold Ball; and

     WHEREAS, the 2025-2026 Pascagoula High School Panthers Boys Basketball Team Roster:  Marcus Cooper, Blake Nettles, Christopher Rogers, Wiliam Rogers, Evan Beck, Michael Troche, Kyrin Nunnery, Travion Williams, Jaeden Blanchard, Tamir Boykin, Dominic Arevalo, Camerien Rogers and Derick Powe; and Head Coach Lorenzo Wright; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize these student-athletes who have won a first-ever championship to honor their school, their county and reflect positively on the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Pascagoula High School Panthers Boys Basketball Team and Coach Lorenzo Wright for their first state title in school history, and extend to the team, coaches, parents and sponsors our best wishes for future championships.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach Lorenzo Wright and Pascagoula Principal Milton Ray III for the team, forwarded to the Jackson County Board of Education, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
