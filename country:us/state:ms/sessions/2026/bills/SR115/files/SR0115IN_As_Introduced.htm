MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Senator(s) Rhodes, Kirby, Harkins, McCaughn

Senate Resolution 115

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE 2025 EAST RANKIN ACADEMY LADY PATRIOTS GIRLS CROSS COUNTRY TEAM AND COACH DAVID HOPPER FOR THEIR 5TH CONSECUTIVE MAIS 4A STATE CHAMPIONSHIP.

     WHEREAS, on October 29, 2025, the East Rankin Academy Lady Patriots Girls Cross Country Team won their fifth consecutive MAIS Class 4A State Championship at Choctaw Trails; and

     WHEREAS, the Lady Patriots finished off a very competitive season.  This win comes under the inaugural new classification system that pairs ERA with the much larger former 6A schools, collectively competing under the MAIS 4A grouping.  Finishing first overall, the Lady Patriots were named the MAIS 4A, Division I champion, followed by Madison Ridgeland Academy (second overall and named the Division II champion), Simpson Academy, and Oak Forest Academy, rounding out the top four teams; and

     WHEREAS, the East Rankin Academy 2025 Cross County State Championship Roster:  Jewel Baker, Caroline Thornton, Isabella McLeod, Alex Burkard, Chandler Hunt, Hadley Erickson, Emma Raines, Jensen Baker, Kate Bryant, Haley Headrick, Edie Tate, Arin Hawk, and Coach David Hopper; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize these student-athletes who have brought honor to their school, their community and to the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the 2025 East Rankin Academy Lady Patriots Girls Cross Country Team and Coach David Hopper for their 5th consecutive MAIS 4A State Championship and extend to the team, coaches, parents and sponsors our best wishes for future championships.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach David Hopper and East Rankin Academy Head of School Robyn Winstead for the team, forwarded to the East Rankin Academy Board of Trustees and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
