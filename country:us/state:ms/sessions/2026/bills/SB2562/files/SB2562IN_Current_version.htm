Senate Bill 2562

AN ACT TO CREATE THE MISSISSIPPI VETERANS PTSD IBOGAINE RESEARCH PILOT PROGRAM WITHIN THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH TO SUPPORT LAWFUL CLINICAL RESEARCH INVOLVING IBOGAINE WITH VOLUNTEER MISSISSIPPI VETERANS DIAGNOSED WITH PTSD, SUBJECT TO STRICT SAFEGUARDS AND FULL COMPLIANCE WITH APPLICABLE STATE AND FEDERAL LAW; TO REQUIRE THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH TO ADOPT AND DEVELOP A RESEARCH PROTOCOL; TO PROVIDE ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS FOR PARTICIPANTS; TO REQUIRE THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH TO PROVIDE AN ANNUAL REPORT TO THE LEGISLATURE; TO PROVIDE THAT THIS SECTION SHALL REPEAL BY OPERATION OF LAW ON JULY 1, 2028; TO AMEND SECTIONS 41-29-113 AND 41-29-115, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO RESCHEDULE IBOGAINE FROM SCHEDULE I TO SCHEDULE II OF THE UNIFORM CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES ACT; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

     BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI:

     SECTION 1.  (1)  The Legislature finds that post-traumatic stress disorder among Mississippi veterans is a significant public health concern and that controlled, ethically approved clinical research is necessary to evaluate investigational interventions.  The purpose of this section is to authorize a pilot program to support lawful clinical research involving ibogaine with volunteer Mississippi veterans diagnosed with PTSD, subject to strict safeguards and full compliance with applicable state and federal law.

     (2)  As used in this section, the following terms have the meaning ascribed herein unless the context requires otherwise:

          (a)  "Department" means the State Department of Health;

          (b)  "Ibogaine" means the controlled substance regulated under Section 41-29-115(f)(3).

          (c)  "Program" means the Mississippi Veterans PTSD Ibogaine Research Pilot Program established in subsection (3) of this section.

     (3)  The Mississippi Veterans PTSD Ibogaine Research Pilot Program is hereby created in the State Department of Health.  Subject to appropriation by the Legislature, the department administer the program.  The department may consult with the Department of Mental Health and the State Veterans Affairs Board in the administration of the program.  The department shall develop and adopt a research protocol for the program.

     (4)  (a)  The department shall limit participation to individuals who:

              (i)  Are veterans of the United States Armed Forces;

              (ii)  Are residents of the State of Mississippi;

              (iii)  Have a documented diagnosis of PTSD by a licensed physician, psychologist, or other appropriately licensed mental health professional; and

              (iv)  Meet medical screening and inclusion or exclusion criteria established in the approved research protocol.

          (b)  Participation by an individual shall be voluntary without any compensation paid to participants.

     (5)  (a)  No ibogaine may be possessed, manufactured, distributed, dispensed, or administered under the program unless done so under a research protocol that has received:

              (i)  Approval by an Institutional Review Board duly constituted under applicable federal law; and

              (ii)  Approval, registration, and licensing required under state and federal law, including any necessary authorization from the United States Food and Drug Administration and registration with the United States Drug Enforcement Administration.

          (b)  All activities under the Program shall be conducted in compliance with the Mississippi Uniform Controlled Substances Law and rules of the appropriate Mississippi licensing boards governing controlled-substance registration for research.

     (6)  (a)  Ibogaine obtained or used under this section shall be limited to use within an approved clinical research protocol and may not be prescribed, dispensed, or distributed for routine clinical treatment outside the program.

          (b)  Nothing in this section shall be construed to authorize retail sale of ibogaine or distribution or use of ibogaine outside the research protocol.

     (7)  A qualified research institution, principal investigator, licensed practitioner, research staff member, or program participant acting in accordance with approvals and authorizations required by subsection (5) shall be exempt from state criminal prosecution for possession, administration, dispensing, or distribution of ibogaine to the extent of such authorization.

     (8)  The department shall submit an annual report to the Governor, the Lieutenant Governor, and the Chairs of the Senate and House Committees with jurisdiction over public health.  Reports shall include:

          (a)  Number of participants enrolled and completing the protocol;

          (b)  Adverse events and safety outcomes (in aggregate, de-identified form);

          (c)  Outcome measures defined in the protocol (in aggregate, de-identified form);

          (d)  Program costs and funding sources; and

          (e)  Recommendations for continuation, modification, or termination.

     (9)  This section shall stand repealed on July 1, 2028.
