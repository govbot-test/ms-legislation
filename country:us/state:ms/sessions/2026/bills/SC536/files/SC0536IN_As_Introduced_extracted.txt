SC 536 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Norwood, Frazier Senate Concurrent Resolution 536 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION COMMENDING THE PARTICIPANTS IN THE CRIME VICTIMS SURVIVORS PROGRAM AND RECOGNIZING MARCH 11, 2026, AS "CRIME SURVIVORS SPEAK MISSISSIPPI DAY" AT THE MISSISSIPPI STATE CAPITOL. WHEREAS, on March 11, 2026, more than 150 crime victims and survivors from across the State of Mississippi will gather at the Mississippi State Capitol for "Crime Survivors Speak Mississippi Day 2026"; and WHEREAS, this gathering provides an opportunity for survivors to unite in healing, elevate their voices, and advocate for policies that advance safety and justice; and WHEREAS, Crime Survivors Speak Mississippi is committed to elevating survivor leadership and ensuring that public safety policies reflect the lived experiences of those most impacted by violence; and WHEREAS, crime affects individuals and communities throughout Mississippi, and survivors often face significant barriers to accessing trauma recovery services, financial support, and long-term stability; and WHEREAS, increased investment in trauma-informed services, victim compensation access, and community-based prevention strategies is essential to improving outcomes for survivors and families; and WHEREAS, survivors bring critical insight to discussions of public safety, victim services, and criminal justice reform and should be included as key stakeholders in policy development; and WHEREAS, many survivors do not receive the assistance they need and benefit from policies that strengthen prevention, rehabilitation, accountability, and healing-centered responses: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, that the Senate recognizes March 11, 2026, as "Crime Survivors Speak Mississippi Day" at the Mississippi State Capitol.    BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Senate commends the survivors, advocates, and community leaders participating in "Crime Survivors Speak Mississippi 2026" for their commitment to advancing healing, safety, and justice across the state. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Crime Survivors Speak Mississippi in recognition of this event, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
