SR 83 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Michel Senate Resolution 83 A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING MAY 28, 2026, AS "BLOOD CANCER AWARENESS DAY" IN MISSISSIPPI. WHEREAS, blood cancers, including leukemia, lymphoma, and myeloma are among the most commonly diagnosed cancers in the United States, with someone diagnosed approximately every three minutes; and WHEREAS, more than 1.3 million Americans are currently living with or are in remission from a blood cancer, and these diseases collectively account for approximately ten percent of all new cancer diagnoses nationwide each year; and WHEREAS, blood cancer diagnoses affect individuals of all ages, geographic regions, and socioeconomic backgrounds, including residents across the State of Mississippi; and WHEREAS, treatment for blood cancers often involves long-term therapy, repeated hospital visits, and ongoing medical monitoring, and many of these treatments are among the most expensive cancer therapies, creating significant financial hardship for patients and their families; and WHEREAS, financial strain is a documented barrier to treatment adherence and recovery, and patients frequently require assistance not only for medical expenses, but also for transportation, housing, and basic living needs during treatment; and WHEREAS, Blood Cancer United is a national nonprofit organization dedicated to accelerating blood cancer research while improving the quality of life for patients and families, having invested more than $2 billion in blood cancer research that has led to major advances in targeted therapies and immunotherapies; and WHEREAS, research supported by Blood Cancer United emphasizes early diagnosis, equitable access to care, sustained treatment support, and improved patient outcomes; and WHEREAS, public awareness of blood cancer symptoms and risks remains low, contributing to delayed diagnosis, while increased awareness leads to earlier detection, improved survival rates, and stronger patient and community support systems; and WHEREAS, during fiscal year 2025, Blood Cancer United served more than 600 patients and caregivers in Mississippi, providing 303 co-pay assistance awards totaling $1,354,600.00 and 300 patient financial assistance awards totaling $99,600.00 to help with non-medical expenses; and WHEREAS, nearly half of Mississippi patients receiving financial assistance from Blood Cancer United report annual household incomes of $40,000.00 or less, underscoring the critical need for continued support and resources; and WHEREAS, Mississippi is home to a PedAL Pediatric Acute Leukemia Master Trial, serving as a clinical trial screening site at the University of Mississippi Medical Center, expanding access to cutting-edge precision medicine research for children and young adults diagnosed with acute leukemia: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby recognize May 28, 2026, as "Blood Cancer Awareness Day" in the State of Mississippi, and encourage all citizens, communities, healthcare providers, and organizations to observe this day by increasing awareness, supporting patients and families, and promoting continued investment in research, early diagnosis, and access to life-saving care. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be transmitted to the Mississippi State Board of Health and be made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
