MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representatives Holloway (76th), Hulum

House Resolution 125

(As Adopted by House)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND EXPRESSING GRATITUDE TO MR. WILLIE W. MOSES, JR., FOR HIS MANY YEARS OF SERVICE TO ALCORN STATE UNIVERSITY AND THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI.

     WHEREAS, Mr. Willie W. Moses, Jr., former longtime director of the James L. Bolden Campus Union at Alcorn State University, has devoted his life to public service and higher education in the State of Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, a native of Magnolia, Mississippi, Mr. Moses, the third of ten children born unto Willie and Virgia Moses, graduated from Eva Gordon High School in 1969 before earning a bachelor's degree from Alcorn State University in 1973; and

     WHEREAS, the Moses family's lifelong legacy with Alcorn began long before Mr. Moses ever stepped foot on the Lorman campus when Mr. Moses' late Uncle O.W. Moses, who Mr. Moses greatly credits with "whatever minimum success I may have achieved," graduated from the university in 1950 and immediately began his career at the school which spanned 29 years, 25 of which were as the institution's Chief Financial Officer; and

     WHEREAS, with each of his nine siblings also attending Alcorn, purple and gold runs deep in the Moses family veins, making it virtually impossible for Mr. Moses not to have followed in his uncle's footsteps, and not long after completing his degree, Mr. Moses returned to the campus to begin a long and distinguished career of 45 years as the third of only four directors for the James L. Bolden Campus Union in its 63-year history; and

     WHEREAS, during Mr. Moses' many years as director of the student union - the very hub of campus activities - he genuinely enjoyed interacting with fellow employees and thousands of students, positively impacting their lives and helping them achieve their dreams and goals in life; and

     WHEREAS, the very epitome of "Always Alcorn!", Mr. Moses considers Alcorn State University to be a "natural resource in southwest Mississippi that has stood the test of time," and although he retired from his position in 2020, he proudly volunteers for the institution and remains committed to helping Alcorn and mankind reach their rightful place in history going into the future; and

     WHEREAS, it is most appropriate to recognize and commend outstanding Mississippians, especially those such as Mr. Willie W. Moses, Jr., whose years of dedicated and impeccable service at Alcorn State University has significantly enriched the Magnolia State as well as the individual lives of countless students, coworkers, fellow alumni, volunteers and his beloved university, bringing honor to the Alcorn family and the entire State of Mississippi:   

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and express sincere gratitude to Mr. Willie W. Moses, Jr., for his many years of service to Alcorn State University and the State of Mississippi and express heartfelt wishes for only the very best for many years to come.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Mr. Willie W. Moses, Jr., and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
