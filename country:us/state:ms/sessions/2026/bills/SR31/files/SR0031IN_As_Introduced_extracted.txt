SR 31 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Senator(s) Tate, Simmons (12th) Senate Resolution 31 A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING KAYTELYN HARRIS OF QUITMAN, MISSISSIPPI, AS THE STATE GAMES OF MISSISSIPPI 2025 FEMALE ATHLETE OF THE YEAR. WHEREAS, the State Games of Mississippi 2025 Ochsner Rush Health Athletes of the Year were recognized by the Mississippi State Legislature and Governor Tate Reeves during a visit to the Capitol in March; and WHEREAS, the 2025 Games featured more than 5,200 athletes competing in more than 40 events across 18 counties, and three athletes personified the ideals of the State Games in participation, sportsmanship, special achievements and positive attitudes; and WHEREAS, the three athletes, who each competed in the 2025 games, were unique contributors to their individual sports, and thus were named Ochsner Rush Health Athletes of the Year:  Kaytelyn Harris of Quitman (Female Athlete of the Year), Aubrey Lynn Atchison of Biloxi (Youth Athlete of the Year), and Daniel Wile of Meridian (Male Athlete of the Year); and WHEREAS, "It is a great honor to be recognized by our statewide lawmakers and Governor Reeves," said Missye Dozier, Executive Director of the Games.  "State Games holds sporting events throughout the state, creating multiple opportunities for amateur athletes and contributing to the economies of each city in which they are held"; and WHEREAS, Kaytelyn Harris of Quitman has only been playing disc golf for a year and a half, but she's already earned back-to-back Gold Medals in the State Games of Mississippi and placed in ten tournaments across the region; and WHEREAS, this year, she reached a personal milestone by shooting her best tournament round at just four strokes over par.  She said the biggest challenge isn't physical, but mental: "I work at getting out of my head and just throwing.  Each round, I make a conscious effort to just have fun and enjoy the sport, no matter the score"; and WHEREAS, as a single mom to three young children, Kaytelyn found disc golf during a difficult time  and with it, a supportive community.  "She's out there every chance she gets  rain or shine.  Her dedication and improvement have been incredible to watch," said Disc Golf Commissioner Bruce Bullock; and WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize these athletes who have brought honor to their city, their community and to the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Kaytelyn Harris of Quitman, Mississippi, as the State Games of Mississippi 2025 Female Athlete of the Year, and extend to Kaytelyn and her family our best wishes for future success. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Kaytelyn Harris and her family, forwarded to the State Games of Mississippi, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
