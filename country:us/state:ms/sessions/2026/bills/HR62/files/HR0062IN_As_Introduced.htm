MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representatives Boyd (19th), Turner, Bell (21st), Watson

House Resolution 62

A RESOLUTION MOURNING THE LOSS AND COMMEMORATING THE LIFE, LEGACY AND SERVICE OF FORMER STATE REPRESENTATIVE EDWIN JEROME "JERRY" WILBURN, AND EXPRESSING DEEPEST SYMPATHY TO HIS FAMILY, FRIENDS AND COMMUNITY UPON HIS PASSING.

     WHEREAS, it is written in Ecclesiastes 3:1 that "To everything there is a season and a time for every purpose under the Heaven," and as such, on January 13, 2026, the immaculate author and finisher of our souls' destiny summoned the mortal presence of the distinguished gentleman from Itawamba, former State Representative Edwin Jerome "Jerry" Wilburn, to eternal rest, rendering great sorrow and loss to his family, friends and community; and

     WHEREAS, the State of Mississippi and, particularly, the City of Mantachie lost a loving husband, father, neighbor, fierce advocate, loyal friend, community treasure and a real character with the passing of Representative Wilburn, and there is now a hush in our hearts as we pay our respects to the memory of one who has been called to join that heavenly caravan; and

     WHEREAS, Representative Wilburn, the youngest of five children, was born on September 18, 1940, to Elijah and Lula Wren Wilburn on the west side of the Tombigbee River, and he remained a proud Itawamba Countian for his entire life; and

     WHEREAS, a member of the Mantachie High School Athletic Hall of Fame and the Itawamba Community College Hall of Fame, Representative Wilburn was a standout on the football field in his high school years, and he went on to play football at Itawamba Community College before attending Mississippi State University; and

     WHEREAS, Representative Wilburn served in the Mississippi National Guard with unwavering love for both his state and his country, and he represented the great people of his beloved Itawamba County in the Mississippi House of Representatives; and

     WHEREAS, known for his authenticity, comedic wit and idiosyncratic rhetorical style, Representative Wilburn established a legacy as an inimitable leader during his 20 years in the Mississippi Legislature; and

     WHEREAS, Representative Wilburn sponsored legislation to create the North Mississippi Regional Center in Oxford, which has provided a home for hundreds of children and adults with physical and mental disabilities for over 50 years, and the Center recently recognized his advocacy, vision and unwavering commitment to improving the lives of others by dedicating the Jerry Wilburn Memorial Bridge and Service Road on campus to him; and

     WHEREAS, countless individuals and families were impacted by Representative Wilburn's contributions to his community, both as a legislator and as a compassionate individual who used his annual "13th check" from the state retirement system to fund the Wilburn Family Foundation; and

     WHEREAS, remembering his childhood years when he did not always have shoes or fancy clothes, Representative Wilburn used the Wilburn Family Fund to provide shoes and clothing at Christmas for needy children in the Mantachie community for the last 29 years, most recently furnishing shoes and clothing for 92 kids on Christmas; and

     WHEREAS, Representative Wilburn has cemented his place in the annals of the great Magnolia State's history through his generosity, sincerity and determination to help the underdogs and the downtrodden, and though we must go forward now alone, we carry with us the lessons he taught us - the value of mirth, love for our neighbor, and empathy, especially for those who are the least among us; and

     WHEREAS, absent from the body but present with the Lord, Representative Wilburn has traversed the river of life to join in heavenly reunion with his parents and his sisters, Lavelle Wilburn Hinkle, Jettie Pearl Wilburn Bean and Edna Ellen "Toots" Wilburn Wesson, as well as his oldest brother he never met, Quentin Wilburn; and

     WHEREAS, the memory of Representative Wilburn's unconditional love for his family will continue to refresh the hearts and spirits of his survivors:  his wife of 61 years, Margaret Ann Silas; daughters, Bronson Prochaska (John) and Millie Wood (Chuck); grandchildren, Matthew Prochaska (Catherine), Will Wood (JoyBeth) and Carter Wood; great-grandchildren, Eli Prochaska, Margaret Prochaska and Declan Wood; and a host of extended family members, friends and neighbors; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and honor the lives of selfless Mississippians who served the state with distinction, especially those such as Representative Wilburn, one we proudly call our own, whose life's light illuminated the path of hope for all he encountered along this earthly sojourn:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby mourn the loss and commemorate the life and legacy of former State Representative Edwin Jerome "Jerry" Wilburn, and express deepest sympathy to his family and friends upon his passing.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to the family of Representative Wilburn and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
