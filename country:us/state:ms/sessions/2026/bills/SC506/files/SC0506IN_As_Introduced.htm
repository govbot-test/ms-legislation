Senate Concurrent Resolution 506

A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION HONORING THE RICH HISTORICAL CONNECTION AND LONGSTANDING PARTNERSHIP BETWEEN THE PROVINCE OF QUÉBEC AND THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI.

      WHEREAS, Québec and Mississippi share a rich historical connection dating back to the late 17th Century, when Québec-born explorer Pierre Le Moyne d'Iberville led a French expedition that established the first European settlement in what is now Mississippi, at Fort Maurepas near present-day Ocean Springs in 1699; and

      WHEREAS, d'Iberville's arrival marked the beginning of French colonial influence in the region, laying the foundation for cultural and linguistic ties that continue to be celebrated today, including through annual commemorations in Ocean Springs that honor this shared heritage; and

      WHEREAS, the legacy of French exploration and settlement in Mississippi, rooted in Québec's history, contributes to the state's diverse cultural identity and offers opportunities for educational, historical, and tourism initiatives; and

      WHEREAS, the Province of Québec has maintained a permanent presence in the Southern United States since 1978, and both Québec and Mississippi are member jurisdictions of the Southeast United States-Canadian Provinces (SEUS-CP) alliance, which fosters economic and cultural collaboration; and

      WHEREAS, Québec serves as a diverse supplier to Mississippi's key industries, including the automotive sector, health and life sciences, machinery, and aluminum; and

      WHEREAS, Québec provided more than half of all U.S. imports of primary aluminum in 2023, positioning itself as a significant supplier to Mississippi's automotive and manufacturing industries; and

      WHEREAS, automobiles and automotive components manufactured in Mississippi account for approximately 75% of Québec's imports from the state, demonstrating a mutually beneficial trade relationship; and

      WHEREAS, the enduring economic, historical, and cultural ties between Québec and Mississippi continue to strengthen through trade, investment, and shared values; and

      WHEREAS, it is abundantly fitting and proper that this remarkable partnership be appropriately recognized during this special time:

      NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING THEREIN, That the members of this Legislature do hereby honor the longstanding and fruitful partnership between the Province of Québec and the State of Mississippi, and express their commitment to continued collaboration in areas of mutual interest.

      BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be transmitted to the Premier of Québec, the Québec Government Office in Atlanta, the Governor of the State of Mississippi, the Mississippi Division of Tourism, and other appropriate officials as a token of respect and goodwill, and be made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
