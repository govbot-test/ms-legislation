HR 80 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Calvert House Resolution 80 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE LAMAR SCHOOL "RAIDERS" BOYS SOCCER TEAM AND HEAD COACH RUSTY WARDEN UPON WINNING THE 2025-2026 MISSISSIPPI ASSOCIATION OF INDEPENDENT SCHOOLS CLASS 4A DIVISION II STATE CHAMPIONSHIP. WHEREAS, the Lamar School "Raiders" Boys Soccer Team achieved a remarkable season culminating in a decisive 4-1 victory over Heritage Academy in the 2025-2026 Mississippi Association of Independent Schools (MAIS) Class 4A Division II State Championship at Madison-Ridgeland Academy in Madison, Mississippi; and WHEREAS, the Raiders demonstrated exceptional determination, teamwork and athletic excellence throughout the championship contest, striking early when Logan Cross delivered a well-placed ball upfield to Blake Harris, who maneuvered skillfully past a defender and delivered the game's opening goal to give Lamar a 1-0 advantage in the first half; and WHEREAS, continuing their offensive momentum, Harris later advanced the ball to Austin Wichman, who capitalized on a one-on-one opportunity against the Heritage goalkeeper to score Lamar's second goal and extend the Raiders' lead to 2-0 before halftime; and WHEREAS, after Heritage Academy narrowed the score to 2-1 before the break, the Raiders returned to the field determined to secure the title, and Wichman once again demonstrated his offensive prowess early in the second half by drawing out the goalkeeper and placing a well-executed shot into the back of the net to widen Lamar's lead; and WHEREAS, with the championship firmly within reach and time winding down, senior Benson Oates delivered the final blow by converting a penalty kick with precision to give the Raiders their fourth goal of the match and seal the commanding 4-1 victory; and WHEREAS, the championship victory represents the culmination of years of dedication and perseverance by the Lamar program, particularly for a senior class that remained committed through seasons of adversity before achieving the ultimate goal of a state championship; and WHEREAS, the Raiders have been superbly guided by Head Coach Rusty Warden, whose leadership, mentorship and commitment to the growth of his players both as athletes and as young men has been instrumental in the team's development and championship success; and WHEREAS, the 2025-2026 Lamar School Raiders Boys Soccer Team roster includes:  Gavin Aull, Luke Morrow, Benson Oates, Logan Cross, Emmanuel Donkor, Carter Hill, Briggs Calvert, Wesley Cosby, Weston Cox, Will Duncan, Matthew Hall, Blaise Hannington, Blake Harris, Wyatt Purdy, Landon Smith, Alex Turnipseed, Abram Warden, Austin Wichman, Joseph Donkor, Holt Haarala, Mason Hill, Winn Leigh, Maddox Mims, Joe Morrow, Max Myers, Jackson Spitzkeit, Christopher Hall, Hayes Lisi, Jax Confait, Anderson Honeycutt, Clarke Newlin, Lucas Purdy, John Quedado, Jase Shedd and Arshdeep Singh; and WHEREAS, the success of the Raiders is also a reflection of the leadership and support provided by the Lamar School administration and athletics program, including Head of School Leigh Ann Ballou, Athletic Director Matt Boone and Athletic Trainer Alan Tingle, whose commitment to excellence in athletics and academics continues to inspire student-athletes to reach their highest potential; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend exceptional student-athletes whose dedication, discipline and teamwork bring honor to their schools, their communities and the State of Mississippi: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Lamar School "Raiders" Boys Soccer Team and Head Coach Rusty Warden upon winning the 2025-2026 MAIS Class 4A Division II State Championship, and extend best wishes for continued success in all their future athletic and academic endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Head Coach Rusty Warden on behalf of the Lamar School "Raiders" Boys Soccer Team, Head of School Leigh Ann Ballou, Athletic Director Matt Boone, and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
