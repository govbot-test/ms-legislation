MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Senator(s) Tate, Hickman, Blount, McCaughn, DeLano, Hopson, England

Senate Resolution 42

A RESOLUTION EXTENDING THE CONDOLENCES OF THE MISSISSIPPI SENATE TO THE FAMILY AND FRIENDS OF MRS. DONNA JILL JOHNSON OF MERIDIAN, MISSISSIPPI, THE FIRST REPUBLICAN CIRCUIT CLERK ELECTED IN THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, WHO SERVED WITH INTEGRITY FOR 32 YEARS.

     WHEREAS, it is with sadness that we join the family and friends of Mrs. Donna Jill Johnson of Meridian, Mississippi, who passed away on February 5, 2026, at the age of 80; and

     WHEREAS, Donna Jill was the very first Republican Circuit Clerk elected in Mississippi, serving the people of Lauderdale County and the State of Mississippi with integrity and competence for over 32 years; and

     WHEREAS, she possessed a wonderful sense of humor, which her numerous friends and colleagues enjoyed daily.  She certainly lived life to the fullest and cherished time with family and friends and had an undying love of animals.  She loved Meridian and the people of Meridian; and

     WHEREAS, Donna Jill is survived by her loving sister and best friend, Lynne J. Grimes; brother, Duane Johnson; a host of nieces, nephews, and numerous cousins, in both Mississippi and Texas; as well as special friends, Jeff Tate, Ann Watts, Ann O'Leary, and Dianne Hackett.  She is preceded in death by her parents, Hermon Gray Johnson, Jr., and Dorothy Duane Johnson; brother, Gray and sister-in-law, Peggy Joyce Johnson; and

     WHEREAS, we pay tribute and cherish fondly the memory of this most public-spirited citizen of Mississippi with enormous civic energy who will be missed:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby extend the condolences of the Mississippi Senate to the family and friends of Mrs. Donna Jill Johnson of Meridian, Mississippi, the first Republican Circuit Clerk elected in the State of Mississippi, who served with integrity for 32 years, and offer our tribute to her memory.
