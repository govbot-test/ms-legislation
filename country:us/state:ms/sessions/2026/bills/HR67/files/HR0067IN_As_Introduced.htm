MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative James-Jones

House Resolution 67

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING MR. DEMARCUS "SHOUTOUT 2 D" FORD UPON HIS MUSICAL ARTISTRY AND SUCCESS AS AN AMBASSADOR AND CULTIVATOR OF THE SOUTHERN SOUL SOUNDSCAPE.

     WHEREAS, Mr. DeMarcus Ford, affectionately known throughout the Southern Soul and Blues community as "Shoutout 2 D Ford" or simply "D Ford," stands as a gifted musician, producer and songwriter whose artistry is deeply rooted in the fertile musical soil of the Mississippi Delta; and

     WHEREAS, born in Indianola, Mississippi, and educated at John F. Kennedy Memorial High School in Mound Bayou, Mississippi, Mr. Ford was raised amidst the enduring legacy of blues, soul and storytelling that has long defined this region as the cradle of authentic American music; and

     WHEREAS, now residing in Belzoni, Mississippi, he continues to carry forward that rich cultural inheritance, shaping and preserving the Southern Soul soundscape through his creative excellence and disciplined craftsmanship; and

     WHEREAS, inspired at the age of 16 by his brother-in-law, Terrence Liddell, to begin playing the guitar, Mr. Ford further refined his distinctive style under the influence of the legendary Delta blues icon Muddy Waters, whose innovative guitar sound helped cultivate Mr. Ford's appreciation for the raw and honest expression of Mississippi blues; and

     WHEREAS, drawing inspiration from Mississippi hip-hop pioneer David Banner, particularly in the art of beat-making and personal branding, Mr. Ford created his now-recognizable producer tag, "Shoutout 2 D Ford," which has become a signature hallmark stamped across his productions and widely recognized throughout the Southern Soul and trail-ride music communities; and

     WHEREAS, as a skilled multi-instrumentalist proficient in guitar, bass and piano, Mr. Ford possesses the rare ability to compose and produce full, authentic Southern Soul arrangements that resonate in juke joints, dance halls and trail rides across the South, preserving a cultural sound that reflects both heritage and innovation; and

     WHEREAS, his production credits include notable works such as "Keep On Stepping" by Mike Clark, Jr., "Welcome to the Country" by Arthur Young, "Cowgirl Trailride" by Tonio Armani, "Juke Joint Love" by Simply Wayne, and "My Type of Carrying On" by Nephew Jones, along with hundreds of additional recordings that continue to energize audiences and elevate artists throughout the region; and

     WHEREAS, beyond his professional achievements, Mr. Ford is a devoted husband to his wife, Felicia, and a proud father to Zoey, Aalayah, Jayda, Jordan and Ziyon, embodying the values of faith, family and community as a faithful member of Strangers Home Church in Isola, Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, guided by his abiding belief that "Where words fail, music speaks," Mr. Ford has used his gifts not only to entertain but to uplift communities, tell authentic Southern stories and safeguard the cultural rhythm of Southern Soul for future generations; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize citizens of Mr. Ford's esteemed caliber, whose talent and integrity reflect honor upon the State of Mississippi and who serve as ambassadors of its cultural legacy through excellence in their chosen craft:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Mr. DeMarcus "Shoutout 2 D" Ford upon his musical artistry and success as an ambassador and cultivator of the Southern Soul soundscape, and extend sincere appreciation for his contributions to the preservation and advancement of Mississippi's rich musical heritage.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Mr. DeMarcus "Shoutout 2 D" Ford and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
