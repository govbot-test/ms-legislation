MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Judiciary, Division B

By: Senator(s) Younger

Senate Bill 2328

AN ACT TO BAN THE INTENTIONAL RELEASE OF BALLOONS FILLED WITH LIGHTER-THAN-AIR GASES OUTDOORS; TO ALLOW FOR THE INTENTIONAL RELEASE OF BALLOONS UNDER CERTAIN CIRCUMSTANCES; TO PROVIDE PENALTIES; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

     BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI:

     SECTION 1.  (1)  The Legislature finds that the release of balloons into the atmosphere poses a danger and nuisance to the environment, particularly to wildlife and marine animals.

     (2)  For the purpose of this section, the term "balloon" means a sac made from rubber, latex, polychloroprene, nylon, mylar, foil or any other material which is or can be filled with lighter-than-air gases.  The term "balloon" includes a floating sky lantern.

     (3)  It is unlawful to intentionally release a balloon outdoors, to organize the release of a balloon outdoors or to intentionally cause a balloon to be released outdoors, except under any of the following circumstances:

          (a)  A balloon is released on behalf of a governmental agency or pursuant to a governmental contract for scientific or meteorological purposes;

          (b)  A hot air balloon is recovered after launching; or

          (c)  A balloon is released and remains indoors.

     (4)  Any person who violates this section shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor and be fined an assessment of Twenty-Five Dollars ($25.00) per balloon released.

     SECTION 2.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after July 1, 2026.
