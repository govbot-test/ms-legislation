Senate Resolution 131

A RESOLUTION DECLARING THAT APRIL 2, 2026, IS "DRIVE TO ARRIVE DAY" ENCOURAGING STUDENTS, FAMILIES AND COMMUNITIES ACROSS THE STATE TO COMMIT TO DISTRACTION-FREE DRIVING.

     WHEREAS, Mississippi continues to rank among the states with the highest rates of teen driving fatalities.  Last year alone, 88 teenagers lost their lives in car crashes.  Each of these lives represents a future cut short and families forever changed.  These are not just numbers; they are classmates, friends, and neighbors; and

     WHEREAS, Mattie Grace Morris, Miss Mississippi Volunteer 2026, has spent the past year working alongside the Mississippi Highway Patrol as the official spokesperson of the D.R.I.V.E. campaign, speaking with students across the state about safe driving.  Through these conversations, she has seen firsthand that distracted driving is a serious and growing issue, particularly among young drivers; and

     WHEREAS, because of this, Mattie Grace believes the state has an opportunity to make a meaningful difference by raising awareness and uniting communities around safe driving.  April is nationally recognized as Distracted Driving Awareness Month, making it an ideal time for Mississippi to take action:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby declare April 2, 2026, to be "Drive to Arrive Day" in Mississippi in observance of National Distracted Driving Awareness Month encouraging students, families and communities across the state to commit to distraction-free driving.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Mattie Grace Morris as the official spokesperson of the D.R.I.V.E. campaign with the Mississippi Highway Patrol, transmitted to the Commissioner of Public Safety for appropriate distribution, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
