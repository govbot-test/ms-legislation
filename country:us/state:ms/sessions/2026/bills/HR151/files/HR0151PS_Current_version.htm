MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representative Summers

House Resolution 151

(As Adopted by House)

A RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING AND COMMENDING TATYANA ALI UPON BEING FEATURED BY THE WOMEN'S FOUNDATION OF MISSISSIPPI FOR VISIONARIES 2026:  WOMEN WHO MOVE MISSISSIPPI IN HONOR OF HER OUTSTANDING ADVOCACY WORK IN MATERNAL HEALTH.

     WHEREAS, on March 27, 2026, the Women's Foundation of Mississippi is hosting its signature annual fundraising event, Visionaries 2026:  Women Who Move Mississippi, celebrating exceptional leaders whose vision and impact are transforming families and improving outcomes for women and girls across the state; and

     WHEREAS, actress, singer, writer, entrepreneur, activist, Broadway-trained performer, Harvard University graduate, and five-time NAACP Image Award winner, Tatyana Ali, is being featured for Visionaries 2026 in recognition of her extraordinary advocacy for reproductive justice and remarkable efforts to ensure women receive loving, comprehensive reproductive and maternal health care; and

     WHEREAS, with an illustrious career that spans 40 years, Tatyana has appeared in numerous television, film and theatrical projects, including Sesame Street, the iconic comedy series The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air, The Young and the Restless, Disney's Glory Road and a growing collection of holiday and dramatic films for Lifetime TV, and she currently appears as a recurring character on ABC's award-winning hit comedy series, Abbott Elementary; and

     WHEREAS, Tatyana, a standout on both screen and stage, also stunned audiences in the Pulitzer Prize winning production of Fences on Broadway; and

     WHEREAS, flexing her creative abilities in her off-screen pursuits, Tatyana has produced many projects for television and digital networks, including the first original scripted program on the TV One Network, Love That Girl!; and

     WHEREAS, Tatyana has earned acclaim as a recording artist and received a gold record for her 1998 debut album Kiss the Sky, featuring the platinum single "Daydreamin'" and "Boy You Knock Me Out," as well as a Grammy Award in the Best Musical Album for Children category for her recording of "Precious Wings" for The Adventures of Elmo in Grouchland soundtrack; and

     WHEREAS, with diverse musical releases that include the independent EP Hello and the live music Christmas EP All I Have, Tatyana's music has inspired, entertained and connected listeners near and far, and she has performed for audiences across the globe, touring with bands like *NSYNC and Backstreet Boys; and

     WHEREAS, Tatyana authored the children's book Aszi and the Octopus, captivating readers with a story centering her son's love of the ocean and the imaginative power of black boy joy; and

     WHEREAS, using her skills as a public speaker to amplify issues and motivate voters, Tatyana served as a surrogate for President Barack Obama during his 2008 and 2012 presidential campaigns, speaking to communities across America about voting as an imperative and promoting the Affordable Care Act; and

     WHEREAS, after facing her own traumatic hospital birth with her first baby boy, Tatyana channeled her advocacy work to champion Black maternal health, steadfast in her determination to find answers to improve outcomes for mothers and families, and she has drawn upon her talent for storytelling to mobilize others into action, penning persuasively written op-ed pieces and using her public platform to make a strong case for change across the country; and

     WHEREAS, Tatyana detailed the experience of her life-threatening delivery in her personal testimony before the United States Congress, and through her firsthand attestation of the lasting impact of childbirth trauma, she was able to give voice to countless others with similar stories and shine a spotlight on the nation's Black maternal health crisis; and

     WHEREAS, in further support of the movement for reproductive justice, Tatyana serves as the founder and CEO of Baby Yams, a baby quilt company that donates 10% of its proceeds to grants and scholarships for Black and Indigenous birth workers; and

     WHEREAS, the Baby Yams Quilt Project honors the legacy of each individual's history, struggles and triumphs, which seamlessly come together - one stitch at a time - to beautifully create a communal tapestry, and with each thoughtfully designed quilt, Baby Yams spreads a message of possibility and inspiration, providing cover for a community seeking to uplift and heal one another in a powerful show of unity, strength and collective support; and

     WHEREAS, in partnership with organizations on the ground and through generous donations, Baby Yams has made significant contributions to increasing access to a more equitable birth environment, empowering Black and Indigenous birth workers and uplifting their work as they continue their vital efforts to support families; and

     WHEREAS, Tatyana, who resides in California with her husband and two sons, has spent years tirelessly working to support other mothers like herself and families across the United States, and it is most fitting to honor her exemplary efforts through Visionaries 2026, which highlights innovative approaches to education, wellness, economic stability and community strength while mobilizing resources to advance access to opportunity for all Mississippians; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize exceptional individuals, especially those such as Tatyana Ali, whose extraordinary advocacy work in maternal health and passion for ensuring each person has access to a system of care that is culturally-rooted, community-led and deeply relational has made an indelible impact on women and families across the nation and, particularly, on those who live in this great Magnolia State:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby recognize and commend Tatyana Ali upon being featured by the Women's Foundation of Mississippi for Visionaries 2026:  Women Who Move Mississippi in recognition of her outstanding advocacy work in maternal health, and extend best wishes for continued success in all her future endeavors.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Tatyana Ali and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
