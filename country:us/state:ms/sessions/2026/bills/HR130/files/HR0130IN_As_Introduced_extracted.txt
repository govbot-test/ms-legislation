HR 130 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Shanks House Resolution 130 A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CELEBRATING THE 70TH ANNIVERSARY OF THE ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT PARTNERSHIP BETWEEN THE CITY OF GREENWOOD AND LEFLORE COUNTY THAT LED TO THE ESTABLISHMENT OF THE GREENWOOD-LEFLORE INDUSTRIAL BOARD. WHEREAS, on March 31, 1956, the Mississippi Legislature approved House Bill 963, authorizing "the board of supervisors of Leflore County and the governing authorities of the City of Greenwood to employ and pay the salary of an industrial engineer or similar expert for advertising purposes, in which they may act separately or jointly," thereby laying the legislative foundation for coordinated economic development efforts in Greenwood and Leflore County; and WHEREAS, this legislative action led to the establishment of the Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Board, one of the oldest public economic development organizations in the State of Mississippi, created to promote industrial growth, job creation, and investment in the region; and WHEREAS, over the past seventy years, the partnership between the City of Greenwood and Leflore County has fostered sustained economic growth and diversification, resulting in the successful recruitment and expansion of manufacturing and industry, including AerCap Materials, America's Catch, Baldwin Piano, Balkamp, Coburn Supply, Conmar, Ferguson Machine Tools, Hickok-Waekon, Heartland Catfish, Hydra Tool, Irvin Industries, John-Richard, Milwaukee Tools, National Picture Frame, Saylor Wheel, Staplcotn, Takata, Upchurch Services, Viking Range, Wurlitzer, and other prominent employers; and WHEREAS, the Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Board, comprised of seven members appointed to oversee development within the 2,200-acre Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Park located at the intersection of Highway 82 West and Highway 49 South in Greenwood, continues to serve as a central driver of regional economic activity and job creation; and WHEREAS, the organization's scope was further expanded in 1980, with the creation of a 501(c)(6) non-profit entity, the Greenwood-Leflore Economic Development Foundation, Inc., enabling broad-based membership, private fundraising, and enhanced capacity to pursue economic development initiatives; and WHEREAS, in 1990, the Foundation formally changed its name to the Greenwood-Leflore-Carroll Economic Development Foundation, Inc., reflecting its expanded service area to include Carroll County and demonstrating an ongoing commitment to regional partnership and prosperity; and WHEREAS, today the Greenwood-Leflore-Carroll Economic Development Foundation is governed by a 27-member board of directors, with a seven member Executive Committee, providing strategic leadership to advance economic opportunity and industrial recruitment initiatives across the region; and WHEREAS, in 2025, the Greenwood-Leflore-Carroll Economic Development Foundation formalized a partnership with Carroll County, designating the foundation as the official economic development partner for Carroll County, further strengthening regional cooperation; and WHEREAS, in 2026, the Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Board and the Greenwood-Leflore-Carroll Economic Development Foundation launched a master planning effort to guide future development of the Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Park, reinforcing the community's long-term vision for growth, infrastructure, and economic resilience: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and celebrate the 70th anniversary of the economic development partnership between the City of Greenwood and Leflore County that led to the establishment of the Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Board, recognize the enduring legacy of cooperation and innovation that has produced decades of job creation, investment, and community prosperity, and extend our sincere appreciation to all those public and private leaders who have contributed to this success over the course of seven decades. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Legislature of the State of Mississippi expresses its gratitude to the Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Board and the Greenwood-Leflore-Carroll Economic Development Foundation, Inc., its board members, executive leadership, staff, business partners, and community stakeholders for their steadfast commitment to fostering a vibrant local economy and improving the quality of life for the citizens of Greenwood, Leflore County, and Carroll County. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That a copy of this resolution be furnished to the Greenwood-Leflore Industrial Board and the Greenwood-Leflore-Carroll Economic Development Foundation, Inc., the City of Greenwood and the Board of Supervisors of Leflore County as an expression of support and congratulations from the Mississippi Legislature, and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
