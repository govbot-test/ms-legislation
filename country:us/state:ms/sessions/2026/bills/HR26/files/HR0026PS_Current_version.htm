House Resolution 26

(As Adopted by House)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING, CELEBRATING AND CONGRATULATING JAMAL ROBERTS UPON WINNING THE NATIONALLY ACCLAIMED 23RD SEASON OF AMERICAN IDOL AND WISHING HIM MANY MORE YEARS OF SUCCESS IN HIS FUTURE ENDEAVORS.

     WHEREAS, Meridian, Mississippi, native Jamal Roberts became the winner of the 23rd season of American Idol after endearing himself to millions of viewers by using his transformative vocal talents during each round of the highly-rated competition; and

     WHEREAS, episode to episode, round to round, Jamal bested his fellow American Idol competitors through many rounds of showcasing his vocal versatility with stirring renditions of Jelly Roll's "Liar", Sam Cooke's "A Change Is Gonna Come" and Marvin Gaye and Tammi Terrell's "Ain't No Mountain High Enough" to secure a spot in the show's finale; and

     WHEREAS, on Sunday, May 18, 2025, he sealed the deal as the newest member of the American Idol club when he stunned the crowd by performing his version of "Heal", by adding a new verse that shocked the consciousness of the audience, bringing many to tears and jubilation at the same time; and

     WHEREAS, on that day, he also made history, by becoming the second Mississippian to win the American Idol title and the second African-American male to win the coveted title; and

     WHEREAS, in spite of the driven nature of this internationally acclaimed competition, Jamal described his experience as positively "mind-blowing" and credited the competition for teaching him the "do's and don'ts" of being an artist; and

     WHEREAS, this former Physical Education coach from Crestwood Elementary in Meridian, Mississippi, and doting father of three daughters, has been quoted as saying he is most proud of the fact that he showcased his "most authentic self" to the audience and will endeavor to continue being his "most authentic self" as he evolves as an artist; and

     WHEREAS, since winning the 23rd season of American Idol,  Jamal's rendition of "Heal" has charted #1 on the Billboard Hot Gospel Songs Chart, and he has received his first-ever Grammy nomination for Best Gospel Performance/Song for his version of "Still"(Live Performance); and

     WHEREAS, in addition to these major milestones, Jamal has added nationwide touring artist to his resume by performing as an opening act for R&B powerhouses Monica and Brandy during their nationwide tour, The Boy is Mine Tour; and

     WHEREAS, his musical roots are steeped deep in the church, as his paternal grandfather was a bishop and his maternal grandfather a deacon, Jamal grew up singing and serving as an usher in church; and

     WHEREAS, Jamal's life triumphs are a testament to James 2:17 that "In the same way, faith by itself, if it is not accompanied by action, is dead.", because he had previously been a contestant on Sunday Best, without a win, then auditioned for American Idol twice before finally becoming a contestant, yet never gave up on himself; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to celebrate the outstanding accomplishments of Mississippians, such as Jamal Roberts, whose talents transcend the boundaries of this state in a manner that brings positive attention to the talents of our citizens:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Mississippi's very own Jamal Roberts upon winning the 23rd season of American Idol, and wish him many more years of success in his future endeavors.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Mr. Jamal Roberts and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
