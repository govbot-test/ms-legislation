Section 1:
SB2725 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Business and Financial Institutions By: Senator(s) McMahan Senate Bill 2725 AN ACT TO PROVIDE THAT PAWNBROKER BUSINESSES HOLDING A LICENSE AT THE TIME OF THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF THIS ACT SHALL BE CONSIDERED LEGACY PAWNBROKERS; TO PROVIDE THAT LEGACY PAWNBROKERS SHALL HAVE THEIR LICENSES INTERNALLY RENEWED ON AN ANNUAL BASIS WITHOUT THE NEED FOR REAPPLICATION; TO PROVIDE REQUIREMENTS FOR NEW PAWNBROKER BUSINESSES; TO PROVIDE FOR THE TRANSITION TO LEGACY STATUS FOR NEW BUSINESSES; TO REQUIRE NEW OWNERS TO APPLY FOR A NEW LICENSE UPON SALE OR TRANSFER OF A LEGACY PAWNBROKER BUSINESS; TO REQUIRE THE DEPARTMENT OF CONSUMER FINANCE TO INTERNALLY REISSUE LEGACY LICENSES WITHIN 30 BUSINESS DAYS; TO PROVIDE THAT LICENSES NOT RENEWED WITHIN THE 30-DAY TIMEFRAME SHALL AUTOMATICALLY RENEW; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI: SECTION 1.  (1)  Any pawnbroker business currently holding a pawn license with the Department of Consumer Finance at the time this legislation is enacted shall be designated a "legacy pawnbroker."  Such legacy pawnbrokers will have their licenses internally renewed annually by the Department of Consumer Finance without the need for an external reapplication process. (2)  Pawnbroker businesses established after the effective date of this act shall apply for a license according to the process established and administered by the Department of Consumer Finance, including the initial licensing process via third-party vendor. (3)  Upon completion of one (1) year of operation free of written infractions, a newly-licensed pawnbroker business shall be considered a legacy pawnbroker and given all benefits ascribed thereto, including internal, annual renewal of its license. (4)  In the event that a business considered a legacy pawnbroker business is sold or transferred to new principal ownership, the legacy status of the existing pawn license shall not be transferred.  Each new owner shall apply for a license through the standard licensing process as a new applicant. (5)  The Mississippi Department of Consumer Finance shall issue the internally renewed license to a legacy pawnbroker within thirty (30) business days.  If the department fails to physically issue the renewed license within such thirty-day period, the license shall automatically renew. SECTION 2.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after July 1, 2026.
