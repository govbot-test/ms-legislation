Section 1:
SB2125 (As Introduced) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Judiciary, Division A By: Senator(s) Younger Senate Bill 2125 AN ACT TO PROVIDE THAT A PERSON SHALL BE STRICTLY LIABLE FOR INJURIES CAUSED IN AN ACCIDENT IF THE OPERATOR IS FOUND FAILING TO HAVE THE MINIMUM AMOUNTS OF MOTOR VEHICLE LIABILITY INSURANCE REQUIRED BY CHAPTER 15 OF TITLE 63, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, WHEN THE ACCIDENT OCCURRED; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI: SECTION 1.  A person operating a motor vehicle on a public road, street or highway of the State of Mississippi shall be strictly liable for damages caused in an accident if the operator is found failing to have the minimum amounts of motor vehicle liability insurance required by Chapter 15 of Title 63, Mississippi Code of 1972, when the accident occurred. SECTION 2.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after July 1, 2026.
