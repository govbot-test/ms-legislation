HR 119 (As Adopted by House) - 2026 Regular Session MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE 2026 Regular Session To: Rules By: Representative Bell (65th) House Resolution 119 (As Adopted by House) A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING MS. SHASTA AVERYHARDT FOR HER ELITE ATHLETICISM, PROFESSIONAL SUCCESS AND ENDURING INFLUENCE AS A MEMBER OF THE LADIES PROFESSIONAL GOLF ASSOCIATION AND AS A TRAILBLAZING MEDIA INFLUENCER AND ADVOCATE FOR WOMEN IN SPORTS. WHEREAS, the Mississippi House of Representatives takes great pride in recognizing individuals whose extraordinary accomplishments reflect excellence, perseverance and an unwavering commitment to uplifting others; and WHEREAS, Ms. Shasta Averyhardt, whose journey into the game of golf began at the age of seven in Flint, Michigan, developed a deep and abiding passion for the sport, guided by the steadfast encouragement of her parents and fueled by a relentless dedication to excellence; and WHEREAS, through years of disciplined training and competitive play on junior and amateur tours, Ms. Averyhardt distinguished herself as a formidable competitor, capturing multiple tournament victories and laying the groundwork for an exceptional career in the sport of golf; and WHEREAS, her talents carried her to Jackson State University, one of Mississippi's distinguished Historically Black Colleges and Universities, where she pursued a degree in business with a concentration in accounting while simultaneously establishing herself as one of the most accomplished collegiate golfers in program history; and WHEREAS, during her collegiate career, Ms. Averyhardt achieved an extraordinary record of nine tournament victories, including four individual Southwestern Athletic Conference (SWAC) championships, and set a conference record with a score of six-under-par, firmly cementing her legacy as one of the premier student-athletes to represent the university; and WHEREAS, in 2011, Ms. Averyhardt reached one of the highest honors in professional golf by earning her coveted Ladies Professional Golf Association (LPGA) Tour card, becoming only the fourth African-American woman to reach this milestone, thereby blazing a trail of inspiration and expanding opportunities for women of color in the sport; and WHEREAS, throughout her professional career, Ms. Averyhardt has demonstrated remarkable skill and competitive excellence, winning seven professional tournaments and competing in the prestigious U.S. Women's Open, while also earning the Epson Tour Rising Star Award in 2012 in recognition of her emerging prominence in professional golf; and WHEREAS, her professional journey reflects both resilience and adaptability, as she briefly stepped away from competitive golf in 2014 to pursue a career in accounting as a tax associate with PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP, only to return to professional competition in 2017 with renewed determination and passion for the game; and WHEREAS, in recognition of her historic collegiate achievements and lasting contributions to athletics, Jackson State University proudly inducted Ms. Averyhardt into the JSU Sports Hall of Fame in 2018, celebrating her enduring legacy as one of the institution's most distinguished athletes; and WHEREAS, continuing her global impact within the sport, Ms. Averyhardt competed during the 20232024 season on a part-time basis with the Ladies European Tour while simultaneously engaging in numerous golf-related initiatives, including media appearances, mentorship and youth outreach designed to grow the game among younger generations; and WHEREAS, beyond the golf course, Ms. Averyhardt has become a respected media personality and influencer, using her voice and platform to advocate for women in sports, promote opportunities for Historically Black Colleges and Universities, and encourage health, wellness and empowerment among aspiring athletes; and WHEREAS, her multifaceted pursuits and compassionate spirit extend beyond athletics, as she embraces travel, community engagement and animal welfare, embodying the values of integrity, generosity and leadership; and WHEREAS, through every chapter of her journey, Ms. Averyhardt has demonstrated the character of a champion, proving that perseverance, discipline and faith can transform a childhood dream into a legacy that inspires countless others to pursue their purpose with courage and conviction; and WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to honor and recognize individuals whose accomplishments bring distinction to their profession, inspiration to their communities and encouragement to future generations of leaders and athletes: NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate Ms. Shasta Averyhardt for her elite athleticism, remarkable professional accomplishments as a member of the Ladies Professional Golf Association, and her growing influence as a media personality and advocate for women in sports, and extend best wishes for continued success in all her future endeavors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Ms. Shasta Averyhardt and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
