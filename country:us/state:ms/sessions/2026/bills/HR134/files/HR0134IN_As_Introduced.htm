MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Rules

By: Representatives Aguirre, Boyd (19th), Thompson, Turner

House Resolution 134

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE TUPELO HIGH SCHOOL GOLDEN WAVE AND ATHLETICS DIRECTOR JASON MILLER ON WINNING THE 2024-2025 MISSISSIPPI HIGH SCHOOL ACTIVITIES ASSOCIATION (MHSAA) CLASS 7A ALL-SPORTS AWARD.

     WHEREAS, in an astonishing year dominated by win after win, again and yet again, across several different athletic programs, the Tupelo High School Golden Wave and Athletics Director Jason Miller achieved their crowning moment in the 2024-2025 school year when the Mississippi High School Activities Association (MHSAA) declared the school the winner of the highly coveted Class 7A All-Sports Award; and

     WHEREAS, this prestigious recognition, earned by the top athletics program in the state, is based on a points system for overall athletic performance across various sports, and in their quest for the top, the Golden Wave racked up a grand total of 365 points, tumbling Madison Central High School from its multi-year reign as the holder of the 7A All-Sports Award, as the Tupelo Girls Cross Country Team, Football Team, Girls Basketball Team and Tennis Team all captured state championships; and

     WHEREAS, with runner-up finishes in both boys and girls swimming and boys cross country, Tupelo started the fall strong before winning its second consecutive 7A Girls Cross Country Championship on November 2, 2024, at the Choctaw Trails in Jackson, Mississippi, with a race time of 1:44:32:74 and team score of 81; and

     WHEREAS, only a month later on December 5, the 14-0 Golden Wave Football Team, then ranked No. 204 in the High School Football America 300 national rankings, brought home the first Golden Ball since 1992 by defeating Brandon High School 28-16 in the 7A State Football Championship played on the campus of the University of Southern Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, with seemingly no limit to the talent in the Tupelo athletics department, the 2025 Girls Basketball Team claimed its second consecutive state title when the ballers reigned supreme on the hardwoods at the Mississippi Coliseum in the 7A State Basketball Championship played on February 27, 2025; and

     WHEREAS, topping off the year of state championships for the Golden Wave, the Tupelo High Tennis Team displayed nothing but power and consistency with each stroke and rally in the April 2025 7A State Championship with both the boys and girls squads dominating the courts and securing the state title; and

     WHEREAS, the tremendous showing of the Golden Wave in championship play was not limited to the four teams bringing home state titles, as other Tupelo High teams performed remarkably well during 2024-2025, reaching the playoffs in 7A girls and boys soccer, boys basketball, softball and baseball; and

     WHEREAS, while the 2025 All-Sports Award, Tupelo's first since 2017, would not have been possible without each team's passion for their game and unparalleled spirit of teamwork and sportsmanship, the key to the Golden Waves' ascent to the pinnacle of sporting excellence has been the superb coaching and inspiration by the dedicated athletics staff at Tupelo High School under the expert leadership of sixth-year Athletics Director Jason Miller; and

     WHEREAS, a 1997 Tupelo High School graduate who lettered in both football and baseball, Jason Miller, personally familiar with the high caliber of the students and faculty of the school, welcomed the opportunity to return to his Tupelo roots in 2020 to build on the successful history of Golden Waves athletics that is now distinguished by having garnered the esteemed Mississippi All-Sports Award an unbelievable 20 times; and

     WHEREAS, since Jason Miller's first year back on his home turf, a year in which 17 teams advanced to the postseason and the boys golf team claimed the 2021 Class 6A crown, the athletic programs have thrived, and in the last two years, the Golden Wave has amassed an incredible seven state championships; and

     WHEREAS, under Jason Miller's guidance, Golden Wave athletes have excelled not only in sports, but in the classroom as well, with 960 student-athletes engaged in sports having posted a 3.67 GPA last fall, the highest in Tupelo High's history; and

     WHEREAS, it is the policy of the House of Representatives to recognize and commend outstanding Mississippians such as the Tupelo High School championship student-athletes, coaching staff and Athletics Director Jason Miller, and it is with great pride that we congratulate this elite group who has brought honor and pride to their school, the City of Tupelo, and the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Tupelo High School Golden Wave and Athletics Director Jason Miller on winning the 2024-2025 Mississippi High School Activities Association (MHSAA) Class 7A All-Sports Award and extend best wishes to the school's student-athletes, coaching staff and Athletics Director Jason Miller for continued success in all their future endeavors.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be furnished to Tupelo High School, Athletics Director Jason Miller and to the members of the Capitol Press Corps.
