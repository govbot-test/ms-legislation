MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

By: Senator(s) Barrett

Senate Resolution 55

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE LOYD STAR HIGH SCHOOL GIRLS AND BOYS GOLF TEAMS AND COACH JONATHAN BREAKFIELD FOR WINNING BOTH THE GIRLS AND BOYS 2025 CLASS 2A STATE GOLF CHAMPIONSHIPS.

     WHEREAS, in a historic achievement, Loyd Star High School captured both the Girls and Boys 2025 Class 2A State Golf Championships, cementing the program's place among the elite in Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, competing against the best teams from across the state, the boys' and girls' squads delivered clutch performances under pressure, showcasing depth, discipline, and a season's worth of hard work.  The rare double championship marks only the third time in Mississippi history that both teams have claimed state titles in the same year; and

     WHEREAS, the girls' team secured its championship after a tightly contested final round, pulling away from Tupelo Christian late with consistent play on the back nine, to win by 7 strokes.  Junior Kaylin Hester led the charge, finishing at the top of the individual leaderboard and securing medalist honors after firing a 78 and 80.  Hester said, "This was a goal of ours.  We wanted to do something special.  We have talked about it and worked so hard to accomplish it."  Other members of the girls' varsity team were Sophomore Camille Creel, Freshman Ann Claire Case, and 7th Grader Embry Hester; and

     WHEREAS, not to be outdone, the boys' team dominated from start to finish, entering the final round with a substantial lead before widening the gap and eventually winning by 52 strokes, over runner-up Baldwyn.  Sophomore standout Brooklyn Lee delivered two of the best rounds of the tournament, shooting a 72 and 68 while helping seal the team championship, as well as individual medalist.  Lee said, "I love our team.  It is so much fun being around these guys.  It is a big family."  In high school golf, six golfers play, with the top 4 scores of each round counted.  In the State Championship, a 73, 74, and 75 were discarded.  Other members of the boys' varsity team who competed include Seniors Casen Easterling (Co-Lin), Riley Case (Co-Lin), and Brody Thibodeaux (Meridian), along with Junior Phillip Ross Spencer and Freshman Bentley Ferguson; and

     WHEREAS, Head Coach Jonathan Breakfield, who oversees both programs, credited the success to dedication and culture.  "These athletes put in countless hours, not just on their swings, but on the mental side as well.  Our boys team has had a lot of success over the years and won several State Championships.  To get the girls one and to see both teams reach the top together is incredibly rewarding," said Coach Breakfield; and

     WHEREAS, the championships cap off remarkable seasons for both squads, each finishing with multiple tournament victories.  The girls finished in the top ten in scoring average per 18 holes in all divisions of the MHSAA, and the boys finished Number One, ahead of all of the biggest schools and best programs in the state.  Support from families, teachers, and the community was evident throughout the season and especially during the state tournament, where school colors lined the fairway and surrounded the green at the eighteenth hole as the golfers finished their rounds and wrapped up their championships; and

     WHEREAS, school administrators praised the teams for representing Loyd Star with class and excellence, both on the golf course and in the classroom.  "This achievement goes beyond trophies and rings.  These are great kids and phenomenal students as well," said Principal Seth Lofton; and

     WHEREAS, with returning players already looking ahead to the next season, the future of the Loyd Star golf program appears bright.  For now, though, the focus remains on celebrating a rare and remarkable accomplishment:  a clean sweep of the Girls and Boys 2A State Golf Championships:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Loyd Star High School Girls and Boys Golf Teams and Coach Jonathan Breakfield for winning the 2025 Class 2A State Golf Championships, and extend to the teams, coach, parents and sponsors best wishes for future success.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach Jonathan Breakfield and Loyd Star Principal Seth Lofton for the team, forwarded to the Lincoln County Board of Education, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
