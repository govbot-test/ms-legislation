Senate Resolution 112

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE WINSTON ACADEMY PATRIOTS FOOTBALL TEAM AND HEAD COACH PAT BYRD UPON WINNING THE 2025 MAIS CLASS 2A DIVISION II STATE CHAMPIONSHIP.

     WHEREAS, the Winston Academy Patriots Football Team demonstrated excellence by winning the 2025 MAIS Class 2A Division II State Championship with a commanding 20-0 shutout victory over Oak Hill Academy on November 22, 2025, in Clinton, Mississippi; and

     WHEREAS, this championship marks the seventh state football title in Winston Academy history and represents the program's first state championship in 25 years, completing a remarkable turnaround and restoring championship tradition to Patriot Field; and

     WHEREAS, the Patriots' championship season stands as a testament to perseverance, as the team overcame early season adversity, including injuries, inexperience and a 0-5 start, before rallying to win five of its final eight games and earn the right to compete for a state title; and

     WHEREAS, Winston Academy delivered a dominant defensive performance in the championship contest, holding Oak Hill Academy scoreless while limiting the Raiders to just 129 total yards of offense, forcing key turnovers and recording three sacks by Eli Goforth, Owen Woodward and Graison McDill; and

    WHEREAS, standout senior, Jonah Hood, was the lead rusher during the regular season for the Patriots with 495 yards, rushed for 14 yards in the championship game and recorded five tackles on defense with one defensive tackle for a loss; and

     WHEREAS, other impressive senior stars on the team, Owen Woodward and Eli Goforth also recorded five tackles each on defense during the championship game which were pivotal moves in bringing home the coveted title; and

     WHEREAS, notable seniors, Ben Caperton and Rhett Hatcher also distinguished themselves by contributing to the offense and defense of the Patriots during the entire season, and especially the championship game; and

     WHEREAS, senior leader, Beau Boatner anchored the Patriots' championship effort with an outstanding two-way performance, rushing for 107 yards and a touchdown on 19 carries while also recording nine tackles on defense, exemplifying leadership, toughness and commitment throughout the season; and

     WHEREAS, quarterback Jayson McMillin guided the Patriots' offense with poise and efficiency, accounting for two touchdowns, including a nine-yard scoring pass to King Ashford and a three-yard rushing touchdown, while finishing his final game with 239 total yards of team offense; and

                WHEREAS, the team's success throughout the season would not have been possible without the tireless dedication of the talented student-athletes who comprised the Winston Academy Patriots team:  Colton Mills, Paxton Greer, James Haven Joiner, Jayson McMillin, Beau Boatner, Kingsley Ashford, Trip Hill, Trenton Rhodes, Rhett Hatcher, Tanner Lee, Ben Caperton, Eli Goforth, Pervis Holmes, Jonah Hood, Owen Hood, Kipton Edwards, Tyler Fortenberry, Beaux Blanton, Easton Jordan, KJ Fulton, Johnson Wyckoff, Carson Pham, Levi Prisock, Madden Snow, Matt Dempsey, Hayden McDill, Hayden Blanton, Graison McDill, Cole Caperton, Landon Clark, Owen Woodward, Jesse Wilson, John Brady Stuart and Blaine Vanlandingham; and

     WHEREAS, this team has thrived under the leadership of Head Coach Pat Byrd, who spent eight years rebuilding the program's foundation and culture.  The Patriots embraced discipline and conditioning, proving that sustained commitment and character development are essential ingredients of championship success; and

     WHEREAS, Coach Byrd credited the team's conditioning, unity and belief in one another as decisive factors in the second-half surge that secured the championship, noting that the Patriots imposed their will through preparation, endurance and collective resolve; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize and commend outstanding achievements by these student-athletes whose accomplishments bring honor to their schools, communities and the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Winston Academy Patriots Football Team and Head Coach Pat Byrd upon winning the 2025 MAIS Class 2A Division II State Championship and upon completing a historic and inspirational season.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Winston Academy Head of School Michael Brown, Head Coach Pat Byrd on behalf of the Patriots Football Team, forwarded to the Winston Academy Board of Trustees and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
