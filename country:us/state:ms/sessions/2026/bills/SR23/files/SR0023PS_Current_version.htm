Senate Resolution 23

(As Adopted by Senate)

A RESOLUTION COMMENDING AND CONGRATULATING THE CALHOUN CITY WILDCATS FOOTBALL TEAM AND HEAD COACH M.D. JENNINGS FOR WINNING THE 2025 MHSAA CLASS 1A STATE CHAMPIONSHIP, WHICH IS THEIR THIRD STATE TITLE.

     WHEREAS, on December 4, 2025, the Calhoun City Wildcats High School Football Team won the 2025 MHSAA Class 1A State Championship, defeating Simmons 14-8 in a rain-soaked game at Starkville, marking their third title and first since 2016; and

     WHEREAS, the rain and cold were relentless under the bright lights of Davis-Wade Stadium at Mississippi State University, but so was the Calhoun City defense, which proved the difference in the Wildcats earning the Class 1A State Championship.  Calhoun City shut down the lauded running game of Simmons and D'andre Gatson for the 14-8 win over the Blue Devils.  "We knew it was going to be a tough game so we just kept fighting in the trenches to do all we could.  The defense just played sound, played physical and played together all night," Wildcat Defensive Leader Javontae Harvey said; and

     WHEREAS, the Wildcats limited the Blue Devils' Simmons to fewer than 100 total yards in the game.  "We got off to a bad start, but it's football.  We had to deal with the elements and all of that.  We knew it would be a tough one, a four-quarter game.  I'm just so proud of these kids and the way they dealt with adversity and were able to bring the Gold Ball back to Calhoun City," Wildcat Head Coach M.D. Jennings said.  Both teams struggled to do much offensively in the game fighting cold, wet conditions that made the ball hard to hold on to.  Xavian Pittman and Joseph Powell made the biggest plays late in the third quarter; and

     WHEREAS, the first half was a struggle for both teams with numerous bad shotgun snaps, fumbles and slippery footballs.  The Wildcats found their traction via the running game with Xavian Pittman and Demarious "Bug" Pittman both making plays.  A 28-yard run by Xavian put City in scoring position and Bug capitalized from there with a six-yard touchdown run.  He would also power in up the middle for the conversion to level the game at eight apiece.  The Wildcats' defense came up big the rest of the game.  Three times Simmons was inside the Wildcats 30-yard line, but every time Calhoun City came through with a big stop; and

     WHEREAS, the Calhoun City High School 2025-2026 Varsity Foodball Team Roster:  Jaylon Jackson, Trell Dorrough, Jaquavion Jennings, Shane Farmer, Demarious Pittman, Zay Pittman, Kejuan Armstrong, Carson Lawrence, Harland Pittman, JJ Armstrong, Bryson West, Dereon Mays, Kayden Holland, Za Gary, Aden Hill, Brennan Lawrence, Jamarius Hill, Jamarion Deloach, Kari Ewing, Makavion Wilson, Makevion Gray, Kemauri Salley, Makalob Wilson, Deshun Jones, S.J. OBriant, Javontae Harvey, Joseph Powell, Carson Brumley, Xzavier Maldonado, Russell Pass, Tykevius Ford, David Draine, Laden Graham, Bryan Buckley and Marshall McFall; Head Coach M.D. Jennings; and Assistant Coaches Sawyer Byars, Cameron Clarke, Travis England, Chakel Gates, Bradley Mitchell and Justin Moore; and

     WHEREAS, it is with great pride that we recognize this group of student-athletes who have brought honor to their school, their community and to the State of Mississippi:

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI, That we do hereby commend and congratulate the Calhoun City Wildcats Football Team and Head Coach M.D. Jennings for winning the 2025 MHSAA Class 1A State Championship, which is their third state title, and extend to the team, coaches, parents and sponsors our best wishes for continued success.

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this resolution be presented to Coach M.D. Jennings and Calhoun City High School Principal Heather Nix for the team, forwarded to the Calhoun County Board of Education, and made available to the Capitol Press Corps.
