MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2026 Regular Session

To: Business and Financial Institutions

By: Senator(s) Johnson

Senate Bill 2714

(As Passed the Senate)

AN ACT TO AMEND SECTION 7-7-42, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO PROVIDE THAT THE STATE FISCAL OFFICER SHALL TRANSFER FUNDS AND REPORT ASSOCIATED PAYEE RECORDS FOR ANY CANCELLED WARRANT TO THE ABANDONED PROPERTY FUND OF THE UNCLAIMED PROPERTY DIVISION OF THE STATE TREASURY; TO DELETE THE REQUIREMENT THAT SUCH FUNDS SHALL REMAIN IN SUCH FUND FOR FIVE YEARS; TO AMEND SECTION 89-12-3, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO DEFINE "LAST-KNOWN ADDRESS," "LAWFUL CHARGES" AND "TANGIBLE PERSONAL PROPERTY," AND TO REVISE CERTAIN TERMS; TO AMEND SECTION 89-12-5, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO REVISE PROVISIONS RELATED TO ABANDONED PROPERTY; TO AMEND SECTION 89-12-17, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO PROVIDE THAT CERTAIN TANGIBLE PERSONAL PROPERTY SHALL BE PRESUMED ABANDONED IF CONDITIONS FOR A PRESUMPTION OF ABANDONMENT EXIST; TO CREATE NEW SECTION 81-5-60, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO ESTABLISH A SAFE DEPOSIT BOX ESCHEATMENT PROCESS; TO CREATE NEW SECTION 81-5-60.1, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO CLARIFY REPORTING REQUIREMENTS FOR REMOVAL OF SAFE DEPOSIT CONTENTS PRIOR TO THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF THIS ACT; TO AMEND SECTION 89-12-11, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO PROVIDE CIRCUMSTANCES IN WHICH A SECURITY SHALL BE PRESUMED ABANDONED; TO CREATE NEW SECTION 89-12-18, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO SET FORTH CONDITIONS UNDER WHICH PROPERTY HELD IN A SAFE DEPOSIT BOX MAY BE PRESUMED ABANDONED AND SUBJECT TO REPORTING AND REMITTANCE IN ACCORDANCE WITH SECTION 89-12-28; TO AMEND SECTION 89-12-30, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO AUTHORIZE THE STATE TREASURER TO OFFER FOR SALE TANGIBLE PROPERTY DELIVERED UNDER SECTION 89-12-28; TO AMEND SECTION 89-12-31, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO PROVIDE PROTECTION FROM LIABILITY TO THE STATE TREASURER AND THE STATE FOR CERTAIN DAMAGES; TO CREATE NEW SECTION 89-12-44, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO REQUIRE RETENTION OF CERTAIN RECORDS FOR FIVE YEARS; TO CREATE NEW SECTION 89-12-28, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO DIRECT DELIVERY OF A REPORT AND OF CERTAIN PROPERTY TO THE STATE TREASURER; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

     BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI:

     SECTION 1.  Section 7-7-42, Mississippi Code of 1972, is amended as follows:

     7-7-42.  Any State of Mississippi warrant issued by the State Fiscal Officer against any fund in the State Treasury which has not been presented to the State Treasurer for payment within one (1) year after the last day of the month in which it was originally issued, shall be null and void, the obligation thereafter shall be unenforceable and the State Fiscal Officer shall not issue an additional warrant.

     The State Fiscal Officer is authorized and directed to cancel all outstanding warrants over one (1) year old at the end of each month and shall notify the State Treasurer who shall remove such warrants from his list of outstanding warrants.

     The State Fiscal Officer shall transfer the funds * * * reflected by the cancellation of the and report the associated payee records for any cancelled warrant to the Abandoned Property Fund authorized by Section 89-12-37 of the Unclaimed Property Division of the State Treasury * * * where the funds shall remain for five (5) years.  After five (5) years, if the funds are unclaimed, the State Treasurer shall transfer the funds back to the original source of funds.

     This section is applicable to warrants issued on and after January 1, 2000.

     SECTION 5.  The following shall be codified as Section 81-5-60, Mississippi Code of 1972:

     81-5-60.  (1)  Beginning on the effective date of this act, if the rental payment for a safe deposit box is delinquent for at least one (1) year or the lease agreement for the safe deposit box terminated not less than one (1) year prior, the lessor may send written notice, by registered United States mail, and also by electronic notice, if the lessor maintains an electronic mail address for the lessee in its records, to the address on file for the subject box that the lessor will remove the contents of the box if the rent is not paid before the date specified in the notice, which may not be earlier than sixty (60) days after the date the notice is sent. 

          (a)  The written notice under subsection (1) of this section must:

              (i)  Contain a heading that reads substantially as follows:  "Notice:  The State of Mississippi requires us to notify you that your safe deposit box contents shall be deemed abandoned and may be transferred to the custody of and disposal by the State Treasurer if you do not timely contact us."

              (ii)  State that the safe deposit box contents will be turned over to the State Treasurer if the box rent remains unpaid for a period of three (3) years;

              (iii)  State that, after the safe deposit box contents are turned over to the State Treasurer, an apparent owner(s) that seeks return of the property must file a claim with the State Treasurer;

              (iv)  Identify any apparent owner(s) of the safe deposit box contents;

              (v)  Summarize the nature of the contents that are the subject of the notice;

              (vi)  Indicate the amounts currently owed for unpaid rent and other lawful charges, and state that the apparent owner will become liable for additional amounts in the absence of timely responding;

              (vi)  Provide instructions that the apparent owner(s) must follow to prevent the holder from reporting and paying or delivering the property to the State Treasurer; and

              (vii)  Indicate that in the absence of promptly claiming the property from the State Treasurer, the property may be sold and thereafter the owner's claim shall be limited to the proceeds of sale, less any amounts due the lessor for unpaid rent or other lawful charges arising from the abandonment of the safe deposit box.

          (b)  The mailing of the notice under this subsection (1) shall be in addition to the notice requirements of Section 89-12-23(5).

     (2)  If the rent is not paid within sixty (60) days of the date of the notice, the lessor may open the box and shall do so in the presence of no fewer than two (2) employees, of which at least one (1) shall be a notary public.  The lessor shall inventory the contents of the box in detail on a form as provided by the State Treasurer's reporting instructions, notarize such form and place the contents of the box in a sealed envelope or container bearing the name of the lessee to be held in the vault of the lessor until the contents are escheated to the State Treasurer's office.

     (3)  The lessor shall have a lien on the package and its contents to the extent of any rent or other lawful charges due and owing plus the actual, reasonable costs of removing the contents from the box and other lawful charges.

     (4)  The procedures provided for in this section shall be strictly and exclusively followed as of the effective date of this act and thereafter where the lessor of a safe deposit box elects to remove contents of the box for reasons of delinquent lease payment or other lease termination.

     SECTION 6.  The following shall be codified as Section 81-5-60.1, Mississippi Code of 1972:

     89-5-60.1.  (1)  If a lessor removed the contents of a safe deposit box prior to the effective date of Section 81-5-60 because payment of the rent on the box was delinquent or the lease agreement for the safe deposit box was otherwise terminated, such contents shall be reportable to the Treasurer pursuant to Sections 89-12-23 and 89-12-28, and any associated rules as adopted by the State Treasurer.

     (2)  Provided that the lessor acted in good faith in removing the contents from the safe deposit box and the subsequent custody of the contents, the lessor shall be afforded the protections provided for in Section 89-12-31.

     (3)  For the purposes of this section, "good faith" means that there is no showing that the procedures followed by the lessor in removing and storing the contents failed to meet reasonable commercial standards of practice in the industry, and that in doing so the lessor did not act negligently.

     SECTION 8.  The following shall be codified as Section 89-12-18, Mississippi Code of 1972:

     89-12-18.  All property removed from a safe deposit box on which the lease or rental period has expired due to nonpayment of rent or where the lease has otherwise terminated under Section 81-5-60 and has been unclaimed by the owner(s) for more than three (3) years from the date on which the lease or rental period expired or the lease otherwise terminated shall be presumed abandoned and subject to reporting and remittance in accordance with Section 89-12-28.

     SECTION 9.  The following shall be codified as Section 89-12-28, Mississippi Code of 1972:

     89-12-28.  (1)  A lessor who removes the contents from a safe deposit box pursuant to the provisions of Section 81-5-60 and 81-5-60.1 shall report such contents to the State Treasurer.  The contents shall be reported separately from any other type of unclaimed property reported to the State Treasurer by the lessor.  The State Treasurer shall create a separate and distinct unclaimed property report form for the reporting of safe deposit box contents.

     (2)  By December 31, 2026, the State Treasurer shall promulgate rules detailing the process for the delivery of contents listed in any reports of unclaimed safe deposit box contents filed with the State Treasurer's office.

     (3)  Following a filing of a report of unclaimed safe deposit box contents with the State Treasurer's office, the State Treasurer determine the date for delivery of the contents to the State Treasurer's office.  The lessor shall receive notice no less than thirty (30) days before the date scheduled for delivery.  No delivery of contents shall be made to the State Treasurer by a lessor in the absence of, or prior to, an assigned date of delivery.

     (4)  If the lessee contacts the lessor to claim the contents of the safe deposit box before the scheduled delivery date, an updated report shall be included with the delivery that excludes such contents from those delivered to the State Treasurer's office.

     (5)  If after investigation the State Treasurer, in his or her sole discretion, determines that the property delivered from a safe deposit box has insubstantial commercial value, the State Treasurer may destroy or otherwise dispose of the property at any time after one hundred twenty (120) days from the date the State Treasurer took custody of the property.  As used in this subsection (5), "insubstantial commercial value" means that the probable cost of the sale exceeds the State Treasurer's estimation of the value of the property.  No action or proceeding may be maintained against the state or any officer or against the holder for or on account of any action taken by the State Treasurer or such holder pursuant to this section with respect to the property.

     (6)  Notwithstanding subsection (5) of this section, any military awards, medals or decorations among the contents of any safe deposit box shall not be sold, but shall be retained by the State Treasurer until claimed by the lessee of the safe deposit box or the lessee's estate or released to a person or entity lawfully entitled to possession thereof.  The State Treasurer shall endeavor to work with any other entities, including other states or federal agencies, as applicable, to locate the person or entity lawfully entitled to the possession of any such military awards, medals or decorations.

     (7)  The State Treasurer shall develop rules specifying the procedures and requirements for the disposition of any firearms and ammunition, illicit or inherently dangerous or hazardous materials, and contraband removed by a lessor from a safe deposit box.  A lessor who acts in substantial compliance with such rules shall be afforded the protections set forth in Section 89-12-31.

     (8)  With regard to any property from a safe deposit box delivered to the State Treasurer in accordance with this section that has more than insubstantial commercial value, the State Treasurer shall conduct a public sale of the abandoned property with commercial value pursuant to Section 89-12-62.

     (9)  Property removed from a safe-deposit box or other safekeeping repository that is received by the State Treasurer's office shall be subject to the holder's right under this chapter to be reimbursed for the actual cost of the opening and to any valid lien or contract providing for the holder to be reimbursed for unpaid rent or storage charges and any other lawful charges incurred by the lessee in connection with such safe deposit box lease.  The State Treasurer shall make the reimbursement to the holder out of the proceeds remaining after the deduction of the State Treasurer's selling cost.

     SECTION 10.  Section 89-12-30, Mississippi Code of 1972, is amended as follows:

     89-12-30.  (1)  Securities listed on an established * * * stock exchange must be sold at prices prevailing at the time of sale on the exchange.  Other securities may be sold over the counter at prices prevailing at the time of sale or by any other method the Treasurer considers advisable.

     (6)  The State Treasurer may proportionately deduct auction fees, preparation costs and related expenses of sale from the amount payable to an apparent owner when safe-deposit box contents are sold.

     SECTION 11.  Section 89-12-31, Mississippi Code of 1972, is amended as follows:

     89-12-31.  (1)  Upon the payment or delivery of property to the Treasurer, the state assumes custody and responsibility for the safekeeping of the property.  A person, including the lessor of a safe deposit box, who pays or delivers property to the Treasurer in good faith is relieved of all liability for any claim then existing or which may arise or be made in respect to the property.

     (2)  If the holder pays or delivers property to the Treasurer in good faith and thereafter another person claims the property from the holder or another state claims the money or property under its laws relating to escheat or abandoned or unclaimed property, the Treasurer, upon written notice of claim, shall defend the holder against any liability on the claim.

     (3)  The Treasurer and the State of Mississippi are protected from liability for damages incurred while the property or the proceeds from the sale of the property are in the Treasurer's possession in accordance with Mississippi statutory and common law addressing sovereign immunity.  But, in any event, the liability of the State or the Treasurer is limited to the extent of the value of the property delivered under this chapter and remaining in the possession of the Treasurer at the time a suit is filed.

     ( * * *34)  The holder of an interest under Section 89-12-11 shall deliver a * * * duplicate replacement certificate or other evidence of ownership if the holder does not issue certificate of ownership to the administrator.

     ( * * *45)  Any holder who has paid moneys to the Treasurer pursuant to the provisions of this chapter may make payment to any person appearing to such holder to be entitled thereto and, upon proof of such payment and proof that the payee was entitled thereto, the Treasurer shall forthwith reimburse the holder for the payment.

     SECTION 12.  The following shall be codified as Section 89-12-44, Mississippi Code of 1972:

     89-12-44.  (1)  Except as otherwise provided in subsection (2) of this section, a holder required to file a report under this section shall maintain the records containing the information required to be included in the report for ten (10) years after the holder files the report, unless a shorter period is provided by rule of the State Treasurer.

     (2)  A business association or financial organization that sells, issues or provides to others for sale or issue in this state, traveler's checks, money orders, or similar instruments other than third-party bank checks, on which the business association or financial organization is directly liable, shall maintain a record of the instruments while they remain outstanding, indicating the state and date of issue, for three (3) years after the holder files the report.

     SECTION 13.  This act shall take effect and be in force from and after July 1, 2026, and shall stand repealed on June 30, 2026.
